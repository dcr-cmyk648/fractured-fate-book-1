# 0027: Treasury Placement and Containment Function

Date: 2026-06-25

Status: Accepted

## Decision

`loc-treasury` owns the physical high-up Treasury/vault chamber, access/workplace behavior, and accepted chamber-level containment function.

The Tzorrax Heart as an object, the Tzorrax Sphere as an object, and exact zä/Heart mechanics remain separate later reviews.

The accepted physical baseline is: high in the Rezin spire, guarded stair access, huge domed zä-lit chamber, enormous central suspended Treasury sphere, and no elevators.

Weightlessness is a feature of the chamber produced by the Tzorrax Heart interacting with its containment armature. It is not operationally controlled by the Registry. When Davian shatters the sphere and takes the Heart, the weightlessness ends and gravity returns.

The sphere has no visible usable entrance. A prior entrance was sealed or blocked.

The sphere does not fall or drop in the accepted theft version. Davian throws/launches it at Niall to shatter it and steal the Heart.

Treasury wards envelop the more dangerous vault wards, so work proceeds outside-in.

Treasury security uses restricted work hours, exit searches, and Registry/vetting-based access.

Heart containment and gravity negation as a side effect of that containment are accepted direction. Exact object mechanics, zä sinks, Nizän-protection details, and inner armature design remain for later object and advanced zä reviews.

## Consequences

- Later manuscript cleanup should remove the visible usable doorway and preserve a sealed/blocked former entrance instead.
- Later revision should avoid "sphere falls/drops" language.
- Later Registry material should not claim Registry actively controls the chamber's weightlessness.
- Later Heart, Tzorrax Sphere, and zä reviews should preserve that gravity negation is a side effect of Heart containment.

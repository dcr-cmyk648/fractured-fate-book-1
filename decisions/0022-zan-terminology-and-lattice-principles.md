# Decision 0022: Zän Terminology and Lattice Principles

Date: 2026-06-24

## Status

Accepted; stored in `bible/magic/magic-zan-lattices.md`.

## Decision

The final in-world spelling is accented: `zä`, `zän`, and `zänwright`. Unaccented `za`, `zan`, and `zanwright` may appear in notes and control-file IDs for ease of typing, but the accepted reference spelling uses the accent.

`Zänwright` is the final profession term for Book 1. Older or alternate terms such as `glyphwright` and `jinwright` should not supersede it.

The zän model is like a circuitboard. Zä are components in a connected lattice. The base-6 number system is structurally linked to zä design, and the strike-line running through numbers and characters conducts energy between zä in a lattice.

Ancient zä are more complex but less durable and less scalable. Their complexity represents more complex circuitry. In Book 1, ancient-style zänwork is used by Zirene and possibly some Registry systems.

The Heart-powered inefficiency model is accepted broadly: major Rezin systems work, but many are overbuilt around near-limitless Heart input and fail or shrink after the Heart is stolen.

Zirene is accepted at high level as the advanced zänwright behind or connected to Niall's metals, Davian's bands, Archeon-derived technology, and Antithesis lineage. Exact device inventory and object mechanics remain for later reviews.

Modern writing systems and masked/Concord hand signs derive from older zä/zän principles.

Number-system details, `Nizän` / `nïza`, and archetypal zä are deferred to more specific culture/terminology reviews.

Skein-layer zä / reality-layer etching remains candidate material for later cosmology or advanced-zän review.

## Rationale

This decision settles the central terminology and mechanics while preserving object-specific and culture-specific details for later records. It keeps zänwork grounded as magical circuitry rather than generic runes, and it explains why Heart-backed Rezin infrastructure can be both powerful and inefficient.

## Consequences

- Accepted reference files should use `zä`, `zän`, and `zänwright` in prose.
- Control-file IDs may remain unaccented for stability.
- `glyphwright` and `jinwright` become older/draft/candidate terms unless later assigned distinct in-world roles.
- Registry, Treasury, Niall's silver, Zirene's devices, Archeon gauntlets, and Antithesis should be reviewed through zä-lattice principles without making this file own every object.
- Number-system, archetypal zä, and Skein-layer zä details remain deferred.

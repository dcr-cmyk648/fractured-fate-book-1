# Decision 0019: Ink Pact Terminology and Core Mechanics

Date: 2026-06-24

## Status

Accepted; stored in `bible/magic/magic-ink-boons.md`.

## Decision

All Ink grantings are called `Ink Pacts`. Earlier `Ink Boon` and `Boon` language is superseded and should be brought into line during later manuscript/revision work.

Davian's canonical Book 1 Ink Pact abilities are:

- absorbing the aura of a target when they are killed
- rapid movement related to moving through a wrinkle-in-space manner through the Skein
- healing through unpleasant forced restructuring of his body when it is broken

Antithesis transformation is no longer canon. Antithesis is just a knife throughout Book 1. Davian will need inside help concealing it before he is searched; the exact revision details remain for later scene work.

Ink healing is a specific deeper Pact case, not a universal Ink property. Davian receives this deeper healing Pact when Elric visits him in the Rezin citadel after Davian saves Selwin and is promoted. Kelyra does not have this healing ability. Karra has it, and Vorren later has it.

Ink influence exists on a spectrum from light pressure, to psychotic symptoms, to deep puppet-like control. The Harbinger leader is the deepest accepted case, essentially becoming Elric's puppet. Davian's intrusive pressure to kill Niall is a psychotic side effect of receiving all of the Ink rather than a separate Pact ability.

Ink-before-Shroud timing remains tentative. Current direction is that Ink may need to be received before Shroud manifestation, but Shroud interruption and the exact rule are unresolved.

Transfer mechanics remain candidate and are likely too confusing for the Book 1 plot. Derivative or mass-produced Ink Pacts are later-book or later-story material, not Book 1 accepted canon.

## Rationale

Using one term simplifies the magic system and avoids a Boon/Pact taxonomy that would add complexity without current story benefit. The accepted Davian effects preserve the Registry bypass, movement, and survival mechanics while removing the Antithesis transformation branch.

## Consequences

- `magic-ink-boons` is approved as `Ink Pact`.
- Direct dependent summaries should use `Ink Pact` rather than `Ink Boon`.
- Antithesis transformation should be treated as obsolete and routed to later revision cleanup.
- Ink/Shroud timing remains queued for later magic consistency work.
- Transfer and derivative Ink remain candidate or later-book material.

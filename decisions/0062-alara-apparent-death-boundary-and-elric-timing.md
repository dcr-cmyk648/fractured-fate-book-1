# Alara Apparent Death Boundary and Elric Timing

Date: 2026-06-27

Status: accepted; stored in approved `event-alara-death-war-memory` record

## Decision

The stable entity ID remains `event-alara-death-war-memory`, but the accepted event boundary is `Alara's apparent death`.

Alara/Niall war memories should remain primarily in the Alara journal packet and later chapter mapping rather than being duplicated in this event record.

Alara's apparent-death route is accepted at the high level: postpartum/fateweaving decline after Selwin's birth leads to her apparent death. Book 1 characters believe she is dead or unrecoverably lost.

The hidden truth remains that Alara is likely trapped in a higher plane after trying to kill the extraplanar deity, but exact higher-plane, Nizän, Sheol, Skein, "eye," and death-scene mechanics remain deferred. The apparent death may not come up explicitly in Book 1.

Niall believes Alara is unrecoverably dead/lost and does not know the higher-plane truth. Niall subconsciously, but not consciously, blames Selwin.

Elric likely did not want Alara to die, because she was a potential fateweaver who could have been manipulated to damage the Skein and summon the extraplanar deity to eat existence. During Alara's decline and apparent death, Elric was already dead in his original body and still in the process of recrystallizing / taking over the Syndicate through the Ink Pact route.

Therefore, Elric's accepted role in this event is not causing Alara's death. His accepted role is exploiting the aftermath through the tampered Alara codex, parent-memory trap, and broader fate/trauma pressure system.

## Deferred

- Exact apparent-death choreography.
- Whether Niall shows Maya his codex to warn her about fateweaving.
- How much, if anything, of Alara's apparent death appears explicitly in Book 1.
- Exact eye/Nizän/Sheol/higher-plane/Skein mechanics.
- Exact relationship between Alara's decline, postpartum state, fateweaving, and the extraplanar deity.
- Whether Alara is reachable, recoverable, or relevant in later books.

## Rationale

This gives Niall's grief and Maya's family/fateweaving inheritance enough structure for the future outline without prematurely locking the endgame cosmology.

It also avoids redundant ownership: the war memories remain with Alara's journal, while this event owns the death wound and its consequences.

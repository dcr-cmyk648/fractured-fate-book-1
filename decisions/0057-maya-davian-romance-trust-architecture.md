# Maya / Davian Romance and Trust Architecture

Date: 2026-06-27

Status: accepted; stored in approved `thread-maya-davian-romance` arc seed

## Decision

The Maya/Davian romance and trust arc is accepted as probably the most important Book 1 plot arc.

Accepted core:

- The thread is built around attraction, class/status misreading, shared protective values, practical training trust, real betrayal, and endgame resurrection.
- Maya is attracted to Davian partly because he represents chaos outside the perfectionistic boundaries she has built around herself, even though she does not consciously understand that at first.
- Davian is attracted to Maya partly because of her perfection, strength, and principle, including moments such as her fighting off Caius.
- Davian's attraction deepens as he gets to know Maya and sees that she is a real person underneath the perfect exterior who has to try hard to be that way.
- The kiss remains emotionally real but messy: genuine connection, followed by Davian's Ink/Harbinger-triggered panic, flight, and Heart betrayal.
- Maya's post-betrayal arc should preserve the meta truth that Davian's feeling was real and the betrayal still matters, while Maya's internal experience is confused and realistic.
- Davian does not consciously think he is in love with Maya when he sees her on the wall. He has a rush of feeling he cannot understand. Only later can he revisit the image and understand what it meant for Maya to go out there.
- Davian's resurrection by Maya is accepted future-plan emotional architecture. Maya may not fully understand why she resurrects him and may later question whether Elric manipulated her into all of it.
- Exact kiss choreography, confession wording, resurrection mechanics/cost, and final relationship status remain deferred for endgame outline review.

## Rationale

This arc gives Book 1 its central emotional structure. It ties Maya's perfectionism and need to be seen as a person to Davian's movement from negation toward values. It also makes the Heart betrayal, Davian's death, Maya's resurrection choice, and the later Paradox/identity material emotionally specific rather than only plot-mechanical.

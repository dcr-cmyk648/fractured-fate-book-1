# Prologue Functional Worldbuilding Guidance

Date: 2026-06-30

## Status

Accepted future prose-revision guidance.

## Decision

The Prologue should avoid over-explanatory phrasing for functional worldbuilding details, such as explicitly stating that Maya's clothing permits movement because it is "a necessity given her responsibilities."

Later prose should show such information through POV-relevant action or perception. For Maya's clothing, this could mean showing her testing, using, or noticing freedom of movement and letting the reader infer the social and practical implications.

Do not edit manuscript prose now. Apply this during later authorized prose revision, Chapter Goal Cards, or detailed outline/prose-preparation work.

## Rationale

This keeps worldbuilding organic and character-grounded. It preserves the information value of clothing/function without slowing the Prologue with explanatory aside.

# Demi-Human Six-Lineage Structure and Social Role

Date: 2026-06-27

Status: accepted; stored in approved `culture-demi-human-peoples` record

## Decision

There are six major lineages, aligning culturally with the base-six number system.

The six-lineage structure is accepted now. Important caveat: pure versions of any non-human lineage are extremely rare in the Six Cities. Humans are the main exception. In the Six Cities, most demi-human-descended people look fairly ordinary, with only subtle traces of lineage visible.

Less obviously human-looking demi-humans are more likely to be pushed toward social outskirts, rural margins, or the criminal world because social pressure, discrimination, and lack of magic make mainstream advancement difficult.

Human-dominant culture usually labels humans as `1`, with other numeric zän corresponding to other lineages:

1. human
2. Durnai, singular/adjective Durna
3. Ghorreni, singular/adjective Ghorren
4. Veyri, singular/adjective Veyr
5. Thaleni, singular/adjective Thalen
6. Naerothi

These non-human names are human-language phonetic glosses of words from each lineage's ancestral tongue. The glosses are often inaccurate approximations. All non-human lineages are pluralized in the human language by adding `i`; this does not necessarily reflect the ancestral languages.

Everyone broadly speaks the human language as a trade language because of High Clade military dominance.

Humans can produce Shrouds and reality-distorting hereditary magic. High Clades think of this in a self-congratulatory way as proof that humans are superior, justifying expansionist policies. The hidden truth is that this magic originates with the original sin and damage to reality, not anything good.

Durnai are shorter, stockier, dwarf-like, and the most prevalent demi-human lineage in the Six Cities.

Ghorreni are orc-like: generally larger and muscular, with subtle eye and forehead differences. They are somewhat common in the Six Cities but face racism around being unintelligent or violent, making them more likely to be low-class or to avoid Six Cities life.

Veyri are thin, large-eyed, nervous-appearing, almost as if they are "prey" animals. Descendants manifest these traits to different degrees and are more often used as scouts than muscle.

Thaleni are giant/troll-like, face even stronger racism than Ghorreni, are rare in the Six Cities, and are obvious when present. They mostly live in rural areas outside direct or constant Clade influence. A few serve the Harbingers in the catacombs beneath the city.

Naerothi are Maya's demi-human lineage and are covered more fully in `culture-naeroth-lineages`. They are closest in-world to elves through perception of elegance, beauty, refinement, and harmony with nature. They are extremely rare in the Six Cities and face fear-based racism around bewitching or manipulation.

Mercenary death-money economics are accepted at high level: mercenaries are often desperate poor people willing to die so their families receive death payments. Detailed military/economic handling is deferred to `thread-demi-human-mercenary-forces`.

Anti-Naeroth rumors that Alara bewitched Niall into ending the war and marrying her are canon rumors, partly based on the rarity of Naeroth interactions. How much Davian has heard specifically remains candidate.

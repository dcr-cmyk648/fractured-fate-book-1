# Zän Spelling Convention Correction

Date: 2026-06-26

Status: accepted; supersedes the acute-accent spelling convention in `0042`

## Decision

The accepted in-world spelling uses a diaeresis on the `a`:

- `zän`: singular/root term for a meaningful magical sign, script element, or component.
- `zä`: plural for multiple signs/components.
- `zänwright`: profession term.
- `Nizän`: zero / negation / void.

Unaccented forms such as `zan`, `za`, `zanwright`, and `Nizan` may still appear in notes, control-file IDs, filenames, or quick author input for ease of typing.

The prior acute-accent convention `zán` / `zá` / `zánwright` / `Nizán` is superseded.

Historical source files, raw imports, normalized imports, old review packets, and old decision records may retain the spelling that was current when they were created.

## Rationale

This corrects the durable project convention to match the author's intended spelling while preserving prior historical records.

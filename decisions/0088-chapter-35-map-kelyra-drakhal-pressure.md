# Chapter 35 Map, Kelyra Captivity, and Drakhal Pressure

Date: 2026-06-30

Status: accepted current-draft map guidance

## Decision

The `d1-ch-35` current-draft chapter map is approved.

The main chapter purpose is Kelyra captivity/Serathis gaslighting, Lethira as Archeon child-system embodiment, Kelyra's Shroud block, Drakhal/Archeon pressure, and Vorren-breeding-contract setup.

The Archeon healing beat should later be updated toward the newer painful gold strike-line healing model while preserving that Archeon can only maintain one expensive healer.

Lethira's function is a Serathis-controlled emotional manipulation cameo who is violently discarded. Exact death/survival wording can remain flexible.

Serathis's "he found you" and Kelyra's old-Ink crawl / "kill her" whisper are accepted as subtle Elric/Ink connection cues, without making Kelyra a deeper-healing Ink case like Davian or Karra. Psychotic or compulsive symptoms can come from long-term Ink exposure without being tied directly to deeper healing.

Under the newer Ink system, Kelyra can still have some rapid-movement ability during this sequence rather than being completely depowered. The boundary is that she does not have deeper forced-restructuring healing and still lacks reliable Shroud access.

Evana and Caius intrude primarily to throw Drakhal's weight around, remind Archeon that Drakhal is supposedly in charge, and see what is happening inside Archeon after the Heart theft. Their main concern is Niall's uncertain weakness; they probe this indirectly by blaming Archeon and assessing enemy strength by proxy while trying not to expose Drakhal's concern.

Drakhal's broader approach is to stack the deck, avoid exposure, and politically/psychologically maneuver both allies and enemies.

The breeding-contract implication points toward Vorren, which is horrifying because of Vorren's violent nature and because he killed Kelyra's daughter.

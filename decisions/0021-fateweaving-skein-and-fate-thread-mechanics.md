# Decision 0021: Fateweaving, Skein, and Fate-Thread Mechanics

Date: 2026-06-24

## Status

Accepted; stored in `bible/magic/magic-fateweaving-skein.md`.

## Decision

Keep Fateweaving, the Skein, and fate ties in one umbrella file for now.

The Skein is a real structure or layer of reality. Human perception experiences it through threads, strands, paths, possibility, and time-adjacent mind-space imagery, but the perceived structures correspond to something real.

Elric's fate sight is probabilistic trauma-script / path-of-least-resistance perception rather than deterministic future sight. He sees strands tied to people acting out trauma, urges, negative impulses, fear, guilt, avoidance, rage, responsibility, and other compulsive patterns. He uses those strands to nudge events, but character agency remains intact.

The source of Elric's fate sight remains unresolved.

Maya is still developing. In Book 1 she can perceive threads, follow a path through an object, alter spatial access in limited circumstances, glimpse branches, and eventually enter or tear the Skein to resurrect Davian. She should not yet be treated as someone who can freely rewrite fate.

Alara's fateweaving and Convergence should be stored here only at a high level until `char-alara` and `magic-convergence-heat-transfer` are reviewed.

Davian's resurrection is a canon event that will happen at the end of the book.

Original sin / first magus / fractured reality can be stored here at a high level because it links directly to Elric, the Skein, and the endgame.

## Rationale

The decision keeps the magic file useful without prematurely splitting cosmology, Naeroth abilities, and Elric's manipulation into separate owners. It also protects agency: fate-thread sight can create the feeling of inevitability without making characters' choices meaningless.

## Consequences

- `magic-fateweaving-skein` is approved as the umbrella magic/metaphysics record.
- Elric's fate sight should be described as trauma-path perception, not absolute future vision.
- Maya's fateweaving should remain developmental in Book 1.
- Davian's end-book resurrection is accepted future-plan canon, while the exact scene mechanics remain unresolved.
- Elric's fate-sight source, Heart/Ink/Skein mechanics, resurrection cost, larger cosmology, and Fateweaving/Convergence relationship remain candidate material.

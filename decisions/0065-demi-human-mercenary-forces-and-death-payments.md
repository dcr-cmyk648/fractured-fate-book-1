# Demi-Human Mercenary Forces and Death Payments

Date: 2026-06-27

Status: accepted; stored in approved `thread-demi-human-mercenary-forces` record

## Decision

Mercenary forces in and around the Six Cities often recruit desperate poor people who accept lethal risk so their families receive death payments.

Death money creates a grim economic logic: dying may help a family more than surviving crippled and unable to work.

Mercenaries can be real attackers, tools of High Clade politics, and exploited poor people at the same time.

For the opening Drakhal-backed attack on Rezin, Drakhal likely spends a great deal of money to buy extra disposable troops. The force includes both long-term mercenaries or experienced soldiers who help coordinate attacks and want to avoid casualties, and disposable shock troops who are not really trained soldiers and sign up primarily for death money.

Maya's early casualties are not as clean or efficient as they would be if Niall were fighting from the start. She is in her first real combat and takes time to get the hang of killing at that scale.

Even when Maya begins killing more attackers, the troops remain unusually persistent. Maya does not know this in the moment, but later context can reveal that the attack structure, money, and disposable shock troops explain some of that persistence.

When Niall appears and begins scything down soldiers, the force breaks almost immediately because of widespread carnage, general fear of Niall, and the fact that the disposable shock layer is giving way to more experienced "real" soldiers who have stronger incentives to run.

Demi-human-descended people are overrepresented in these troops because discrimination, poverty, and lack of magic push them toward marginal military work. Poor humans also serve in the troops; the force is not all demi-human.

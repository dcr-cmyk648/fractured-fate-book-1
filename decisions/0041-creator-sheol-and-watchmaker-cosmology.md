# Creator, Sheol, and Watchmaker Cosmology

Date: 2026-06-25

Status: accepted

## Decision

`Creator` is accepted as the in-world term for the Creator.

Modern culture primarily uses Creator language in oaths, curses, exclamations, and references to older worship traditions. Modern practice mostly ignores the concept of the Creator except for acknowledging that there definitely is one.

The prevailing modern posture is deist-like: the Creator made the world, set it in motion, and left. Serathis and Elric believe this absent-watchmaker cosmology more explicitly.

`Sheol` is accepted as vague language for something or somewhere bad. It can include the void, somewhere an extraplanar deity exists, non-existence, planes outside the Skein, or a related hostile metaphysical outside. It should remain vague for now.

The Niall/Serathis conversation about Death, Sheol, gods, the absent Creator, and the broken world will happen, but the exact dialogue remains in development. It is primarily a place to surface Elric's cosmology of the absent watchmaker and the broken created order, which underlies Elric's psychotic depression.

Archeon throne-room stained glass remains accepted as remnants of prior High Clade Creator-worship.

Alara's good-God/spiritual orientation remains owned by `char-alara`, not by this culture-wide record.

## Deferred

- The true nature of the fractured fate of reality.
- The literal nature of Sheol.
- The exact relationship among the Creator, original sin, first magus, Skein, Heart, Nizan, Sheol, and reality fracture.
- The literal status of any good deity, evil god, dark god, extraplanar deity, Nizan entity, Zerakh'tum, or other cosmological force.
- Exact religious institutions, practices, clergy, doctrine, holidays, and distribution of belief.
- Exact Niall/Serathis dialogue.

## Rationale

This keeps the Book 1 religious language usable while preserving the deeper cosmology for later system and thread review. The record should not overdefine distant metaphysics during the initial story-bible pass, but it must not lose the author's developed thinking about fractured fate, Sheol, and the broken created order.

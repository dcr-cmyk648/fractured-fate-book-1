# 0028: Spire Name and Central Amphitheater Form

Date: 2026-06-25

Status: Accepted

## Decision

`loc-spire` is the shared High Clade Presentation and central meeting location in the Six Cities. It is separate from the Rezin citadel's ordinary central architectural spire.

In-world characters commonly call the location the Spire or Central Spire. `Presentation Spire` can appear as casual descriptive speech because Presentations happen there, but it is not the official term.

The accepted physical form is the later direction: the Spire is an open-air amphitheater or arena built into the old blast hole from a long-ago High Clade war cataclysm. It is not an intact tower.

Maya's Presentation happens in the open-air amphitheater version. Current literal tower approach, doors, halls, chamber-atop-spire, and open-crown language should be treated as revision material.

The central dais, circular geometry, six High Clade sections, and floor/map symbolism are retained for the accepted location.

The plaza/checkpoint/market approach and shared High Clade security survive the revision. The Spire is one of the few places where the High Clades truly collaborate.

Old rubble and remnants have been removed from the area. The name is historical, not maintained by obvious ruins dominating the site.

There are six High Clade citadels, and the Spire is at the center of the Six Cities. The one-off "seven citadels" line is obsolete/suspect unless later geography deliberately creates a separate non-High-Clade structure.

## Consequences

- Later manuscript revision should replace intact-tower Presentation staging with the open-air amphitheater form.
- Later location and culture work should preserve six High Clade citadels / city-wedges unless a separate seventh structure is explicitly approved.
- Later High Clade politics should preserve the Spire as shared collaboration ground rather than territory owned by a single Clade.

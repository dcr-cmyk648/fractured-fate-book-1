# 0026: Registry Chamber Placement and Interface

Date: 2026-06-25

Status: Accepted

## Decision

The Registry chamber is below ground in the Rezin citadel and fairly far from the main entryway.

It is reached through guarded heavy double doors and is circular or broadly circular, with pale or smooth stone, zä-etched walls, and a central operating interface.

The central interface may be described with draft terms such as platform, pedestal, arcane circle, aurametric array, projection sphere, or ghost-map until final terminology is cleaned up. The accepted model is a central magical control surface that projects or models the Rezin citadel.

The map/projection can verify, locate, pulse, zoom, and refine results inside the citadel, but it does not show every person all the time. It requires someone to be named or otherwise specifically called out before it locates them or zooms/refines to their position.

Registration appears both in the chamber stone and in the citadel projection/model.

The chamber's distance from the main entryway and its below-ground placement support the passive-mode raid logic: Registry staff do not necessarily hear the initial Archeon entryway fight before the situation is already moving fast.

Exact access routes, depth, stairs, Registry-to-Treasury relationship, and active-mode discharge geometry remain deferred.

Davian no longer returns to the Registry after stealing the Heart. The high-window Registry escape and magical-elevator solution are obsolete/rejected.

Post-Heart repair ideas remain candidate/future repair material and are not realized Book 1 chamber canon.

## Consequences

- Later manuscript/revision work should not use a high-window Registry escape.
- Later maps must preserve the Registry as below ground and the Treasury as high up without adding elevators.
- Later Registry scenes should use targeted lookup/refinement rather than a universal always-visible people display.
- Later active-mode, Heart, and zä reviews may define attack/discharge details without changing the accepted chamber geography.

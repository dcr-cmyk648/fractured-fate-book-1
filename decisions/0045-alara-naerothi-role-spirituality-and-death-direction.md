# Alara Naerothi Role, Spirituality, and Death Direction

Date: 2026-06-26

Status: accepted

## Decision

Alara is a structurally central deceased character, not merely an object/codex backstory entry.

Someone of Naeroth descent may be called `Naerothi`; Alara is Naerothi.

Alara is Niall's wife, Maya and Selwin's mother, a former anti-Elarion commander/war leader, and a Shroud + fateweaving + Convergence user at the accepted high level.

Niall was already a hopeful reformer, similar in some ways to much younger Elric. Elric likely allowed Niall to go through that phase in the hope that Niall would turn toward the same goals. Alara gave Niall's ambitions a more concrete spiritual and moral undertone, and her apparent death twisted that reform impulse.

Alara's personal spirituality is accepted at the high level: she sees ordinary life, consciousness, goodness, and moral knowledge as miraculous or Created. Exact theology and good-God language remain flexible.

Alara's postpartum/fateweaving death direction is accepted at the high level. Although she is "dead" to the characters, hidden cosmology points to her being trapped in a higher plane after going there to try to kill the extraplanar deity. No one in Book 1 knows this, and it likely does not come up except possibly during Maya's resurrection of Davian and contact with higher planes.

Alara's codex memories are emotionally meaningful and partly reliable, but filtered, selected, and idealized through Elric's codex trap. Their danger for Maya is precisely that they feel meaningful and reliable. Elric only had to play into Alara's positive perception of Niall to distort the memories toward Maya's belief that she should be able to achieve essentially anything by trying hard enough.

That same distortion also backfires against Elric: by the end of Book 1, Maya can see Niall as both broken and aspirational, integrate that dialectic, and help Niall temporarily overcome his grief.

## Deferred

- Exact Alara death scene.
- Exact higher-plane mechanics.
- Exact "eye," Nizán, Sheol, Skein, and extraplanar-deity relationship.
- Exact Niall codex staging.
- Exact theology and good-God language.
- Exact Naerothi culture and fateweaving practice.
- Exact truth/distortion split in each codex memory.

## Rationale

This gives Alara enough accepted structural weight for future outline work while preserving the detailed death/cosmology mechanics for event, magic, and endgame reviews.

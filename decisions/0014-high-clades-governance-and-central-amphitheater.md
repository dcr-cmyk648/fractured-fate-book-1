# Decision 0014: High Clades Governance and Central Amphitheater

Date: 2026-06-24

Status: accepted; stored in approved `org-high-clades` record

## Decision

The High Clades are the ruling Clade tier of the Six Cities system. A High Clade is politically defined by holding one of the six citadels that form the core of a city-wedge / legally distinct city.

There are six canonical High Clades, named at Presentation:

- Rezin
- Archeon
- Nyctaris
- Drakhal
- Cryndor
- Lyranth

City-wedges in the Six Cities are governed through Low Clade councils and proxy structures reporting to the relevant High Clade.

Presentation should be stored in the High Clades organization record only as a legitimacy institution. Detailed ritual, training, fashion, and Presented/unPresented culture belong to `culture-magus-presentation`.

Birth contracts are a system-wide High Clade practice. They are exploitative, but some participants may experience them as leverage or protection because the system offers few other guarantees.

The Central Spire is in the center of the Six Cities, but `Spire` is a historical reference. It is now an amphitheater built into a blast hole from a long-ago cataclysm in the High Clade wars.

Anti-demi-human racism should be mentioned at a high level in the High Clades record because it reinforces and informs the whole system.

## Rationale

The High Clades record should establish the political structure and core institutions without absorbing the whole caste system, Presentation culture, detailed magic taxonomy, or location design.

## Implications

- `culture-clade-hierarchy` should own broader caste hierarchy, Low Clade council details, Masked Clades, and social mobility.
- `culture-magus-presentation` should own Presentation ritual detail, training, fashion, Shroud expectations, and Presented/unPresented status.
- `thread-high-clade-politics` should own the specific Rezin / Archeon / Drakhal political conflict.
- Location review should reconcile `Central Spire` language with the accepted amphitheater / blast-hole direction.
- Nyctaris, Cryndor, and Lyranth need later review before their leadership, powers, and political roles are treated as fully settled.

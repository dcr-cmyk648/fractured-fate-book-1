# Decision 0003: Maya's Shroud and Prologue Revision Direction

Date: 2026-06-23

Status: accepted; stored in approved `char-maya` record and prologue revision planning

## Decision

Maya's Shroud is effectively permanent while she is awake, not continuously permanent while asleep.

She can mute it enough to eat or interact with objects, but muting requires conscious effort and can easily get out of control. Her Shroud can activate while she sleeps if she has a bad dream or similar trigger.

The prologue should eventually be revised so Maya is attacked partway through by a Drakhal-affiliated magus who is far away and not directly visible to her. Maya reacts frantically, causing widespread destruction that affects Davian's combat.

After combat is fully over, Maya should have dissociation, flashbacks, and numbness. That aftermath should be barely shown in the prologue itself and is more likely to emerge in later chapters.

## Rationale

The awake-only Shroud behavior fits Maya's psychology better than a fully permanent sleep-state Shroud. The prologue revision clarifies that Maya's destructive response is a panic reaction to an unseen attack, not simple indifference or effortless dominance.

## Implications

- Supersedes the earlier outline version that Maya cannot put her Shroud down even when asleep.
- Turns the bullet-note first-combat/prologue idea into accepted revision direction.
- Requires later revision planning before manuscript edits.

# Elric Reality/Skein Arc and Trauma Determinism

Date: 2026-06-27

Status: accepted; stored in approved `thread-elric-reality-skein` arc record

## Decision

Approve the Elric/Skein thread as the structural hidden-antagonist arc for Book 1.

Elric sees fate strands tied to trauma, negative impulses, compulsions, fear, grief, rage, responsibility, attachment, and paths of least resistance. His sight is real. Elric likely experiences fate as mostly deterministic, especially where trauma is concerned.

The story's governing theme cuts against Elric's interpretation. Trauma creates powerful repeating patterns, but it does not permanently determine people. Characters can change, grow, choose values, accept help, and stop repeating the same injuries.

Approve the Book 1 endgame function: Elric uses Niall, Davian, Maya, the Heart, Ink, the Harbingers, and Davian's resurrection to damage/unmake reality or enable the extraplanar threat.

Approve `A Cord of Six Strands` as the late-revealed title-level frame of two intertwined triads:

- Elric-Niall-Maya
- Serathis-Kelyra-Davian

This framing is revealed very late, when Maya is about to resurrect Davian.

## Deferred

- Exact fate-sight source.
- Exact Heart exposure pathway.
- Exact Heart/Skein/resurrection mechanics.
- Exact Elric persistence mechanics across Ink, codex, remnant, body, and contingency systems.
- Exact Alara codex bridge mechanics.
- Exact Sheol, Nizän, higher-plane, extraplanar-deity, original-sin, and reality-fracture cosmology.
- Exact Davian death and Maya resurrection choreography.
- Exact resurrection cost and reality-damage mechanics.
- Exact staging and wording of the six-strand reveal.

## Rationale

This gives the future outline a stable hidden-antagonist engine while protecting character agency. Elric can be frighteningly good at reading and leaning on trauma patterns without making Davian, Maya, Niall, Harvick, or other characters puppets.

The approved arc also preserves the title architecture and endgame direction without forcing premature cosmology decisions.

# Chapter 32 Map, Maya Violence Boundary, and Elric Remnant Clues

Date: 2026-06-30

Status: accepted current-draft map guidance

## Decision

The `d1-ch-32` current-draft chapter map is approved.

Maya kills all the Archeon attackers after the manor attack. This is accepted as morally gray and defensible wartime violence because Archeon attacked her/Rezin, but it should still raise meaningful questions about permissible violence, wantonness, and later psychological consequences.

Aldira's questioning of Maya should not be read as Aldira being simply right or Maya being simply wrong. The point is to notice Maya changing and to keep moral pressure alive without reducing the book to a trite "killing is always bad" answer.

The underground Ink remnant's desire to be destroyed is accepted as the hope of the remnant specifically, not the goal of full Elric himself.

The remnant's `blood in the water`, `arrow of fate`, and `third coffin` phrases are intentional future-book setup. Their high-level planning meanings may be recorded for continuity, but Book 1 should not spell them out.

## Planning Meanings

- `blood in the water`: damage to the Skein draws the attention of the extraplanar deity.
- `arrow of fate`: Selwin's forward-only entropic powers and their possible future-book potential to kill the extraplanar deity; this is what Elric originally sought before psychotic depression shifted his plan toward destroying reality.
- `third coffin`: a three-caskets / psychological-symbol reference to Elric's failure to accept imperfection and the inevitable horrors and difficulties of existence.

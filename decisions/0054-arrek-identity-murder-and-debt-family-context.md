# Arrek Identity, Murder, and Debt-Family Context

Date: 2026-06-26

Status: accepted; stored in approved `char-arrek` record

## Decision

Arrek Hadrin receives a dedicated standard-depth character record focused on the real murdered worker. Davian's cover identity remains owned mainly by `char-davian`.

Arrek is a twenty-six-year-old Low Clade guild-licensed glyphwright with a valid Sorevin/Registry identity. He has no surviving primary relatives and has two debt-contracted cousins tied to Jorra Halvek.

The house/ledger material is canon: Arrek was living poorly because he was sending substantial money toward buying out his cousins' debt contracts.

The accepted murder sequence is:

- Davian and the Concord track Arrek to a bar.
- They follow him into an alley.
- Kelyra pressures Davian into killing Arrek by framing him as a High-Clade collaborator and all Clade collaborators as evil.
- Davian murders Arrek while simultaneously using the Ink to capture Arrek's aura.
- Davian then uses Arrek's aura and name as his Rezin-citadel cover.

Current source/manuscript material still contains stale Jalen-fieldwork pressure references. These are superseded by Kelyra as the fieldwork/pressure figure and should be cleaned up during later revision work.

Arrek-family restitution or cousin consequences remain candidate material. Possible future options include Davian sending money to buy out the cousins' debt contracts or showing up and killing the contract-holder.

## Rationale

Arrek's record preserves the moral cost of Davian's cover identity without making Arrek a sprawling subplot. He should read as a rough, ordinary worker with real obligations, not as a clean enemy or an invented alias.

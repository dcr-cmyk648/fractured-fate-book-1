# Decision 0011: Rezin Clade, Family, and Household Structure

Date: 2026-06-24

## Status

Accepted; stored in `bible/organizations/org-rezin.md`.

## Decision

The Rezin family and Rezin Clade are generally synonymous and should be stored in one accepted organization file for now.

Aldira, Harvick, and Erynn are inner-circle household figures. Alden is the prior name/gender version of Aldira from earlier drafting, not a separate current accepted character unless later review establishes otherwise.

The Rezin organization reflects general High Clade culture: Presentation, Shroud competence, tactical secrecy, command posture, harsh standards, and projected authority. The Rezin version is comparatively less harsh because the Clade sits at the apex and can rely on ultra-powerful magi such as Niall and Maya. Selwin's lack of constant extreme Shroud-forcing training would be atypical in another Clade.

Elric's hidden antagonist role should remain minimal in the Rezin organization file. He has little day-to-day operational influence on the Rezin Clade and primarily works through direct character influence and other organizations such as the Harbingers. His earlier influence and political-reform desires may still inform some Rezin operating differences.

Alara's Naeroth origin is important Rezin family history and marks a significant divergence between Rezin political posture and general High Clade leanings.

## Rationale

This keeps `org-rezin` useful as the durable record for Rezin family/Clade operations without turning it into the owner file for Elric's hidden plot, High Clade culture as a whole, Alara/Naeroth worldbuilding, Registry mechanics, or citadel infrastructure.

## Consequences

- `org-rezin` can store concise family, household, power-base, and culture facts.
- Broader High Clade culture remains for `org-high-clades`, `culture-clade-hierarchy`, and `culture-magus-presentation`.
- Alara and Naeroth details remain for `char-alara` and Naeroth/culture reviews.
- Old `Alden` references are suspect and should be reconciled during Aldira review or later manuscript cleanup.

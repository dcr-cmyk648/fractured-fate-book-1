# Alpha-Continuation Chapter Division Proposal

## Purpose

This is a proposed chapter-level division for the rest of the alpha draft after the current mapped prose cutoff. It sits between the general source sequence map and future detailed unit outlines.

This proposal is not a detailed outline and does not authorize prose drafting. It is meant to make chapter boundaries, POV flow, and major beat placement visible before the project starts close outlining.

## Source precedence

This proposal uses the accepted repo state and current author guidance as the main authority. Older source notes are used for missing beats, texture, and candidate choreography, but they do not override accepted decisions.

The old "raid Drakhal" path is preserved as political trigger material, not as the final physical target. Drakhal's arrival/proposal, Caius's move toward Maya, the marriage/banishment trap, and Niall's decision to retaliate remain important. The assault itself routes through Archeon because Archeon is the better strategic first target and Drakhal can preserve plausible deniability through Vorren.

## Workflow support

The workflow supports the requested stepwise process:

1. Lock the general/overarching alpha map.
2. Approve or revise this proposed chapter division.
3. Create one detailed chapter/unit outline at a time, starting with Chapter 37.
4. Review exact locations, transitions, logistics, POV handoffs, character positions, magic constraints, and consistency risks for that one unit.
5. Revise and approve the detailed outline.
6. Draft prose only if the author explicitly requests prose for the approved unit.
7. Run targeted consistency/comment checks after each accepted unit.
8. Repeat through alpha completion.
9. Run an alpha-complete consistency pass.
10. Only then return to beta-draft front-to-back re-outline/rewrite.

## Approval Status

Approved as the working alpha-continuation structure on 2026-06-30, with flexible 37/38 boundary management. If Chapter 37 becomes too long, Jalen's healing/intelligence material can shift into Davian's Chapter 38 POV because Chapter 38 has more room.

The Drakhal proposal is not a replacement for the prior Spire public-provocation. The intended chain is:

1. Evana first uses the Spire/public provocation to test whether Niall will attack in public or fail to appear while other High Clades are positioned to interpret or exploit either result.
2. Niall's refusal to attack publicly becomes another data point suggesting possible weakness, but not definitive proof.
3. Drakhal then continues the scheme by bringing allied or proposal-supporting High Clades to the Rezin citadel.
4. The Rezin-citadel proposal again pressures Niall through Maya/Caius marriage, Niall's banishment, Shade accusations, and legitimacy theater.
5. Evana's likely backup plan is that Niall will retaliate. If he attacks Drakhal directly, she can have allied High Clades ready. If he attacks Archeon, Vorren/Serathis's trap is waiting.
6. Jalen and Aldira can plausibly recommend Archeon without knowing the trap because Archeon is weaker, is Drakhal's collaborator, is where Kelyra is, and can weaken the alliance before a direct Drakhal confrontation.

## Proposed chapter divisions

### Chapter 37 - Dining Hall, Jalen's Return, and First Intelligence

- Primary POV: Maya.
- Starts with: current `d1-ch-37-preposed` material already underway: training, overwork, Registry pressure, Maya/Davian meal invitation.
- Main job: finish the dining-hall social/romantic pressure and interrupt it with Jalen's near-death arrival.
- Major beats:
  - Maya/Davian dining-hall interaction, flirting, awkwardness, class/status missteps, Registry surveillance wound.
  - Tanelle/workers/Selwin/Erynn/Harvick texture as useful, but not so much that Maya/Davian disappears.
  - Jalen arrives basically dead or collapses at Rezin.
  - Niall resists healing Jalen; Maya argues they need his information.
  - Painful gold strike-line healing.
  - Jalen gives initial information: Archeon/Harbinger collaboration, Heart delivery, Kelyra's capture context, and network access.
  - Aldira takes over and forces the others to let Jalen rest.
- Ends with: Rezin has proof/clarity that Archeon is the actionable direction, but not enough for a clean rescue yet.
- Watchpoints:
  - Do not let this become full Archeon-gauntlet lecture if Jalen interruption is the chapter turn.
  - Preserve Maya/Davian warmth and misstep before the interruption.

### Chapter 38 - Davian's Night, Failed Confession, and Unresolved Want

- Primary POV: Davian.
- Main job: process Maya, Jalen's return, and Davian's need to act, then stage the charged nighttime Maya/Davian scene.
- Major beats:
  - Davian cannot metabolize the dinner, Maya's surveillance/misstep, Jalen's injuries, and Kelyra's danger.
  - He thinks through what Rezin training is making possible and what it costs emotionally.
  - Harvick/Selwin material can appear if it supports Davian's processing, but this chapter should not reset him to pre-Shroud.
  - Same night: Davian cannot sleep and encounters Maya.
  - Charged, sexually tense but unresolved conversation.
  - They talk around parents, Niall/Kelyra, inherited damage, Shrouds, and what each sees in the other.
  - Davian tries to confess what he feels but cannot get it out right.
  - The scene ends poorly enough that the relationship remains unresolved before the raid.
- Ends with: Davian more certain he wants Maya and more afraid that he cannot have or deserve this.
- Watchpoints:
  - The failed confession should matter later when he dies and after resurrection.
  - Do not resolve the romance too cleanly.

### Chapter 39 - Intelligence Machine, Awkward Training, and the Drakhal Proposal

- Primary POV: Maya.
- Main job: show the next-day operational ramp and then hit Rezin with the political trap.
- Major beats:
  - Jalen is more recovered but physically out of combat.
  - Aldira and Jalen coordinate intelligence; Aldira is excited by Masked Clade network access.
  - Jalen is impressed by Rezin resources, money, manpower, and institutional reach.
  - Jalen teaches signs/routes/contacts/payoff logic to Rezin agents.
  - Maya/Davian/Selwin/Harvick training continues, but awkwardly after the nighttime scene.
  - Drakhal/Archeon/allies arrive.
  - Proposal/trap: Maya marries Caius; Niall is banished or steps aside.
  - Caius makes some move toward Maya.
  - Davian blasts in to attack Caius; standoff.
  - Niall smooths the scene over only long enough to get Drakhal/Archeon out.
- Ends with: Niall goes to Aldira/Jalen and demands lethal counterattack planning.
- Watchpoints:
  - Niall's composure is tactical, not acceptance.
  - Davian's attack should be protective/jealous/impulsive without making him look strategically clean.
  - This scene follows the prior Spire/public-provocation sequence. It should continue that political trap rather than replace it.

### Chapter 40 - Counterattack Planning and Commitment to Archeon

- Primary POV: Maya or Davian; likely Maya if Niall's command logic is central.
- Main job: turn Niall's rage into an actionable Archeon-first plan.
- Major beats:
  - Niall wants to kill them.
  - Aldira and Jalen shape that impulse into a strategic target choice.
  - Jalen funnels Niall toward Archeon: Drakhal's collaborator, weaker/easier first target, and public lesson.
  - Maya recognizes problems but experiences Niall including her as trust.
  - Maya pushes against disposable-soldier logic and wants to avoid using troops or scouts as expendable bodies.
  - Niall's "trust" is double-edged: partly real reliance on Maya, partly his attempt to prove High Clade magi can still decide power alone.
  - Davian is desperate to get Kelyra.
  - Harvick may give Davian a sword or otherwise hand off support because he cannot join the final assault.
  - Scouts/forerunners/death-money questions can appear here if useful.
  - Davian tries again, or almost tries, to say something to Maya before the raid and fails or is interrupted.
- Ends with: assault party committed and moving.
- Watchpoints:
  - Make clear this is Archeon, not Drakhal, because of strategic prioritization.
  - Keep Niall's reasoning morally mixed: tactical sense plus regression into deterrent violence.

### Chapter 41 - Neglected Wedge Approach and Archeon Breach

- Primary POV: Maya, with possible brief Davian pressure.
- Main job: move physically from Rezin into Archeon and show the world beneath High Clade strategy.
- Major beats:
  - Aboveground approach through neglected wedge/slum boundary.
  - Maya sees survival-level poverty while carrying high-level magic/war problems.
  - Niall's hierarchy worldview surfaces.
  - Breach through Archeon gates/towers/wards.
  - Resistance is oddly weak because Serathis is holding back valuable magi.
  - Davian may attempt technical bypass; Maya/Niall may choose overwhelming force.
  - Information/leak points Davian toward Kelyra.
- Ends with: Davian splits off to rescue Kelyra while Maya/Niall push toward the central tower.
- Watchpoints:
  - The split needs emotional and tactical justification.
  - Maya should feel the cost of letting Davian go and continuing with Niall.
  - The neglected-wedge approach should make High Clade rule feel socially visible, not just provide scenery.

### Chapter 42 - Split Assault Begins: Davian to Kelyra, Maya to the Trap

- POV: alternating Davian/Maya; Niall's situation appears through Maya/Niall sequences.
- Main job: establish simultaneous pressure fronts.
- Major beats:
  - Davian tracks Kelyra through leaked/forced information.
  - Davian fights through Archeon interior resistance or a prepared trap.
  - Maya and Niall enter the central tower/throne-room path.
  - Niall kills apparent defenders or breaks through resistance in a way that feels too easy.
  - Maya notices signs that Serathis has held back valuable force.
  - Throne-room/stained-glass Creator-worship remnants.
  - Serathis needles Niall around godhood, death, weakness, Creator imagery, and what High Clades pretend to be.
  - Serathis triggers the collapsing-floor trap.
  - Niall drops toward Vorren.
  - Maya is pinned above by Serathis/held-back Archeon magi.
- Ends with: Davian still trying to reach Kelyra; Maya separated from Niall and under attrition pressure.
- Watchpoints:
  - Start intercutting cleanly here.
  - Keep spatial logic readable.

### Chapter 43 - Kelyra's Shroud and Maya's Attrition

- POV: alternating Davian/Kelyra-side and Maya-side.
- Main job: escalate both simultaneous fronts and get Davian/Kelyra positioned to relieve Maya.
- Major beats:
  - Davian reaches Kelyra or the final obstacle to her.
  - Archeon heir/magus/trap fight.
  - Kelyra's Shroud block is forced into the open.
  - Davian rejects inherited fear/revenge script and asks for help/protection.
  - Kelyra manifests a Shroud and helps defeat the immediate opponent.
  - Maya fights held-back Archeon magi and realizes time is on Serathis's side.
  - Niall is below with Vorren, under extreme pressure.
- Ends with: Davian/Kelyra free enough to move toward Maya; Maya still cannot reach Niall.
- Watchpoints:
  - Kelyra should not become suddenly healed/clean; this is breakthrough under trauma.
  - Maya's fight should feel costly even if she is enormously powerful.

### Chapter 44 - Serathis, Vorren, and the Rapid-Climax Intercut

- POV: rapid alternating Davian/Maya, with Niall through the Vorren fight.
- Main job: resolve the physical fights.
- Major beats:
  - Davian/Kelyra relieve pressure on Maya.
  - Maya reaches or gets closer to Niall/Vorren.
  - Davian/Kelyra fight Serathis.
  - Serathis reveals enough about her motive: burn the Clade system down, Kelyra as product/value.
  - Kelyra kills or helps kill Serathis.
  - Serathis can die pleased that Kelyra has become "worth it"; Kelyra rejects that logic.
  - Serathis's request for Kelyra to burn the system down can seed later fallout without resolving Archeon succession.
  - Davian leaves Kelyra to help Maya/Niall despite Kelyra wanting him to stay.
  - Niall is breaking/down, seeing failures and Alara grief.
  - Maya sees broken Niall and accepts him rather than demanding perfection.
  - Davian blocks Vorren and ruins Antithesis defensively.
  - Maya opens the ceiling; Niall channels lightning; Maya/Davian/Niall together defeat Vorren.
- Ends with: apparent physical victory, everyone barely standing.
- Watchpoints:
  - POV cuts can accelerate here.
  - Davian's role is support/protection, not solo victory.
  - Vorren's end should feel appetite-frustrated rather than philosophical: not enough life, experience, freedom, or domination.

### Chapter 45 - Elric, the Skein, and Resurrection

- Primary POV: Maya.
- Main job: reveal the real endgame after physical victory and execute Davian's death/resurrection.
- Major beats:
  - Elric appears after Vorren is defeated.
  - He kills Davian.
  - Davian's pre-death smile or unfinished confession lands.
  - Maya enters the Skein/void outside ordinary time.
  - Conversation with Elric: he cannot do what Maya can there; six strands; original sin/damaged reality/Creator interpretation as much as needed.
  - Maya resurrects Davian.
  - The diamond-like eye of the extraplanar deity appears before return.
  - Elric escapes; they cannot pursue.
- Ends with: Maya and Davian back in real space, consequences unresolved.
- Watchpoints:
  - This is Maya POV.
  - Do not overexplain cosmology beyond the scene's needs.

### Chapter 46 - Exit, Kelyra's Choice, and Davian's Ending

- Primary POV: starts Maya or neutral aftermath if needed, ends Davian.
- Main job: land the immediate aftermath and end Book 1 on Davian choosing the Rezins.
- Major beats:
  - Survivors exit Archeon.
  - Kelyra appears on the way out and considers killing Niall/Maya or trying.
  - Davian stands against her.
  - They return toward Rezin.
  - Jalen goes to Kelyra.
  - Davian starts to go with Jalen but Niall stops/turns him back.
  - Niall gives Davian Paradox, physically present in this final movement; exact staging deferred.
  - Davian chooses to stay with the Rezins instead of rejoining his family at Archeon.
  - Davian says, or begins to say, what he could not get out before the raid; Maya does not have to absolve him for the connection to remain real.
- Ends with: Davian's perspective and chosen future posture.
- Watchpoints:
  - This should close Book 1 on choice, not on every consequence being solved.
  - Paradox specifics can be refined in close outlining.
  - Niall's Paradox handoff should be grudging recognition, not full reconciliation.

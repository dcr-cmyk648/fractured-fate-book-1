# Niall Grief, Rigidity, and Recovery Arc

This is an approved seed for Niall's broad arc, not a complete chapter-by-chapter outline.

## Approved Arc Direction

Niall's unresolved grief over Alara is a core wound and a main driver of his interactions throughout the book.

The prologue already shows this grief state affecting his actions: after killing retreating enemies with lightning, Maya physically stops him, and he briefly mistakes her for Alara before returning to himself.

Niall begins from rigidity, grief, perfectionism, and the need to prove he can still stand alone. He is terrified of being exposed as weak, and enemies can exploit that fear.

His arc should move toward accepting help, living with Maya and Selwin, and protecting valued living people rather than only preserving Alara's memory through control, isolation, and violence.

## Approved Boundaries

- Use neutral event language for Niall killing retreating enemies after they have broken; do not impose simple moral labels such as "massacre" or "atrocity."
- Keep detailed Alara history for `char-alara` and `event-alara-death-war-memory`.
- Treat hidden silver zan-strike-line-etched conduits as technology/modifiers for Niall's lightning, not as a separate ability.

## Deferred

- Exact Vorren fight execution
- Niall experiential brine/ink scene
- Exact Heart/silver conduit/charge mechanics
- Exact Alara/Niall backstory
- Possible Niall/Davian late sword beat

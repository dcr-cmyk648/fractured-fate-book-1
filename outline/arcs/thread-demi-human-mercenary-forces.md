# Demi-Human Mercenary Forces and Death Payments

This is an approved seed for the Book 1 mercenary/death-payment thread, not a complete military-economics file.

## Approved Core

Mercenary forces in and around the Six Cities often recruit desperate poor people who accept lethal risk so their families receive death payments.

Death money creates a grim economic logic: for some mercenaries, dying may help a family more than surviving crippled and unable to work.

Mercenaries can be attackers, tools of High Clade politics, and exploited poor people at the same time. The thread should not absolve attacking forces of violence, but it should also not flatten them into morally identical extensions of High Clade magi or political architects.

## Opening Attack

The opening Drakhal-backed attack on Rezin is unusually expensive.

For this attack, Drakhal likely spends a great deal of money to buy extra disposable troops. The force should include:

- long-term mercenaries or more experienced soldiers who help coordinate the attack and want to avoid unnecessary casualties
- disposable shock troops, including people who are not really trained soldiers and have signed up mainly because of death money

Maya's early casualties are not as clean or efficient as they would be if Niall were fighting from the start. She is in her first real combat and takes time to get the hang of killing at that scale.

Even as Maya kills more attackers, the force remains unusually persistent. Maya does not know why in the moment, but later context can reveal that money, shock-troop deployment, and the structure of the attack kept bodies moving into danger longer than ordinary soldiers would have.

When Niall appears and begins scything down soldiers, the force breaks almost immediately. This is because of the widespread carnage, general fear of Niall, and the fact that the disposable shock layer is being stripped away, exposing the more experienced "real" soldiers and coordinators who have stronger incentives to run and survive.

## Demi-Human and Class Composition

Demi-human-descended people are overrepresented in mercenary and military roles because discrimination, poverty, and lack of magic push them toward marginal work.

This overrepresentation is not exclusivity. Poor humans also serve in these troops.

Durnai are plausibly common in military and mercenary roles because they are the most prevalent demi-human lineage in the Six Cities and have useful physical/social placement for labor, archery, and infantry. Other marginalized lineages may appear where useful, but exact force composition remains candidate material.

## Moral and Character Function

This thread should keep the opening battle morally complex.

Maya saves Rezin and is also horrified by what her power does to people who cannot defend themselves from her. Niall's decision to kill retreating enemies can be tactically framed without turning the moment into simple moral certainty.

For Maya, the attack becomes a recurring reference point: her first use of overwhelming force, her horror at killing, and her fear of becoming a copy of Niall.

For the political world, the attack shows how High Clades can convert poverty into disposable violence, then argue about the aftermath through legitimacy, tactics, deterrence, and reputation.

## Continuity Constraints

- Do not make the opening attack random or cheap; it is Drakhal-backed and unusually expensive.
- Preserve the distinction between disposable shock troops and longer-term mercenaries/coordinators.
- Preserve that Maya's killing is horrifying but not as instantly efficient as Niall's would be.
- Preserve that the attackers are unusually persistent under Maya.
- Preserve that Niall's arrival breaks the force almost immediately.
- Do not imply all mercenaries are demi-human.
- Do not erase poor human mercenaries from the force.
- Preserve demi-human overrepresentation in mercenary/military roles.
- Preserve Durnai as plausibly common in military/mercenary roles.
- Preserve the tactical distinction between attacking, broken, retreating, wounded, crippled, and helpless fighters.

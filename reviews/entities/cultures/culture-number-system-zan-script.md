# Number System / Zan Script Review Packet

## Review Depth

Standard.

This entity is not a foundational magic-system review because `magic-zan-lattices` already owns the core circuitry model, but it has meaningful narrative leverage: it affects visible terminology, haloes, Concord cipher/sign language, Davian's mask symbolism, Registry/Treasury zánwork descriptions, and later manuscript cleanup.

## Why This Entity Matters

This review improves the book by:

- resolving the project-wide `zä`/`zän` versus `zán`/`zá` terminology conflict before it spreads further
- clarifying the everyday cultural logic behind haloes, dates, marks, signs, and script
- preserving enough Nizán/zero/void meaning to support Davian's mask, Creator/Sheol candidates, and future cosmology without overdefining them now
- keeping detailed charts, phonemes, and hand signs available for texture without making them mandatory outline blockers

## Source Summary

Approved magic/object records already establish:

- Zánwork operates like magical circuitboards.
- A zán is a meaningful magical sign/component, and lattices connect components into circuits.
- The base-6 number system is structurally linked to zán design.
- A strike-line runs through numbers and characters and conducts energy between zán in a lattice.
- Modern writing systems and masked/Concord hand signs derive from older zán/zá principles.
- Haloes use value marks from 1 to 6, follow base-6 currency conversion, and use an authenticity mark that can be activated.
- Davian's mask has a start-book equality/equilibrium mark and a later Nizán zero/negation/void mark.
- The Nizán is visually two sets of overlapping parallel lines leaving a diamond-shaped empty center.

Resolved conflict:

- Earlier approved reference files used `zä` singular, `zän` plural/system adjective, and `zänwright`.
- The approved convention is now `zán` singular/root, `zá` plural, and `zánwright`, with accents on the `a`.

Source/candidate notes also preserve:

- Nizán is used for zero; in multi-digit numbers, an empty value is usually indicated by a notch rather than repeating Nizán.
- The 36 days of a month each have a two-digit number.
- Two-digit numbers may have phonemes and hand shapes that vary by dialect.
- `strike-line`, `dyad`, and possible `monad` terms exist as candidates.
- Archetypal zán such as the Tyrant remain candidate material.

## Likely Accepted Core

- The culture uses a base-6 number system.
- The number system is not separate from magic culture: its notation, script history, hand signs, haloes, and zánwork share structural roots.
- The strike-line is a core visual/functional feature that can carry meaning in writing and conduct energy in zán lattices.
- Halo value marks and the Nizán mark make the number system visible in daily life.
- Nizán means zero / negation / void at the accepted high level, with exact theological/cosmological meaning deferred.
- Detailed charts, phoneme mappings, dialect variants, hand-shape inventory, and exact script history can remain candidate material unless needed for the outline.

## Outline Readiness

Outline-ready with documented uncertainties. The terminology, base-6/strike-line model, Nizán zero behavior, and monad/dyad terms are accepted; detailed charts, phonemes, hand signs, archetypal zán, and deeper cosmology remain candidate material.

## Checkpoint Questions

1. Should the final accepted spelling now change to `zan` singular, `za` plural, and `zanwright`, superseding the current approved `zán` / `zá` / `zánwright` convention?
2. Is the base-6 number system accepted as culturally central and structurally linked to zan lattice design through the strike-line?
3. Is Nizán accepted at high level as zero / negation / void, with its deeper cosmological meaning still deferred?
4. Should Nizán be used for zero as a concept/sign, while ordinary multi-digit empty place values usually use a notch or other simpler marker?
5. Should detailed digit charts, day-number phonemes, dialectal hand signs, dyad/monad terms, and archetypal zan remain candidate material unless they become outline-relevant?

## Author Decisions

- Final accepted spelling is `zán` singular/root, `zá` plural, and `zánwright`, always with accents on the `a`.
- The base-6 number system is culturally central and structurally linked to zán lattice design through the strike-line.
- Nizán is accepted at high level as zero / negation / void, with deeper cosmological meaning deferred.
- Nizán is used in the written numeric system to mean absolute zero / nothing.
- Nizán has no strike-line; it is visually two sets of overlapping parallel lines leaving a diamond-shaped empty central area.
- When Maya sees the `eye` of the extraplanar deity at the conclusion of Book 1 while resurrecting Davian, it appears as a diamond-shaped eye.
- Ordinary multi-digit empty place values do not use Nizán. They use no strike-line continuation or a small notch to mark an intentionally blank place.
- A value equivalent to decimal 6 would be conceptually equivalent to `10` in base-6 Arabic numerals, but in-world notation would be closer to a first value mark for 1 followed by an intentionally blank second place.
- The author's detailed digit chart is canon to the author and defines Nizán as zero, the cross-lines for values 0-5, and how longer digits string together on a long strike-line.
- `Monad` is canon for single-digit numbers, and `monadic` is canon for single-handed signs.
- `Dyad`, `triad`, and related terms are canon for multi-digit numbers, and `dyadic`, `triadic`, etc. are canon for multi-handed signs.
- Specific archetypal zán remain candidate material for now.

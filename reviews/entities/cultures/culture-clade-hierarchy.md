# Clade Hierarchy / Caste System Review Packet

## Source Summary

Approved records already establish the core shape:

- High Clades are the ruling tier of the Six Cities.
- A High Clade is politically defined by holding one of the six citadels / city-wedges.
- Current canonical High Clades are Rezin, Archeon, Nyctaris, Drakhal, Cryndor, and Lyranth.
- High Clade status can change by violent displacement; Archeon rose from Low Clade status by taking a citadel from a prior High Clade.
- City-wedges are governed through Low Clade councils and proxy structures reporting to the relevant High Clade.
- Sorevin is a Low Clade proxy administrator for Rezin interests, handling contractor vetting and local tasks.
- Haloes are minted under an unspecified High Clade, with a Low Clade proxy managing day-to-day operations.
- The Twilight Concord is a Masked Clade: mafia-esque / resistance-like, not a formally accepted High Clade tier.
- High Clades mock the term `Masked Clade` because its members generally lack high-caliber hereditary magic and citadel legitimacy.

Source material adds:

- Areas far from the Spoke roads and near city-wedge boundaries are poorer, less maintained, and less supported.
- Low Clade councils divide responsibility within city-wedges differently depending on the city.
- High Clade citadels and internal concerns usually matter more to rulers than the broader city-wedge population.
- Birth contracts can create exploitative status leverage for unPresented or lower-status people, especially when they produce a Presented child.
- Presentation, Shroud expectations, fashion, and Presented/unPresented culture belong primarily to `culture-magus-presentation`, not this record.

## Likely Accepted Core

- The Clade system is a caste hierarchy anchored by political control, hereditary magic, Shroud/magus legitimacy, citadel access, bloodline status, wealth, and violence.
- `High Clade` is a formal ruling category tied to citadel/city-wedge control.
- `Low Clade` is a lower formal category that can include administrative houses, contractors, proxy managers, councils, and other subordinate power structures.
- `Masked Clade` is a non-state / underworld / resistance category that appropriates the language of Clades; High Clades treat the term as a joke or insult.
- There is no need to define a full exhaustive taxonomy beyond High, Low, and Masked Clades for Book 1 unless a source forces it.
- Social mobility exists, but usually through violence, political utility, birth-contract leverage, or being useful to a High Clade rather than through fair legal promotion.
- City-wedge neglect and boundary/slum poverty are part of the hierarchy: rulers prioritize citadels and elite power centers over peripheral populations.

## Checkpoint Questions

1. Should the Book 1 hierarchy record focus on three usable categories: High Clades, Low Clades, and Masked Clades, without inventing a full middle-tier taxonomy now?
2. Is High Clade status formally defined by control of one of the six citadels / city-wedges, with Archeon as the accepted example of violent Low-to-High mobility?
3. Should Low Clades be treated as subordinate formal houses that can serve as councils, proxies, contractors, administrators, and mint managers under High Clade authority?
4. Should Masked Clades remain underworld/resistance organizations that appropriate `Clade` language, with High Clades treating the term as mockery rather than legal equivalence?
5. Should birth contracts and Presented/unPresented status be mentioned here only as hierarchy pressure points, while detailed rules move to `culture-magus-presentation` and High Clade politics?

## Author Decisions

- The only real categories are High Clades and Low Clades.
- Masked Clades are criminal/unofficial terminology rather than a formal category.
- Finer distinctions are primarily by family names and reputation, such as everyone knowing the Rezins and Drakhals are more powerful.
- High Clade status is defined by control of one of the six citadels / city-wedges.
- Archeon is the accepted example of violent Low-to-High mobility.
- Low Clades are subordinate formal houses that can serve as councils, proxies, contractors, administrators, and mint managers under High Clade authority.
- Low Clades have Shroud-producing users of hereditary magic.
- People without important Clade affiliation generally subordinate themselves to Clade authority or become criminals.
- Masked Clades may see themselves as revolutionary, but they implicitly buy into Clade language and might-makes-right culture.
- Presented/unPresented status is important enough to include here as a hierarchy pressure point.
- A High Clade that fails to produce enough safely Presented magi shows weakness and may invite attack from another Clade.
- Presenting a magus is both a strength signal and a public exposure risk.
- More Presented magi is generally better only if they are high-quality enough to hold their own.

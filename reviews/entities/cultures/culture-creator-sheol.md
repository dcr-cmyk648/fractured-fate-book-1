# Creator / Sheol / Gods and Religious Language Review Packet

## Source Summary

Current source material uses Creator/Sheol language in several different ways:

- `Creator` appears as a cultural/religious term for the one who made the world, and as an oath, curse, or exclamation in ordinary speech.
- `Sheol` appears as curse/death/hell/underworld language.
- Archeon has accepted location canon for a throne room with stained glass windows that are remnants from prior High Clade Creator-worship.
- Current notes include possible future arguments about the Creator, gods, false gods, original sin, reality being broken, Nizan, the Heart, and an evil/extraplanar deity.
- Approved Fateweaving/Skein and Elric records already accept the high-level idea that Elric learns reality was fractured after the original sin of the first magus, but the detailed cosmology remains unresolved.
- Alara has source/candidate material suggesting a sincere spiritual orientation and belief in a good deity, but that is more character-specific than culture-wide.

Under the minimum-sufficient-canon workflow, this packet should probably settle the usable Book 1 language and cultural residue without resolving the true cosmology.

## Likely Accepted Core

- `Creator` is an in-world religious/cultural term for a maker/deity figure.
- Creator language can appear in oaths, exclamations, curses, and older worship traditions.
- Modern High Clade culture may retain Creator language more as idiom, curse, art, inherited architecture, and cultural residue than as uniformly active devotion.
- `Sheol` is in-world death/hell/underworld language used in curses and religious or mythic comparison.
- Archeon throne-room stained glass remains accepted as a remnant of prior Creator-worship by earlier High Clade occupants.
- References to characters as gods, Death, Sheol, false gods, or divine figures should be treated carefully: many are thematic, rhetorical, political, or candidate cosmology rather than settled literal theology.

## Candidate / Deferred Material

- Whether the Creator literally exists.
- Whether the Creator made a mistake, abandoned the world, wound up the world like a clock, or is misunderstood.
- Whether there is a good deity, evil god, dark god, extraplanar deity, Nizan entity, Zerakh'tum, or other literal cosmological force.
- Exact relationship among the Creator, first magus, original sin, the Skein, Heart, Nizan, and reality fracture.
- Whether the Heart is a divine remnant or something else.
- Whether Niall, Serathis, Davian, Maya, or other magi are framed as gods only rhetorically or in some deeper metaphysical sense.
- Alara's personal theology beyond the high-level idea that she may be spiritually serious.
- Exact religious institutions, worship practices, clergy, holidays, doctrine, and modern distribution of belief.
- Selwin's possible final-series deity-killing role.

## Author Decisions

- Packet boundaries are flexible, but the true nature of the fractured fate of reality, Sheol, and related cosmology has been developed by the author and must be preserved somewhere.
- `Creator` is accepted as the in-world term for the Creator.
- Creator language is mostly used in oaths, curses, exclamations, and sometimes older worship traditions.
- Modern practice primarily ignores the concept of the Creator other than acknowledging that there definitely is one.
- The prevailing modern posture is deist-like: the Creator made the world, set it in motion, and left.
- Serathis and Elric believe the absent-watchmaker cosmology more explicitly.
- `Sheol` is accepted as vague language for something or somewhere bad.
- Sheol could include the void, somewhere an extraplanar deity exists, non-existence, planes outside the Skein, or similar hostile metaphysical outside.
- The Niall/Serathis conversation will happen, but the dialogue remains in development.
- That conversation is primarily a place to surface Elric cosmology: absent watchmaker, broken created order, and the basis of Elric's psychotic depression.
- Archeon throne-room stained glass remains accepted as remnants of prior High Clade Creator-worship.
- Alara's good-God/spiritual orientation should remain owned by `char-alara` because it will not come up much culture-wide.

## Approval Result

Approved. Accepted Book 1 culture language and religious residue are stored in `bible/cultures/culture-creator-sheol.md`; unresolved true cosmology, fractured fate, Sheol metaphysics, Heart/Nizan/deity links, exact Niall/Serathis dialogue, and Alara-specific spirituality are preserved in `planning/candidates/cultures/culture-creator-sheol.md`.

# Demi-Human Peoples, Origins, and Social Roles Review Packet

## Review Depth

Standard.

This entity has real story leverage through class hierarchy, prejudice, mercenary economics, Maya/Alara/Naeroth perceptions, and army composition. It does not need an exhaustive species taxonomy for Book 1.

## Why This Entity Matters

Demi-human peoples can make the setting's class structure more concrete. They also support:

- Davian's working-class and anti-Clade worldview
- High Clade prejudice and human/magic supremacy assumptions
- the moral complexity of mercenary armies
- Alara/Maya/Naeroth prejudice without making Naeroth the only non-human lineage
- future chapter texture around soldiers, contractors, and poor districts

## Source-Backed Information

### Accepted Naeroth Boundary

`culture-naeroth-lineages` already owns Naeroth-specific canon:

- Alara is Naerothi.
- Maya and Selwin are mixed Rezin/Naerothi descendants.
- Naeroth are a rare exception to the dominant High Clade assumption that Shrouds/hereditary magic belong only to humans.
- Naeroth can produce Shrouds and rare fateweaving / Convergence-associated abilities.
- Broader demi-human peoples, race taxonomy, mercenary roles, and non-Naeroth social structures belong here.

### Planning Notes

The strongest relevant notes are:

- smaller people who are not quite dwarves may make up much of the army; compact strength lets them use bows effectively and require less food on long campaigns
- several sub-races similar to orcs/dwarves/etc may exist, but widespread human interbreeding means differences are generally subtle
- any level of non-human lineage is considered low class, except Naeroth occupy a special position because they can manifest magic
- candidate traits include sharp teeth, heavy arms, unusual short/stocky build, gray or greenish skin tint, or unusual eyes
- there may or may not be six races
- mercenaries are desperate poor people willing to die to get death money for their families
- threatening a wounded mercenary with permanent crippling may be worse than death in a death-money economy

### Manuscript / Outline Context

Current and planning material use mercenary forces in the opening attack and later moral reflection. Harvick may explain that attacking mercenaries expect losses, try to pull back when magi arrive, and may be redirected to limit exposure. Maya later explicitly compares a later choice against killing attacking mercenaries who could not defend themselves from her.

## Contradictions / Tensions

- Naeroth are already accepted as a rare magic-capable exception, but broader demi-human peoples remain undefined.
- The notes raise "six races" as a possibility, but locking that number may create unnecessary taxonomy obligations.
- Mercenary death-money economics are narratively useful, but may be better owned jointly with `thread-demi-human-mercenary-forces`.
- Exact physical traits can add texture, but overdefining them could create continuity burdens before chapter mapping.

## Current Narrative Functions

- Social-world function: shows that status is not only High/Low Clade, but also race/lineage/class prejudice beneath magic hierarchy.
- Military function: explains why poor and demi-human-descended people appear in mercenary/army roles despite the dominance of magi.
- Moral function: complicates Maya/Niall/Davian judgments about killing soldiers and mercenaries.
- Character function: gives Davian a plausible social context for anti-Naeroth rumors, class resentment, and anxiety about Maya.
- Setting texture: makes working-class districts, armies, and contractor spaces feel less generically human without requiring a full fantasy-race catalog.

## Proposed Canon Core

If approved, the accepted record would store:

- Demi-human or non-human-descended peoples exist in Elarion and surrounding regions.
- Most non-Naeroth demi-human lineages are heavily interbred with humans, so traits are often subtle rather than sharply separated species categories.
- Non-human lineage is generally treated as low-status in High Clade society.
- Naeroth are a special exception because they can rarely produce Shrouds and fateweaving / Convergence-associated abilities.
- Other demi-human lineages are not known to produce hereditary magic or Shrouds in the way High Clade humans and rare Naeroth do.
- Some demi-human-descended groups, especially compact/stocky people, are common in armies or mercenary forces because they are effective in specific battlefield roles such as archery and have practical campaign advantages.
- Mercenaries are often desperate poor people who accept lethal risk in exchange for death payments to their families.

## Proposed Developmental Profile

Demi-human prejudice should reinforce the book's power logic: Elarion culture does not merely classify people by wealth or official Clade membership. It also sorts bodies, lineages, and magical potential into a hierarchy that High Clades treat as natural.

This can sharpen scenes without requiring lore lectures. A mercenary can be both an attacker and an exploited poor person. A demi-human soldier can be tactically valuable while socially disposable. Davian can believe anti-Naeroth rumors without the narrative validating them.

## Proposed Candidate / Flavor Bank

- Six total peoples/races may exist, but this should stay candidate unless the outline needs it.
- Candidate physical traits: sharp teeth, heavy arms, compact build, unusual short/stocky proportions, gray/greenish skin tones, unusual eyes.
- Smaller not-quite-dwarf people may make up a large share of archers or campaign troops.
- Orc/dwarf-like analogues may exist only as distant inspirations rather than direct fantasy species.
- Anti-Naeroth rumors may include claims that Alara bewitched Niall into ending the war and marrying her.
- Davian may have heard anti-Naeroth rumors and may use them to worry that attraction to Maya is unnatural or magically influenced.

## What Does Not Need to Be Defined Now

- Exact number of peoples/races.
- Full origin mythology.
- Full physical taxonomy.
- Exact homelands, languages, naming conventions, or political structures for each lineage.
- Whether every lineage needs a separate entity.
- Detailed mercenary contract law beyond the death-money concept.

## Continuity Constraints

- Do not contradict the accepted Naeroth exception: Naeroth can rarely produce Shrouds/fateweaving/Convergence-associated abilities.
- Do not give all demi-human peoples magic.
- Do not treat anti-Naeroth rumors as objective truth.
- Do not make mercenaries morally interchangeable with High Clade magi; many are poor people accepting lethal risk for family survival.
- Do not overdefine race taxonomy in ways that later chapter mapping must carry unless the author explicitly wants that level of structure.

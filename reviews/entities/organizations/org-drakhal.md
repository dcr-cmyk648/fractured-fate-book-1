# Entity Review: Drakhal Clade

## Proposed identity

Drakhal is a major High Clade tied to force-magic, birth contracts, political maneuvering against the Rezins after the Heart theft, cooperation or leverage with Archeon, and the characters Evana, Caius, and Vorren.

This review should store Drakhal as an organization only. The exact magic mechanics, Vorren's role, Caius, Evana/Ivana, and High Clade political structure should remain for their own reviews.

## Manuscript-established information

- Rezin records include a Drakhal ledger with many uncertain birth notations, birth contracts, outside-Clade entries, and unconfirmed maternal lines. Maya understands this as part of High Clade intermarriage and fertility-contract politics: contracts that produce a Presented child can dramatically alter status and favor. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:9048-9105`, paragraphs 3287-3305.
- Evana Drakhal and Caius enter Archeon space during Kelyra's captivity. They are immaculate, composed, and visibly high-status even underground. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`, paragraphs 7324-7331.
- Evana and Caius criticize Serathis after Archeon fails to recover the Rezin Heart thief. They are concerned that Niall may not be as weak as hoped, and Caius says their agents found another Archeon worker passing information to the Rezins and had him killed. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`, paragraphs 7332-7347.
- Serathis accuses Drakhal of wanting a one-sided fight where victory is assured; Evana insists Niall is the most powerful magus alive and reframes the Heart thief as a thief rather than assassin. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`, paragraphs 7338-7341.
- Caius shows force-magic or distortion around his hands when angered. Serathis intimidates him by implying a fight inside her citadel would be dangerous, since the plan for Niall involves drawing him there. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`, paragraphs 7348-7358.
- Serathis says Drakhal involvement is a backup plan and implies a breeding contract involving Kelyra and a Presented Drakhal magus, not necessarily Caius. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`, paragraphs 7360-7375.

## Planning-established information

- Planning says the Drakhals and Archeon visit to make a deal after the Heart theft. Drakhal wants collapse followed by controlled rebuilding with Drakhal on top, or at least sees that as an acceptable backup plan. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:270-326`.
- Planning includes a proposed Drakhal deal: Caius marries Maya and Niall is banished; marrying the daughter is a way to claim the legacy. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:276-302`.
- Planning suggests the Drakhals may accuse Niall of kidnapping one of their children under a breeding-contract right. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:276-302`.
- Planning emphasizes that Drakhal and allied High Clades fear Niall even after the Heart theft and wait to see whether his weakness is real before provoking him with overwhelming force. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:299-302`.
- Planning says Drakhal may use political legitimacy traps: provoke Niall into attacking in front of witnesses, arrange for him to disappear underground, or frame Rezin action as continuation of Niall's tyranny. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:299-302`, `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:8655-8676`.
- Planning says Drakhal may coordinate with Harbingers/Vorren through catacombs or underground routes. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:344`, `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:8238-8262`.
- Older Presentation planning labels Drakhal as `Aegistry`, describes them as force-magic users with imposing aura, and says Drakhal members exchange knowing glances during Maya's Presentation. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3236-3300`.
- Planning around Archeon says Drakhal looks down on Serathis/Archeon, uses leverage over Archeon's hidden weaknesses, offers breeding contracts, and wants more time to gather political support if Niall's weakness is uncertain. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7111-7198`.
- Planning includes a violent Vorren/Drakhal memory or scene where a Drakhal magus with a flaring sigil attacks Caius, and Ivana/Evana Drakhal intervenes with a focused force blast. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:945-960`.

## Consistent information

- Drakhal is a High Clade involved in birth-contract politics and force-magic.
- Drakhal is politically ambitious and willing to exploit Niall's weakness after the Heart theft.
- Drakhal is afraid of Niall and wants legitimacy, witnesses, overwhelming force, or indirect leverage rather than a clean fair fight.
- Drakhal is entangled with Archeon through alliance, leverage, information, breeding-contract offers, and shared anti-Rezin objectives.
- Evana is the current Drakhal Head Magus and Caius's mother.
- Caius is the main young Drakhal representative and marriage-candidate figure.
- Vorren is Caius's older brother. He was publicly banished from Drakhal at Niall's insistence, which gave Caius the heir position, but he secretly works for Drakhal-aligned interests.
- Drakhal's plans may be shaped or exploited by Elric's broader manipulation.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Evana / Ivana / Evand naming

- Version A: Manuscript uses `Evana Drakhal`.
- Version B: Later planning includes `Ivana Drakhal` and `Evand`.
- Source of each: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:276-326`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:945-960`.
- Resolution: Evana is the current name and current Head Magus. `Ivana` and `Evand` are prior draft options or obsolete spellings.

### Drakhal magic terminology

- Version A: Older outline label is `Aegistry`, force-magic, imposing aura.
- Version B: Later notes question whether Drakhal might be more about compressing/releasing, whether nomenclature may be Rezin-specific, and whether Drakhal or Lyranth should map to certain labels.
- Source of each: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3236-3300`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7018-7019`.
- Likely consequence: accepted organization file can say force-magic broadly, but exact taxonomy should wait for magic review.

### Drakhal proper versus Vorren/Harbinger underground operations

- Version A: Drakhal shields itself from direct danger and avoids conflicts that are not overwhelmingly in its favor, using Vorren, Archeon, political pressure, and other intermediaries.
- Version B: Manuscript shows Evana and Caius personally inside Archeon space and directly making decisions.
- Source of each: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:875`, `8238-8262`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20640-21160`.
- Resolution: both are true at different levels. Evana and Caius can act directly in controlled circumstances, but Drakhal strategy is to position itself for maximum upside while shielding itself from direct danger or unfavorable conflict.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal:

- Drakhal is a High Clade with force-magic associations. Exact `Aegistry` terminology remains candidate.
- Drakhal uses or participates in birth-contract politics and keeps extensive lineage/birth-contract records.
- Drakhal is politically ambitious and moves against the Rezins after the Heart theft because Niall may be vulnerable.
- Drakhal proposes Caius marrying Maya and Niall's banishment knowing Niall is essentially certain to refuse. The offer is designed to goad him into either a lethal confrontation with Drakhal and many other High Clades where they expect to win, or to send him to the Archeon citadel where Vorren and a lethal trap wait.
- Drakhal fears Niall enough to prefer legitimacy traps, overwhelming force, witnesses, alliances, and deniable intermediaries rather than straightforward confrontation. They position themselves for maximum upside while shielding themselves from direct danger or conflicts that are not overwhelmingly in their favor.
- Drakhal is entangled with Archeon through leverage, mutual anti-Rezin interests, spies/agents, and breeding-contract negotiations.
- Evana is the current Drakhal Head Magus and Caius's mother.
- Caius is the main young Drakhal representative and marriage-candidate figure.
- Vorren is Caius's older brother. He was publicly banished from Drakhal at Niall's insistence, which made Caius heir, but he secretly works for Drakhal interests because Evana and Elric manipulate his desire for chaos and death into situations aligned with their plans.
- The Drakhal/Archeon breeding-contract deal where Drakhal agrees to let Archeon breed Kelyra with Vorren is a canon verbal agreement between the parties. Whether Vorren knows, consents, or would follow through remains separate.
- Store `Aegistry` / exact force-magic terminology as candidate until magic taxonomy is reviewed.
- Keep detailed Vorren psychology, Drakhal/Harbinger operations, and final execution of the trap for later character/thread reviews.

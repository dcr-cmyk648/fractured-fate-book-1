# Entity Review: Registry

## Proposed identity

The Registry is the Rezin citadel's ancient magical security and identification system. It records true names and auras, tracks registered people inside the citadel, controls access/clearance, detects intruders, and can lethally respond when active.

This review is for the Registry as an institution/system. The physical chamber remains separately queued as `loc-registry`, and detailed zan-lattice mechanics remain for `magic-zan-lattices`.

## Manuscript-established information

- Niall orders Aldira/Alden to re-enable the Registry in passive mode after the Presentation attack so the Rezins can detect a bold infiltrator during the chaos. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1299`, paragraph 653.
- Contractors are added to the Rezin Registry and expected to live in the citadel during ward work. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1854-1857`, paragraphs 841-842.
- The Heart-theft plan relies on replacing Arrek Hadrin, using a Boon to copy his aura, and getting added to the Registry under his name. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1878-1905`, paragraphs 849-858.
- Kelyra states that the Registry is ancient High Clade-level magic that operates on immutable truths: aura must match true name, or the Registry will not work. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1890-1905`, paragraphs 853-858.
- Kelyra says Registry actions must happen in passive mode; removing a true name trips alarms, and active mode can burn an intruder out of existence. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1902-1905`, paragraphs 857-858.
- A guard warns Davian that unregistered people should not be in the citadel when the Registry comes back on. Davian understands active mode would treat his unregistered aura as an intruder and kill him. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2670-2679`, paragraphs 1123-1126.
- Alra tells Davian the Registry is the backbone of Rezin security; access can be revoked, and entering/exiting without clearance is dangerous. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2730-2748`, paragraphs 1143-1149.
- Alra says a contractor must provide true name and aura so everyone in the citadel is accounted for. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2736-2748`, paragraphs 1145-1149.
- The Registry process records `Arrek Hadrin` and marks him on a citadel projection after Davian uses the aura-mimic Ink. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2757-2793`, paragraphs 1152-1164.
- Davian recognizes the Registry as far beyond Low Clade or family magic and is unsettled that the Syndicate/Harbingers can temporarily fool it. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2802-2805`, paragraphs 1167-1168.
- Aldira/Alden later reasons that anyone already Registered has had their aura verified, that the magic does not lie, and that remaining contractors can be verified against the Registry. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2938-2994`, paragraphs 1219-1237.
- Maya uses the Registry to verify `Arrek Hadrin` as a valid entry and attempts to locate him in the citadel. The entry exists, but no aura location appears because he is not present. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15156-15234`, paragraphs 5356-5378.
- Maya thinks a citadel with free-flowing movement and near-perfect magical surveillance is a Rezin advantage that other High Clades covet and resent. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15222-15234`, paragraphs 5374-5378.
- During the Treasury crisis, the Registry chimes with a location pulse rather than an intruder alarm, shows `Arrek Hadrin`, and can refine the map to the Treasury corridor with multiple auras. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15940-15955`, paragraphs 5611-5616.
- After the Heart theft, Maya thinks Niall is trying to hide how badly the Registry has been compromised and that too many systems depend on it. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17188-17203`, paragraphs 6035-6038.
- Maya later considers isolating Registry detection to passive mode, focusing it around entrances, and powering it with zan sinks instead of a centralized feed. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20536-20542`, paragraphs 7165-7166.

## Planning-established information

- Planning says Maya spends nights rewriting zan lattices to bring visitor identification online at gateways, but the ancient system assumed near-infinite power input and is inefficient. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:113`.
- Planning says the Registry is overwhelmed in passive mode by multiple unregistered auras moving too fast, because it is built for paranoid magi guarding against stealth assassins rather than blitz attacks from multiple High Clade magi. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:1509-1516`.
- Planning says alarms are only on in active mode. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:1516`.
- Planning says the Heart powers the Registry, but the Registry can store only so much power and can still run out despite near-infinite Heart energy. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:4716`.
- Planning says the Registry is a major Rezin advantage that encourages centralization and protects against spies. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:6270`, `7135-7178`.
- Planning includes a possible simplification: Davian may only need to add Arrek's name and rely on the Ink Boon, rather than also adding/removing his true name. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:4111`.

## Bullet-note material

- Bullet notes say people are searched when coming and going if leaving the citadel, which offends Davian but experienced zanwrights consider it favorable compared with being locked in for a whole contract. Provenance: `imports/normalized/bullet-notes.md:186`.

## Consistent information

- The Registry is ancient, high-level Rezin security infrastructure.
- It binds identity to true name and aura.
- It can register contractors/authorized people and track/verify them.
- Passive mode provides detection/location/verification without full lethal response.
- Active mode is dangerous or lethal to unregistered intruders.
- The Registry is powerful but not infallible: Harbinger Ink can temporarily fool it, and rapid multi-attacker scenarios can overwhelm it.
- The Registry is politically important because it lets the Rezins maintain citadel security while allowing freer movement than other High Clades can manage.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Davian's true name process

- Version A: current manuscript planning has Davian copy Arrek's aura, get added under Arrek's name, then add/remove his true name.
- Version B: later planning proposes simplifying this so Davian adds only Arrek's name and relies on the Ink Boon.
- Likely consequence: accepted Registry record can store the current principle of name/aura verification, but the exact heist procedure should remain revision material.

### Infallible versus compromised

- Version A: in-world Rezin confidence treats the Registry as effectively foolproof once a person is Registered.
- Version B: Davian's Harbinger Ink fools it temporarily, the Heart theft compromises it, and planning says passive mode can be overwhelmed.
- Likely consequence: store the Registry as extremely strong but not actually infallible; the in-world claim of perfection is overconfidence.

### System versus room versus magic

- Version A: `Registry` is used for the security system/institution.
- Version B: `Registry` also names the chamber and its aurametric array/projection platform.
- Likely consequence: this file should own the organization/system; `loc-registry` should own the chamber; `magic-zan-lattices` should own underlying technical mechanics.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal:

### present-canon

- Store the Registry as the Rezin citadel's ancient magical security and identification system.
- The Registry records and verifies people through true name and aura.
- It supports contractor registration, clearance, location, verification, and intruder detection.
- Passive mode can detect, verify, locate, and warn without full lethal response.
- Active mode can trigger alarms and lethal magical response against unregistered/intruding auras.
- The Registry is the backbone of Rezin citadel security and a major political/administrative advantage.
- It allows freer contractor/personnel movement than other High Clades can safely permit.
- It is extremely strong but not infallible. Harbinger Ink can temporarily fool it through aura/name mimicry, and fast multi-attacker scenarios can overwhelm passive detection.

### backstory-canon

- The Registry is ancient and designed around older assumptions, including abundant centralized power and inefficient zan architecture.
- The Rezin advantage from the Registry has shaped citadel security and centralization.

### accepted-future-plan

- Maya attempts to repair or reconfigure the compromised Registry by isolating passive detection, focusing it around entrances/gateways, and using zan sinks or more efficient power arrangements.

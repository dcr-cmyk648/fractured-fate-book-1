# Entity Review: High Clades

## Proposed identity

The High Clades are the dominant ruling magical families / Clades in the Six Cities system. This review should establish the organization-level definition and shared political functions of the High Clades, while leaving broader social hierarchy, Presentation culture, individual Clade records, High Clade politics, and magic-system mechanics to their own entity reviews.

## Manuscript-established information

- The current manuscript shows the High Clades gathering for Maya's Presentation in a formal central chamber with six boxes corresponding to six citadels and the city map. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1425-1449`, paragraphs 695-703.
- Maya understands Presentation as a public legitimacy test: showing the Council that the Rezins are strong without revealing so much that they lose tactical advantage. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:351-372`, paragraphs 152-159.
- Niall tells Maya that other High Clades will respect her more if they do not know what she can do, and she should keep her power close. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1449-1464`, paragraphs 703-706.
- The Presentation ceremony frames the High Clades as the apex of society, born from magical conflict, and bound to train heirs in war, diplomacy, and scholarship. This is ceremonial ideology, not necessarily objective truth. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1491-1524`, paragraphs 717-728.
- Manuscript records and Maya's interpretation show High Clade intermarriage and fertility / birth contracts. Contracts that produce a Presented child can change status and favor, while failed or unPresented lines fade from record. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:9048-9105`, paragraphs 3285-3305.
- Aldira/Alden explains that Elric once wanted to outlaw birth contracts, calling them slavery for those offering the contract and the children born from them, but some exploited parties resisted abolition because contracts were their only leverage and protection. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:9048-9105`, paragraphs 3292-3305.
- Serathis explicitly frames the core question as what makes a High Clade a High Clade and how a Low Clade could rise high enough to become one. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20980-21120`, paragraphs 7321-7323.
- The current manuscript states that High Clade citizens and magi have extreme social privilege, and that public reputation matters enough that injury by a non-magus could collapse a house. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17320-17365`, paragraphs 6157-6173.
- Maya tells Davian that High Clade citadels were originally designed to house a city-wedge population during war, though current magus warfare changes what that would mean. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21460-21490`, paragraphs 7577-7586.

## Planning-established information

- Approved Archeon material establishes the current political definition: Archeon is a High Clade because it holds one of the six citadels that form the core of a city-wedge in the Six Cities. A Low Clade can rise by killing the prior occupying High Clade and taking the citadel. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`.
- Approved Rezin material establishes that the Rezin family / Clade is a major High Clade centered on its citadel, inherited magus status, military command, and assets such as the Heart, Treasury, Registry infrastructure, and citadel defenses. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-rezin.md`.
- Approved Drakhal material establishes Drakhal as a High Clade associated with force-magic, birth-contract politics, and anti-Rezin maneuvering after the Heart theft. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-drakhal.md`.
- Planning says the areas far from the spoke roads are the boundaries between city-wedges and are poorly maintained. It proposes that Low Clade councils report to High Clades, with the Rezins and Maya/Niall running the whole city through proxies. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:360`.
- Later worldbuilding notes revise or clarify city structure: each High Clade governs a legally distinct city / city-wedge; zones within the city are governed by Low Clades; the Rezins have more centralized administration, but most High Clades prioritize citadel concerns. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:6266-6309`.
- Planning says Niall stopped war unilaterally and acts as a one-man deterrent; large standing armies are less central than citadel guards, support forces, and magi. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:6266-6283`.
- Planning says High Clade disputes used to be settled by formalized duels in a central arena until yield, rarely death, because High Clade magi are too valuable. After Alara died, Niall set a precedent of direct lethal dominance. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:1386-1395`, `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:1473-1476`.
- Planning flags that many High and Low Clades hide true ability details through subterfuge, and that other High Clades would gang up on Archeon if their status-lowering vulnerabilities were exposed. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:261`, `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:1452`.
- Planning notes that sleeveless High Clade fashion symbolizes reliance on power. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2391`.

## Consistent information

- There are six High Clade positions tied to six citadels / city-wedges / city cores in the Six Cities structure.
- High Clade status is political and territorial, not purely bloodline purity: Archeon rose from Low Clade to High Clade by taking a citadel.
- High Clades guard the details of their powers and tactics.
- The Presentation is both personal rite and political legitimacy test.
- High Clade families manage lineage, Presentation, Shrouds, birth contracts, marriage, and inherited magical status as power tools.
- High Clades rule by force, inherited magic, citadel control, social ritual, military support, and proxy administration.
- Rezin, Archeon, and Drakhal are approved current High Clades.
- Niall's post-Alara willingness to kill rival High Clade magi altered the political balance and contributes to other Clades' fear of direct confrontation.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Central Spire / six citadels / legally distinct cities

- Version A: Current manuscript Presentation material centers on a Central Spire chamber where all six High Clades convene, with boxes corresponding to six citadels at a hexagon's corners. Source: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1317-1449`.
- Version B: Later notes say the Central Spire is gone or revised, and each High Clade governs a legally distinct city / city-wedge. Source: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:5271`, `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:6266-6309`.
- Likely consequence: the accepted file can store the six-citadel / city-wedge definition, but exact Central Spire geography and whether the Spire survives should remain candidate until location and High Clade politics reviews.

### Known High Clade names and magic labels

- Version A: Current manuscript / older Presentation material lists or implies Rezin, Archeon, Nyctaris, Drakhal, Cryndor, and Lyranth, with older magic labels such as Incarna, Nocturne, Severance, and Resonance. Source: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1425-1467`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3236-3300`.
- Version B: Later approvals have changed Archeon mechanics substantially and left Drakhal force-magic terminology unsettled. Source: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`; `bible/organizations/org-drakhal.md`.
- Likely consequence: only Rezin, Archeon, and Drakhal should be accepted as current named High Clades for now. Nyctaris, Cryndor, Lyranth, and old magic labels should remain candidate.

### Organization file versus culture / politics files

- Version A: High Clades can be treated as an organization / ruling bloc.
- Version B: Many details are better owned by `culture-clade-hierarchy`, `culture-magus-presentation`, `thread-high-clade-politics`, `magic-shroud`, and individual Clade records.
- Likely consequence: `org-high-clades` should stay concise and define the ruling bloc / institutions, while dependent files own social hierarchy, ceremonies, political arc, and mechanics.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal:

- The High Clades are the ruling Clade tier of the Six Cities system.
- A High Clade is defined politically by holding one of the six citadels that form the core of a city-wedge / legally distinct city in the Six Cities.
- High Clade status can change through violent displacement. Archeon was formerly a Low Clade and became High Clade by killing the prior occupying High Clade and taking the citadel.
- Known approved current High Clades are Rezin, Archeon, and Drakhal. Other older names remain candidate until reviewed.
- High Clades generally rule from citadels and through military, household, administrative, and Low-Clade/proxy structures. Exact proxy governance remains candidate.
- High Clade houses guard detailed knowledge of their abilities, bloodlines, weaknesses, citadel layouts, and tactical systems.
- Presentation is a High Clade legitimacy institution: a child/heir becomes publicly acknowledged as useful, controllable, and politically viable, not merely socially introduced.
- High Clade families use lineage management, intermarriage, fertility/birth contracts, and Shroud training as power systems. Birth contracts are exploitative but may also function as leverage or protection for those with little else.
- High Clade culture is hierarchical and includes anti-demi-human racism, including assumptions about humans, hereditary magic, and Shrouds that Naeroth lineages complicate.
- Niall's post-Alara lethal dominance changed the High Clade political environment from older formalized dueling norms toward fear of Rezin intervention.

## Questions for the author

Resolved by author feedback on 2026-06-24:

- `org-high-clades` should own the political definition: High Clade status is defined by holding one of the six citadels / city-wedge cores. `culture-clade-hierarchy` should own the wider caste system.
- Proxy governance of city-wedges in the Six Cities by Low Clade councils is accepted.
- There are six canonical High Clades, named at Presentation.
- Presentation should be stored here only as a legitimacy institution; ritual, training, fashion, and Presented/unPresented culture belong to `culture-magus-presentation`.
- Birth contracts are accepted as a system-wide High Clade practice, including the nuance that some exploited participants may experience them as leverage or protection.
- The Central Spire is in the center of the Six Cities, but `Spire` is a historical reference. It is now an amphitheater built into a blast hole from a long-ago High Clade war cataclysm.
- Anti-demi-human racism should be mentioned at a high level here because it reinforces and informs the whole system.

# Entity Review: Rezin Family / Rezin Clade

## Proposed identity

The Rezin family / Rezin Clade is a major High Clade power centered on the Rezin citadel, the Rezin ruling family, inherited magus status, military command, and control or use of key institutions and artifacts such as the Heart, the Registry, the Treasury, and citadel defenses.

Approved handling: use one accepted organization file for the Rezin family and Rezin Clade. The family and Clade are generally synonymous.

## Manuscript-established information

- Maya is preparing for a Presentation before the High Clade Council. Niall reassures her that she has what she needs to stand before them, and Maya fears letting him down. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:230-420`, Prologue, paragraphs 89-105.
- Maya thinks the Presentation is for the family, not only for herself: it must show the Council that the Rezins are strong without revealing too much tactical information. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:392-560`, Prologue, paragraphs 151-163.
- During the citadel attack, a Rezin soldier tells Maya that Lord Niall was drawn to the eastern wall while the western defenses are losing ground. Maya answers that there is no point in Presentation if she cannot protect the people her family is responsible for. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:392-560`, Prologue, paragraphs 200-218.
- Rezin soldiers fight on the walls; after Maya's destructive defense, soldiers call her Lady Maya and thank her. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:392-560`, Prologue/Chapter 1, paragraphs 222-240 and 360-384.
- Niall commands the military response after the attack. He summons wall commanders, demands accountability, and orders evidence of who financed and coordinated the assault. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:640-760`, Chapter 1, paragraphs 392-410.
- Selwin is guarded by elite Rezin guards. Even before Presentation, his High Clade blood and Niall's status give him enough social authority that the guards hesitate when he wants to speak with Davian. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:4590-4675`, paragraphs 1791-1808.
- Maya trains Selwin inside the citadel and thinks of Presentation as survival for a High Clade child: until a child can fight on the front lines and form a strong Shroud, exposure to the outside world is dangerous or embarrassing for the Clade. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2228-2310`, paragraphs 981-998.
- The current manuscript links Rezin family history to Elric, hidden records, birth contracts, Alara's Naeroth origin, and missing lineage research. Alden says Niall never used birth contracts and that there are no unPresented Rezin children other than Selwin. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:9048-9188`, paragraphs 3288-3334.
- Aldira says Elric had an overwhelming, magnetic presence and that no one before or after made the High Clades work together the way he did. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:13920-13975`, paragraphs 4938-4954.
- Chapter 37 shows Maya training Davian and Selwin while working on Registry zan lattices at night; she feels pressure to project certainty to those the Rezins rule over even though she realizes no one fully knows what they are doing. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1-65`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:128-136`.

## Consistent information

- The Rezin organization is both a family and a major High Clade power.
- Niall is the current feared Rezin patriarch and political/military authority.
- Maya is the Rezin heir / Lady Maya and is being publicly introduced through Presentation.
- Selwin is an unPresented Rezin child whose status still carries power and risk.
- Alara's Naeroth origin is important to Rezin family history, Maya's appearance, and social/political tension.
- Elric Rezin is historically central to the family and broader High Clade history, but much of his role is hidden, suppressed, or dangerous knowledge.
- The Rezin power base includes citadel defenses, soldiers/guards, wall commanders, elite guards, the Treasury, the Heart, Registry infrastructure, and advisers or core retainers.
- Aldira, Harvick, and Erynn are inner-circle household figures. Guards, healers, wall commanders, and zanwrights function inside or around the Rezin power structure, but their exact offices should be resolved in their own reviews.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Family file versus Clade/institution file

- Version A: Treat "Rezin" primarily as the ruling family/bloodline: Niall, Maya, Selwin, Alara, Elric. Source: approved character files and decisions.
- Version B: Treat "Rezin" as a broader Clade/institution with soldiers, commanders, guards, servants, advisers, Treasury workers, Registry systems, citadel administration, and political subjects. Source: manuscript scenes throughout the citadel attack, Presentation, Selwin guard scene, and Chapter 37 planning.
- Likely consequence: A single file may blur family, household, military, and government. Splitting too early may create unnecessary overhead.
- Resolution: Use one `org-rezin` file. The family and Clade are generally synonymous.

### Elric's Rezin legacy

- Version A: Elric is family history: Niall's father, Maya's grandfather, brilliant former Rezin magus, old reformer, lineage researcher.
- Version B: Elric is the hidden antagonist still manipulating the plot through Harbingers/Syndicate, Ink, fate strands, and reality damage.
- Source of each: `bible/characters/char-elric.md`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7428-7560`, `9048-9188`, `13920-13975`; `decisions/0007-elric-rezin-remnant-and-reality-skein-role.md`.
- Likely consequence: The organization file should not become an Elric plot file, but it also cannot omit that the Rezin legacy contains this hidden danger.
- Resolution: Store only a minimal Rezin-org note on Elric: he has little day-to-day influence on Rezin operations, but his early influence and political reform ideas likely informed some Rezin operating differences. Keep detailed hidden-antagonist mechanics in `char-elric`, `org-harbingers-syndicate`, `magic-ink-boons`, and `magic-fateweaving-skein`.

### Rezin power base versus separate entities

- Version A: The Heart, Treasury, Registry, citadel, zan lattices, Presentation, and High Clade Council are part of Rezin institutional power.
- Version B: Each of those has or should have a separate entity record.
- Source of each: `ENTITY_INDEX.md`; manuscript and outline references above.
- Likely consequence: The Rezin file should summarize dependencies without preempting later object/location/culture/magic reviews.
- Proposed resolution: Store high-level relationships only and defer mechanics.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal:

- `org-rezin` stores the Rezin family and Rezin Clade together because they are generally synonymous.
- The Rezin family is a major High Clade power centered on the Rezin citadel.
- Current core family: Niall Rezin, Maya Rezin, Selwin Rezin, Alara, and Elric Rezin.
- Niall is the feared current patriarch and military/political authority.
- Maya is the heir / Lady Maya and is being publicly introduced as a Rezin magus through Presentation.
- Selwin is an unPresented Rezin child whose status is dangerous and politically/socially significant.
- Alara's Naeroth origin is part of Rezin family history, a source of Maya's visible dual heritage and outside prejudice, and a significant divergence from normal High Clade political leanings.
- Elric is a former Rezin patriarch/major Rezin magus. His hidden antagonist role should be minimal in this organization file because he has little influence on day-to-day Rezin operations and primarily works through direct character influence and other organizations such as the Harbingers. His earlier influence and political reform desires may inform some Rezin operating differences.
- Rezin power includes soldiers, wall commanders, elite guards, personal guards, advisers, healers, servants, zanwrights/contractors, citadel defenses, the Treasury/Heart, and Registry infrastructure.
- Aldira, Harvick, and Erynn are inner-circle household figures.
- The Rezin culture shown so far reflects general High Clade culture: Presentation, Shroud competence, tactical secrecy, command posture, harsh standards, and projecting certainty to those under Rezin protection or rule.
- The Rezin version is comparatively less harsh because of the family's apex position and the presence of ultra-powerful magi such as Niall and Maya. Selwin's lack of constant extreme training to force Shroud manifestation would be atypical in another Clade.
- The org file should explicitly defer exact mechanics of the Heart, Registry, Shrouds, silver conduits, citadel layout, and High Clade hierarchy to their own records.

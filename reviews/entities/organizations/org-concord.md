# Entity Review: Twilight Concord / Concord

## Proposed identity

The Twilight Concord / Concord is a Masked-Clade-adjacent criminal or underclass resistance organization operating from a dilapidated former Low Clade manor. It is Davian's family organization and Kelyra's de facto power base.

The record should establish the Concord as a distinct organization from the Harbingers/Syndicate, while preserving its dependence on Harbinger Ink and pressure during the Heart-theft plot.

## Manuscript-established information

- The prologue names the Twilight Concord and shows it operating as a disciplined masked strike crew under Jalen's field direction. Members use masks, hand sign, silence, stealth, and coordinated violence during the Low Clade manor operation. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:273-336`, paragraphs 116-137.
- Concord gear includes matte black masks. Davian's mask is customized with visible scratch marks, and Jalen criticizes the tactical risk. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:282-297`, paragraphs 119-124.
- Concord members use sign in the field. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:282-321`, paragraphs 119-132.
- The Concord manor is a mismatched, weathered former Low Clade home abandoned during old conflict, now patched into a dilapidated fortress / hideout in a grim district. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1704-1707`, paragraphs 791-792.
- The manor has a courtyard with training dummies, repairs, muted conversations, and members/guards. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1707`, paragraph 792.
- Kelyra has authority in the Concord household/manor space, sends Kael and Lirien away from adult business, and directs Jalen and Davian to the central hall. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1707-1752`, paragraphs 792-807.
- The central hall is a repurposed dining area / meeting space used for planning. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1752-1851`, paragraphs 805-841.
- Early manuscript material still frames the Harbinger/Syndicate threat as a threat to Jalen's seat and Trial of Dominion. This conflicts with accepted later direction. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1059-1065`, paragraphs 569-571.
- Kelyra remembers receiving Ink after Selina's death and using that power to kill the former Concord leader. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16585-16615`, paragraphs 5829-5839.
- Kelyra later says or remembers that Karra's Ink has challenged the old power structure, and the Harbingers use Karra as part of the betrayal/penalty. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16636-16888`, paragraphs 5846-5871 and following.
- The Concord uses a cipher described as pared-down zan, stripped of flourish, used phonetically and rearranged in a precise sequence. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17899-17923`, paragraphs 6274-6280.
- The Concord hideout is a maze of grafted rooms, corridors, old stone, newer timber, and questionable structural integrity. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18151-18160`, paragraphs 6356-6359.
- Aldira's records classify the Concord as a criminal organization / Masked Clade-style organization; her index notes `Concord org.` and `K. Virellis`, indicating a leadership change about ten or twelve years earlier. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18682-18700`, paragraphs 6540-6547.

## Consistent information

- The Concord is Davian's family organization and social home.
- The Concord operates through masks, sign/cipher, stealth, assassination/raid work, guarded manor infrastructure, and underclass/criminal networks.
- Kelyra is now the accepted de facto primary leader.
- Jalen remains important as Kelyra's husband and as support/planning/logistics, but is not the martial or political leader.
- The Harbingers/Syndicate can pressure the Concord because the Concord depends on, owes, or has bargained for Ink power and underworld leverage.
- Selina's death is a major emotional driver behind Kelyra/Jalen/Davian's anti-Clade orientation and Kelyra's first bargain for Ink power.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Jalen's seat versus Kelyra's leadership

- Version A: older manuscript/planning says Jalen has a seat, Boons, and Trial of Dominion vulnerability.
- Version B: accepted direction says Kelyra is the de facto primary leader, gained power through an Ink Pact, killed the prior leader in Trial of Dominion, and is the one whose position is threatened.
- Likely consequence: accepted Concord record should store Kelyra as leader and mark older Jalen-seat material as suspect for revision.

### Trial of Dominion participant

- Version A: some planning says Jalen goes to or fights in the Dominion fight.
- Version B: accepted decision says Kelyra canonically fights in the Trial of Dominion; Jalen supports and advises but does not participate.
- Likely consequence: store Kelyra as the Trial participant; preserve older Jalen fight choreography only as suspect transferable material.

### Organization type

- Version A: Concord appears as a family/resistance group and Davian's home organization.
- Version B: Aldira categorizes it among heads of criminal organizations that style themselves as Masked Clades.
- Likely consequence: both may be true. Concord can see itself as family/resistance/underclass defense while outsiders track it as a criminal Masked-Clade-style organization.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal:

### present-canon

- Store one organization file for `org-concord`.
- Name it `Twilight Concord / Concord` unless the author chooses a different formal name.
- Treat the Concord as a Masked-Clade-adjacent underclass/criminal resistance organization rooted in Davian's family and Kelyra's leadership.
- Kelyra is the de facto primary leader.
- Jalen is a respected support/planning/logistics figure and Kelyra's husband, not the martial or political leader.
- Davian is a Concord operator/assassin tied to the organization through family, training, sign/cipher, masks, and missions.
- The Concord operates from a patched, dilapidated former Low Clade manor / hideout with training, guards, central planning space, and maze-like escape/security features.
- The Concord uses masks, sign, cipher, stealth, assassination/raid tactics, and underworld contacts.
- The Concord is vulnerable to Harbinger pressure because Kelyra received Ink power and the family/organization is bound by bargains, debt, leverage, and the threat of leadership challenge.

### backstory-canon

- Kelyra received an Ink Pact after Selina's death.
- Kelyra used Ink, Archeon-derived ability, and martial prowess to kill the prior Concord leader in a Trial of Dominion and take power.
- Aldira's records noticed a Concord leadership change about ten or twelve years earlier and record `K. Virellis`.

### accepted-future-plan

- Someone else is eventually granted comparable Harbinger/Ink power to challenge Kelyra's position.
- Exact Trial of Dominion/challenge execution remains for outline/revision rather than the bible.

# Entity Review: Niall Rezin

## Proposed identity

- Niall Rezin is Maya and Selwin Rezin's father, Alara's widower, and the feared Rezin patriarch.
- He is a powerful High Clade magus associated with lightning, Rezin authority, military command, grief over Alara, and harsh standards that shape Maya's perfectionism.
- His full grief/recovery arc is also represented by queued thread `thread-niall-grief-recovery`; this character packet should establish the person without finalizing every late-book plot beat.

## Manuscript-established information

- Niall enters Maya's Presentation preparation with warm pride and quiet authority. He tells Maya she looks radiant, says he is proud of her growth, and reassures her that she has what she needs before the High Clade Council. Maya responds by promising not to let him down. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:246-261`, Prologue, paragraphs 98-105.
- During the prologue attack, Niall is drawn away to the eastern wall, leaving the western defenses failing and motivating Maya to intervene. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:453-458`, Prologue, paragraphs 210-216.
- After Maya breaks the enemy force and lets retreating soldiers go, Niall cuts down retreating enemies with threaded lightning. Maya screams for him to stop because they are retreating, but he continues until she grabs his arm. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:645-657`, Prologue, paragraphs 334-341.
- When Maya stops him, Niall appears empty and distant, fails to recognize her, and murmurs Alara's name before returning to himself and cutting off the storm. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:660-669`, Prologue, paragraphs 343-348.
- Niall immediately resumes command posture afterward, praises Maya for defending the wall, and tells her to return to her chambers and prepare for her Presentation while soldiers watch with awe and apprehension. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:669-676`, Prologue, paragraphs 348-350.
- In Chapter 1, Niall responds to the commanders' failure with cold fury. He frames the breach through dead Rezin soldiers and the danger to his daughter, then orders concrete evidence of who financed and coordinated the attack. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:392-414`, Chapter 1, paragraphs 392-414.
- In the current manuscript, Niall's authority over Maya's environment is intense: he dismisses Erynn, questions Alra after the breach, and is presented as cold, controlled, and charged with lightning. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:416-420`, `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3102-3114`.
- Later manuscript material shows Niall attacking Davian after the Heart theft, with Maya interposing her Shroud and enduring a sustained lightning assault aimed at Davian. The scene frames Niall's power as both terrifying and weakening after the Heart theft. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20017-20092`.
- Current manuscript exposition from Maya says Niall's lightning method is structurally simple and efficient: he connects high electrical charge to lower charge and maintains long-distance connections. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19897-19900`.

They wait to see whether his weakness is real, then bring overwhelming force to provoke him into an unwinnable fight and create legitimacy for his defeat or disappearance. ## Consistent information

- Niall loves Maya and protects his family, but his love is expressed through command, pressure, and sometimes frightening ruthlessness.
- Niall's reputation is politically central. Enemies fear him and shape strategy around exposing or baiting his weakness.
- Niall's grief over Alara is unresolved and dangerous. It appears in manuscript as dissociation/misrecognition and in planning as a late-book collapse/recovery arc.
- Niall's standards and perfectionism have shaped Maya's self-concept.
- Niall's power is associated with lightning, long-distance decisive force, and a feared ability to annihilate enemies quickly.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Protector vs tactical wartime killing

- Version A: Niall protects Maya, Selwin, the Rezin citadel, and Rezin soldiers with fierce devotion.
- Version B: The current manuscript shows him cutting down retreating enemies after they have broken, continuing after Maya tells him to stop.
- Consequence: The accepted file should preserve the factual tension without imposing a simple moral label. The author clarified that battle actions such as killing retreating soldiers can be morally gray when tactically sound and in the context of war, especially given mercenary death-money practices.
- Resolution: Use neutral event language rather than terms such as "massacre" or "atrocity."

### Strategic restraint vs self-proving pride

- Version A: Niall can frame his choices as protecting soldiers or making efficient strategic examples.
- Version B: Planning says he also wants to prove to himself that he can still do it and that High Clade magi do not need help or wasteful tricks.
- Consequence: His accepted record should distinguish public/strategic framing from private motive if the author approves that layer.

### Lightning, silver, Heart, and charge mechanics

- Version A: Current manuscript clearly establishes lightning and electrical-charge mechanics.
- Version B: Bullet notes say Niall used the Heart to overcharge his silver and later must charge it himself. Author clarified that Niall wears hidden silver zan-strike-line-etched conduits throughout the book, explaining his constant military jacket despite accepted High Clade sleeveless fashion.
- Consequence: Character file can record observed lightning ability and hidden conduit use, but broader silver/Heart/zan-strike-line mechanics should remain for `object-seraph-heart`, `magic-zan-lattices`, or another magic review.
- Resolution: Treat silver conduits as a technology/modifier for Niall's lightning, not as an independent ability.

### Alara material belongs partly elsewhere

- Version A: Niall's grief over Alara is central to his character.
- Version B: Alara herself, her Naeroth background, her death, and war memories are separate queued entities.
- Consequence: Niall's file should include the emotional fact of Alara's loss while routing detailed Alara history to `char-alara` and `event-alara-death-war-memory`.
- Resolution: Keep Alara material minimal here: widower status, grief, misrecognition, and emotional wound.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal only:

- Niall Rezin is Maya and Selwin's father, Alara's widower, and the feared Rezin patriarch.
- He is a powerful High Clade magus whose current manuscript ability centers on lightning and electrical-charge manipulation.
- He loves Maya and is proud of her, but his approval, standards, and perfectionism place heavy pressure on her.
- He is a terrifying military and political figure. Enemies fear him enough to organize around baiting, weakening, or legitimizing attacks against him.
- In the current manuscript, Niall kills retreating enemies with lightning after Maya has already broken their attack, stops only when Maya physically grabs him, and briefly mistakes her for Alara. This is a key manifestation of grief, dissociation, and tactical ruthlessness.
- Niall treats attacks on Maya and the Rezin citadel as personal and responds with cold command pressure.
- Niall's grief over Alara is unresolved and should be stored as a core character wound and core arc driver, while detailed Alara history remains for separate review.
- Niall wears hidden silver zan-strike-line-etched conduits throughout the book. These are a technology/modifier for his lightning and explain his constant military jacket despite accepted High Clade sleeveless fashion.
- Niall's political terror around insults to Maya's Naeroth heritage/Alara should be recognized as accepted world/relationship context from the Maya decision.

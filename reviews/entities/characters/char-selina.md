# Selina Review Packet

## Review Depth

Standard.

Selina is deceased before the main present action, but she is structurally important. Her death drives Kelyra's Ink bargain, the Concord leadership takeover, Jalen's anti-Clade wound, Davian's guilt and revenge logic, the family mythology Kelyra sells Davian, and possibly Antithesis/mask history.

The review should define her story function and accepted history without overbuilding a character who mostly appears through memory, trauma, and family myth.

## Why This Entity Matters

Selina is the wound the Virellis family keeps converting into ideology.

For Kelyra, Selina is proof that running from Archeon and hiding from the Clade system failed. For Jalen, Selina is proof that he failed to protect a child. For Davian, Selina becomes the emotional permission structure for killing Arrek, hating High Clades, and trying to make Niall pay.

She also helps explain why Kelyra lies about the family as noble secret resisters and why Antithesis is falsely framed as an heirloom weapon.

## Relationships and Conflicts That Create Story

Selina and Kelyra:

Selina is the child Kelyra ran to protect from Archeon child-taking. Her death convinces Kelyra that hiding and ordinary safety are not enough, pushing her toward Ink, Concord leadership, and a family mythology of resistance.

Selina and Jalen:

Selina's death is a core wound for Jalen and likely shapes his fear of failing another child, but current accepted canon keeps him as support/logistics/tempering force rather than the primary revenge-arc owner.

Selina and Davian:

Davian uses Selina's death to justify violence and also feels guilt around failing her. His Arrek murder, Niall mission, fear during Archeon combat, and family loyalty all route through Selina memories.

Selina and Antithesis / Mask:

Selina may have used Antithesis, worn a Concord mask, or otherwise left behind an object Kelyra later gives/reframes for Davian. The exact object history remains unresolved.

## Proposed Developmental Profile

Selina should function as a person-shaped wound rather than a saint, a pure victim, or a complete off-page mystery.

The family remembers and uses her differently:

- Kelyra turns Selina into proof that only power prevents loss.
- Jalen turns Selina into proof he failed to protect his family.
- Davian turns Selina into proof that violence against the High Clades can be morally necessary.

This gives Selina real narrative leverage without requiring many new scenes.

## Author Decisions

1. Selina should have a dedicated standard-depth character record because she will probably come up more in later books.
2. The proposed core is accepted: Kelyra/Jalen's deceased child, Davian's sibling, Kelyra ran from Archeon while pregnant with her, Kelyra trained her before Davian, and Selina's death drives Kelyra's Ink bargain, Concord takeover, and family mythology.
3. Selina's death is accepted at high level: she went off to kill Vorren because of Kelyra's anti-Clade/destiny framing and was killed by overwhelming High Clade/Archeon-level power.
4. Object links are accepted: Davian's original mask is not Selina's; the later Nizän-marked mask Kelyra gives Davian is Selina's old Concord mask with the Nizän added; Selina used Antithesis. No other Selina keepsake is necessary now.
5. In Kelyra's mind, Davian could be the weapon Selina was not. Kelyra thinks her only mistake was letting Selina get away on her own to attack Vorren too early, instead of training her more and making a better plan to kill him.
6. Davian does not know the truth of Selina's death. Kelyra tells him Selina left to work for the High Clades, gave him her knife as a keepsake because she no longer wanted to fight and kill, and was later killed by Vorren for no reason. In reality, Selina was already dead by the time Kelyra later tells Davian about Vorren.

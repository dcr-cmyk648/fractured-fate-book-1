# Arrek Review Packet

## Review Depth

Standard.

Arrek has little on-page life as himself, but his death is structurally important. Davian's cover, Registry bypass, aura theft, guilt arc, Maya's investigation, worker-space belonging, and the morality of the Heart theft all depend on the real Arrek being a person rather than just an alias.

This packet should define enough of Arrek to make Davian's murder and identity theft coherent, but it should not turn Arrek into a sprawling subplot unless the book benefits from that.

## Why This Entity Matters

Arrek is the murdered worker whose name, aura, records, and place in the Rezin contractor project make Davian's infiltration possible.

His humanity sharpens Davian's guilt: Arrek is not a faceless High Clade enemy, but an ordinary Low Clade contractor with precarious finances, family debt obligations, and no known direct role in harming Davian's family.

Arrek also matters mechanically: the Registry recognizes `Arrek Hadrin` as valid, while Davian's Harbinger Ink maintains the stolen aura/name identity until it degrades.

## Current Narrative Functions

- Moral consequence for Davian's Heart-theft choice.
- Mechanism for the Registry/contractor infiltration.
- Evidence that the High Clade system harms ordinary Low Clade workers without making those workers villains.
- Foil to Davian's "enemy" framing: Arrek works for a High Clade contract, but he is still a precarious person with obligations.
- Identity pressure: Maya, Selwin, Tanelle, Marek, Harvick, and others know Davian under Arrek's name until the cover breaks.
- Setup for Davian's later confession and guilt over identity theft.

## Relationships and Conflicts That Create Story

Arrek and Davian:

Davian kills Arrek and takes his name/aura. Arrek's ordinary life and debt obligations make the murder a point of guilt rather than a clean tactical act.

Arrek and Kelyra:

Kelyra treats Arrek as an acceptable target because he works for the High Clades and, in her ideology, all Clade collaborators are evil. That framing is crucial to Davian's moral pressure and later disillusionment.

Arrek and the Rezin contractor system:

Arrek is a real registered contractor. His records must be valid enough that the Sorevin packet, guild references, and Registry all support the cover.

Arrek and Maya:

Maya's attempt to understand "Arrek" relies on real Arrek's records. The records seem valid, which makes Davian's deception harder for Maya and Aldira to explain.

- Arrek is not a fake person invented in the packet.

## Proposed Canon Core

- Arrek Hadrin is a twenty-six-year-old Low Clade guild-licensed glyphwright/contractor with brown hair, average height, and about fourteen years of experience including apprenticeship.
- Arrek is scheduled for the Rezin citadel ward-overhaul project through the Sorevin contractor network.
- Arrek has valid guild/Sorevin/Registry records and is not knowingly part of the Harbinger/Concord plot.
- He has no surviving primary relatives; his mother is deceased, his father is unlisted, and two cousins are debt-contracted to Jorra Halvek.
- Arrek is precarious but not especially vulnerable by Sorevin vetting standards.
- Harvick's first-pass impression of the real Arrek is: moody, seems like a nice guy, good at hitting things with a sword but not trained enough to know what he is doing.
- Davian and the Concord track Arrek to a bar and follow him into an alley.
- Kelyra pressures Davian to kill Arrek by framing him as a High-Clade collaborator and all Clade collaborators as evil.
- Davian murders Arrek in the alley while simultaneously using Harbinger Ink to capture/steal/mimic Arrek's aura.
- Davian uses Arrek's aura and name as the Rezin-citadel cover.
- Arrek remains dead in Book 1. The later "Arrek" is Davian under cover.

## Proposed Developmental Profile

Arrek should be handled as a small but morally important absence.

He does not need many scenes, but his ordinary life should be specific enough that the reader can feel Davian killed an actual person. The strongest version is not "Arrek was secretly noble" or "Arrek was secretly terrible." It is that he was a rough, flawed, ordinary worker trying to survive and help family, and Davian killed him because the mission needed a body and a name.

This supports Davian's arc from negation/destruction toward responsibility without making Arrek carry a separate subplot.

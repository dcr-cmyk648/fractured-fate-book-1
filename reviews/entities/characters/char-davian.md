# Entity Review: Davian / Arrek Identity

## Proposed identity

- Davian is a principal character and POV figure tied to the Twilight Concord, Jalen, Kelyra, the Shade persona, Antithesis, Ink Boons, the Harbingers/Syndicate, the theft of the Rezin Heart, emerging Shroud/Subversion ability, and the Maya/Davian romance and trust arc.
- "Arrek" is Davian's Rezin-citadel cover identity, enabled by an Ink Boon that lets him steal or mimic another person's aura. The real Arrek remains a separate queued entity because Davian murdered and replaced him.
- Davian is Jalen and Kelyra's son. Current manuscript also places Kael and Lirien in his family home.

## Manuscript-established information

- Davian operates with the Twilight Concord during the prologue attack. He works beside his father Jalen, uses sign language, wears a black Concord mask marked with five bright scratches for magi he has killed, and carries Antithesis. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:273-336`, Prologue, paragraphs 116-137.
- Davian wants to be seen as dangerous. Jalen challenges his marked mask as attention-seeking rather than tactical diversion. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:285-300`, Prologue, paragraphs 120-125.
- Davian has hereditary magical sensitivity: he feels a spell-trap before it triggers. He uses Antithesis against a Low Clade magus, suffers backlash, and breaks the magus's Shroud through rage, his own facial sigil, and Antithesis. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:393-429`, `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:570-617`, Prologue, paragraphs 177-194 and 287-311.
- Davian sees Maya on the wall during the prologue after her destructive magic affects his combat. He stares at her before Jalen pulls him back into motion. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:537-543`, `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:609-627`, Prologue, paragraphs 261-327; `revision/prologue/maya-shroud-attack.md`.
- Jalen criticizes Davian for reckless pride after the Low Clade magus fight and says he should have accepted the Boon. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:933-978`, Chapter 2, paragraphs 527-542.
- Davian and Jalen report to the Syndicate/Harbingers after the prologue raid. The organization changes the job from stealing documents to infiltrating the Rezin citadel and taking the Heart. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1089`, Chapter 2, paragraphs 544-579.
- Davian volunteers to take the Heart job and to kill Niall because he fears Jalen will be sent to die and believes making noise/action can make him matter. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1062-1089`, Chapter 2, paragraphs 570-579.
- The Harbinger/Syndicate leader gives Davian two Ink Boons: one intended as a mimic's mask to borrow a face/name/aura, and one that modifies Antithesis. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1092-1167`, Chapter 2, paragraphs 581-607.
- After the ritual, Antithesis has become a small workman's knife. Jalen says the second Boon can return it to its former size once, but not switch it back afterward. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1223-1260`, Chapter 2, paragraphs 625-636.
- Davian's family home includes Kelyra, Jalen, Kael, and Lirien. Kelyra learns the Heart mission and pivots quickly into planning while criticizing Jalen and Davian's idiocy. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1707-1788`, Chapter 3, paragraphs 800-819.
- In later manuscript material, Maya/Aldira question Davian after the Heart theft. Davian says Harbinger Ink let him steal someone else's aura, and he admits he killed Arrek to get it. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20185-20230`, later chapter interrogation, paragraphs 7047-7062.
- Davian claims Kelyra called their magic Subversion. He describes draining zan defenses and, during the Treasury fight, pulling energy around the vault sphere, converting/releasing it, suffering backlash that shattered both arms, and surviving because the Ink healed him. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20428-20452`, training/interrogation material, paragraphs 7128-7136.
- Davian is capable of manifesting a Shroud but it is weak and unstable. Harvick and Maya drill him after the theft; his hand-to-hand skill is strong, knife skill is okay, sword/magic/Shroud are weaker, and Maya identifies Shroud improvement as the path that lets him use martial arts and magic together. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21080-21695`, Chapter 36/37 material, paragraphs 7379-7554.
- Davian tells Selwin that his name is Davian, not Arrek, apologizes for lying, and says he thought the Rezins were his enemies but now wants to help make things right. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21611-21635`, Chapter 36/37 material, paragraphs 7527-7536.
- Davian signs with Zirene during the creation of lethal control bands, using Concord sign. He agrees because he needs to prove himself. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19580-19610`, later material, paragraphs 6867-6875.
- Davian is affected by attraction to Maya and by class resentment. He apologizes when he realizes he misunderstood assumptions about Maya's mother and heredity; he also connects Maya's status/mask to the broader Clade system. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19700-19970`, later material, paragraphs 6920-6944 and surrounding scene.

## Consistent information

- Davian is driven by family loyalty, rage at the Clade system, and a need to matter through action.
- Davian is competent as an assassin/fighter but not yet fully competent as a magus.
- Davian's self-image is tied to being dangerous: the Shade mask, magus kill marks, Antithesis, and later his desire to be useful to Maya all orbit that pattern.
- Davian's relationship with Jalen is loving but sharp, built around shared violence, grief, tactical discipline, and mutual fear of loss.
- Davian's relationship with Kelyra is central to his mission and should be reviewed with `char-kelyra`; she is his mother and appears to have Archeon lineage.
- Davian's romance/trust arc with Maya is central but should distinguish attraction, guilt, betrayal, class resentment, and eventual shared values.
- Davian's abilities appear to bridge Subversion, Archeon heritage, Ink, bodily/martial training, and Shroud manifestation.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Organization name: Syndicate vs Harbingers

- Version A: Current early manuscript repeatedly uses `Syndicate` for the organization giving jobs and Ink Boons. Source: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1167`.
- Version B: Later manuscript/planning and entity inventory use `Harbingers`, sometimes with `Syndicate` grouped as a possible alias or rename. Sources: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21703-21739`, `ENTITY_INDEX.md`.
- Consequence: Davian's accepted file can mention `Harbingers/Syndicate` provisionally, but the organization name should be resolved in `org-harbingers-syndicate`.
- Recommendation: Do not decide the final name in Davian's file.

### Arrek identity vs Arrek person

- Version A: `char-davian` includes Davian's use of the Arrek cover identity because it is central to his arc.
- Version B: `char-arrek` remains a separate queued entity because Arrek is also the murdered glyphwright/worker whose aura, name, and life Davian steals.
- Consequence: Davian's file should describe the cover identity and murder, while Arrek's file should preserve the separate person's background and consequences.
- Recommendation: Keep both records separate.

### Degree of coercion vs choice

- Version A: Davian repeatedly frames his actions as not having a choice, and the Harbinger/Ink material supports coercion, manipulation, and intrusive or fate-like pressure.
- Version B: Maya challenges him that he did have a choice and could have trusted her before the theft. Planning also says he must learn to distance himself from thoughts that tell him there is no choice.
- Consequence: Accepted file should not flatten this into either pure victimhood or pure culpability.
- Recommendation: Store as an unresolved tension: Davian was coerced and manipulated, but the story treats his agency and choices as morally important.

### Ink, Shroud, and Subversion mechanics

- Version A: Current manuscript shows Davian had Ink, can raise a weak Shroud, uses Subversion-like energy manipulation, and is healed by Ink backlash.
- Version B: Bullet notes keep mechanics unsettled, including whether Ink must precede Shroud manifestation and whether Ink-adjacent abilities interrupt Shroud.
- Consequence: Davian's accepted file should record observed abilities without finalizing the broader magic system.
- Recommendation: Route mechanics to `magic-ink-boons`, `magic-shroud`, `magic-subversion`, and `org-archeon`.

### Fate of late-book death/resurrection/confession beats

- Version A: Bullet notes include Davian dying, returning, smiling at Maya, and saying what he wanted to say.
- Version B: These are future-scene candidates, not current manuscript facts.
- Consequence: Keep out of accepted Davian canon except as candidate arc material.

## Proposed accepted record

Proposal only:

- Davian is a principal POV character, son of Jalen and Kelyra, associated with the Twilight Concord and the Shade persona.
- He is a skilled assassin/fighter who uses sign language, masks, Antithesis, and underclass/Concord codes.
- His early identity is built around being dangerous and being seen as a threat; Jalen explicitly challenges this in the prologue.
- He volunteers for the Heart theft to protect Jalen and his family, under Harbinger/Syndicate coercion, and receives Ink Boons that let him steal/mimic Arrek's aura and alter Antithesis.
- Davian murders Arrek and uses his aura/name as a cover identity inside the Rezin citadel.
- Davian steals the Heart, attacks Niall, injures Harvick, is exposed to Maya, escapes, and later returns or is held under conditions where the Rezins question and train him.
- Davian has weak but real Shroud manifestation, Subversion-related magical ability from Kelyra/Archeon lineage, strong hand-to-hand skill, and unstable Ink exposure.
- Davian is morally responsible for his choices, but those choices are shaped by family loyalty, coercion, grief, underclass rage, and Ink/Harbinger manipulation.
- Davian and Maya's relationship involves attraction, class resentment, misinterpretation, betrayal, guilt, and shared values around protecting innocents.
- Davian has heard racist/cultural claims about Naeroth demi-humans and may wonder whether his attraction to Maya is unnatural or magically forced; this should be stored as his misconception, not canon truth.

## Proposed planning or arc record

- Store Davian's arc as moving from needing to be the threat toward supporting Maya/Niall and acting according to values rather than rage or compulsive control.
- Store his "no choice" / Ink-thoughts material as a psychological and magic-system arc: he must learn to notice coercive/internal pressure without treating it as destiny.
- Link Davian's arc to `thread-davian-identity-guilt`, `thread-maya-davian-romance`, `object-antithesis`, `object-davian-mask`, `magic-ink-boons`, `magic-subversion`, `magic-shroud`, `org-harbingers-syndicate`, `org-archeon`, `char-jalen`, `char-kelyra`, and `char-arrek`.
- Keep the late Vorren/support role and death/return/confession beats in candidate/arc material pending later plot review.

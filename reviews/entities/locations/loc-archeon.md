# Entity Review: Archeon Location

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `loc-archeon` as the physical Archeon-controlled location: its citadel, city-wedge or territory, compound, arena/training spaces, child-training facilities, and raid/rescue geography.

It should not own:

- `org-archeon`: Archeon as a Clade, Serathis's leadership, bloodline program, release-and-catch system, public branding, and true power stack
- `magic-subversion`: kinesis mechanics and Kelyra's `Subversion` terminology
- `magic-ink-boons`: Ink Pact mechanics and Elric marking
- `char-serathis`: Serathis's personal history, motives, and leadership psychology
- `char-kelyra`: Kelyra's Archeon trauma, escape, Concord role, Shroud block, and family arc
- `thread-high-clade-politics`: Archeon/Drakhal/Rezin political consequences
- `thread-kelyra-archeon-recovery`: Kelyra's capture, rescue, and recovery arc

## Established constraints from approved records

- Archeon is currently a High Clade because it holds one of the six citadels that form the core of a Six Cities city-wedge. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`; `bible/organizations/org-high-clades.md`.
- Archeon was formerly a Low Clade and rose by killing off the prior occupying High Clade and taking the citadel. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`.
- Serathis is Archeon's current Head Magus and leader; most current Archeon members descend from her controlled bloodline project. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`.
- Archeon uses brutal child-training programs and dark breeding programs; children born to Archeon mothers are taken at birth, handed to training halls, returned to the compound around five years old, and trained rather than raised. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`.
- Exact Archeon location, citadel layout, and raid geography were deferred to this review. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`; `planning/candidates/organizations/org-archeon.md`.

## Manuscript-established information

- Late manuscript material treats Archeon as another High Clade citadel that Maya cannot simply storm without a plan, especially while Niall is weakened. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20548`.
- Kelyra's captivity scene occurs in a dim arena cut deep into stone. It has a wide sand-filled pit, iron rings in the walls, torches rather than zänlight, iron grates that open into the pit, and a stone lip from which Serathis watches. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20650-20710`.
- The arena is used as a controlled testing/training/punishment space. Serathis sends waves of fighters into the pit, evaluates Kelyra as an experiment, and later sends Lethira into the arena. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20650-20890`.
- Kelyra's memory states that Archeon children are taken at birth, handed to training halls, returned to the compound around five years old, and shaped with the rest. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20755-20770`; `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`.
- After the arena fight, Kelyra wakes chained against a stone wall in a dim torchlit chamber. It has wall rings, iron cuffs, a narrow passage, rough stone, and enough proximity to the arena/corridor complex that a healer and Serathis move through it easily. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20898-20925`.
- Drakhal representatives can enter the Archeon citadel interior and find Serathis "down here." The scene places Evana, Caius, guards, Archeon magi, and Kelyra in a chamber/corridor network that is underground or below the formal public level. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21000-21110`.
- Serathis explicitly calls the place "my citadel" when Drakhal interferes. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21069`.
- Archeon magi stand at corridor ends wearing gauntlets, and they can close or part access through the corridor. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21090-21105`.
- Earlier manuscript combat at the Concord manor shows multiple un-Presented Archeon magi deployed outside their citadel, but dialogue implies this is unusual: some are not let out much, hidden so outsiders do not realize Archeon has un-Presented magi "lying around," and Serathis controls who gets Presented. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17940-18240`.
- The Rezin citadel discussion establishes a general citadel model: High Clade citadels are massive, with many courtyards, old war-staging spaces, towers, bridges, covered walks, and original design capacity tied to smaller historical city-wedge populations. This can inform Archeon only at the general High Clade citadel level, not as direct Archeon architecture. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21450-21495`; `bible/organizations/org-high-clades.md`.

## Consistent information

- Archeon as a location can be treated as a High Clade citadel plus surrounding city-wedge/territory, with the citadel as the dominant physical and political core.
- The arena, training halls, compound, stone chambers, corridors, and "breeding pits" language all point to a harsh internal complex where Archeon trains, tests, imprisons, and breeds descendants.
- The captivity sequence appears to happen inside Archeon's own citadel, likely below the formal/public levels.
- The slums/boundary approach works with approved Six Cities geography: areas away from Spoke roads and between city-wedges are poorer and less supported.
- Archeon's secrecy is physical as well as political: it can keep un-Presented magi, children, and sensitive training infrastructure hidden inside its citadel.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Archeon citadel vs central tower

- Version A: older planning says Archeon was "obviously" in the central tower during an attack setup.
- Version B: approved High Clade geography has six city-wedge citadels around the Six Cities, with the central Spire/amphitheater as a separate shared place. Archeon is one of the six High Clades and should have its own citadel/city-wedge.
- Likely resolution: treat the central-tower version as obsolete or as confusion with the central Spire/revision leftovers, unless the author wants a specific Archeon tower inside its own citadel.

### Public High Clade citadel vs hidden brutal interior

- Version A: as a High Clade, Archeon has a citadel and appears in Presentation politics with formal delegation, colors, and status concerns.
- Version B: Kelyra's captivity material shows a dim underground/stone arena, chains, training halls, torchlight, hidden un-Presented magi, and "breeding pits" language.
- Likely resolution: accept both by distinguishing public-facing citadel levels from concealed internal/underground facilities.

### Far route through slums vs direct citadel access

- Version A: planning says Maya/Niall walk through slums to attack Archeon.
- Version B: High Clade citadels are core city-wedge seats and may have formal approaches, walls, or multiple wall systems.
- Likely resolution: the final attack can approach through neglected city-wedge boundary/slum districts before reaching Archeon-controlled territory or back access; exact map remains deferred.

### Arena identity

- Version A: High Clade disputes were historically settled in a central arena, now represented by the shared Spire/central amphitheater.
- Version B: Kelyra fights in an Archeon arena/pit inside the Archeon complex.
- Likely resolution: these are separate arenas: the shared central amphitheater is political/ceremonial, while Archeon's arena is an internal training/testing pit.

## Proposed accepted record

### present-canon

- Archeon controls one of the six High Clade citadels and its attached city-wedge / legally distinct city in the Six Cities.
- Archeon's citadel was seized from a prior occupying High Clade when Archeon rose from Low Clade status to High Clade status.
- The Archeon citadel is the physical center of Serathis's power and contains concealed internal spaces for training, testing, imprisonment, and bloodline/breeding control.
- Kelyra's Archeon captivity sequence occurs inside the Archeon citadel, likely in a lower or underground complex rather than a public formal level.
- The internal arena is a dim stone training/testing pit with a sand floor, iron rings, grates, torches, and a raised stone lip where Serathis can observe.
- The citadel/compound includes training halls and communal child-training infrastructure. Archeon children are taken at birth, trained institutionally, and returned to the compound around age five.
- The citadel includes rough stone chambers, corridors, wall rings, chains, guards, healers, messenger routes, and controlled corridor access.
- Archeon keeps much of its internal life hidden. Un-Presented Archeon magi and sensitive training/breeding systems can be kept inside and out of public view.
- The surrounding Archeon approach can pass through neglected/slum or city-wedge-boundary districts, especially away from the Spoke roads.
- The shared central Spire/amphitheater and the Archeon internal arena are separate locations.

### accepted-future-plan

- A late-book Archeon rescue/attack sequence will involve the Archeon citadel or its controlled territory.
- Exact raid geography remains unresolved, but current planning includes Niall/Maya attacking above ground while Davian/Harvick may use an underground route.

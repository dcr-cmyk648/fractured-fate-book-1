# Entity Review: Concord Manor

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `loc-concord-manor` as the physical Concord manor / hideout: district setting, exterior, courtyard, central hall, guarded grounds, maze-like additions, family/resistance use, and escape/security features.

It should not own:

- `org-concord`: Concord identity, leadership, Trial of Dominion, masks/sign/cipher, or operations culture
- `char-kelyra`: Kelyra's leadership, Archeon history, family role, or Trial arc
- `char-jalen`: Jalen's support/planning/logistics role
- `thread-kelyra-archeon-recovery`: future Archeon/Concord recovery arc
- `revision/concord-chronology`: older Jalen-seat/Kelyra-role manuscript cleanup

## Established constraints from approved records

- The formal organization is the Twilight Concord; `Concord` is the short form. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-concord.md`.
- Kelyra is the Concord's de facto primary leader. Jalen is a respected support, planning, and logistics figure, not the Concord's martial or political leader. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-concord.md`; `bible/characters/char-kelyra.md`; `bible/characters/char-jalen.md`.
- The Concord operates from a patched, dilapidated former Low Clade manor or hideout in a grim district. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-concord.md`.
- The manor includes a courtyard with training dummies, repairs, guards or members, and muted activity. Its central hall is a repurposed dining area used for planning. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-concord.md`.
- The hideout has maze-like security and escape features: grafted rooms, corridors, old stone, newer timber, and questionable structural integrity. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-concord.md`.
- Older Jalen-seat and Jalen-Trial material is suspect and should not drive this location review except as manuscript-revision context. Provenance: `CONSISTENCY_QUEUE.md`; decisions `0010` and `0016`.

## Manuscript-established information

- The current manuscript calls it the Concord's manor. It has gates, a mismatched weathered facade, old stone walls scarred by past battles, newer wooden additions, and visible decay. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1704-1746`.
- It was once the home of a Low Clade and was abandoned during some unknown internecine conflict before the Concord repurposed it. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1704`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2998-3016`.
- It stands in a grim district surrounded by ramshackle buildings, smoke, alleys, and low visibility. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1704`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3010-3016`.
- The grounds include a gated courtyard with uneven cobblestones, makeshift training dummies, members loitering or training, repair work, muted conversations, and guards or members watching activity. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1707-1731`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3010-3028`.
- Kelyra appears in the manor doorway and directs Davian, Jalen, Kael, and Lirien. The manor functions as both family home and Concord operational space. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1731-1746`.
- The central hall is a former dining area repurposed into a meeting/planning space. It is sparsely furnished, with a long table, mismatched chairs, cracked stone walls, and patched plaster. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1746-1848`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3031-3058`.
- A small shrine to Selina stands in a corner of the central hall, with fresh flowers. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1845`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3001`, `3055`.
- After Davian returns broken with the Heart, Kelyra, Jalen, and Concord members gather in the courtyard. The courtyard can be torchlit in emergencies, though flame outside at night is normally avoided because it draws attention. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16370-16505`.
- That same return scene references a jagged/shattered gate and Kelyra's intent to move locations immediately because the hideout may have been compromised. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16444-16505`.
- Later manuscript material calls it the Concord's hideout and an abandoned house expanded repeatedly over years. Rooms have been grafted onto rooms; corridors have been narrowed or widened based on scavenged materials; old stone meets newer timber; load-bearing beams are questionable; and the structure has poor integrity. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18151-18160`.
- During the Archeon attack/escape, Davian uses memory of the maze-like interior, exits, adjoining rooms, back window, narrow dark corridor between the rear of the manor and the outer wall, roof, outer wall, courtyard, and alleys around the hideout. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17960-18225`.
- The house can partially collapse under Archeon attack; roof sections, interior rooms, and beams are unstable. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18151-18225`.

## Consistent information

- `Manor`, `hideout`, and `house` can all describe the same place from different viewpoints: a former Low Clade manor now used as the Concord's guarded hideout and family base.
- The location works as a hybrid domestic/operational space: Kelyra and Jalen's family lives there, Concord members guard/train/repair there, and central planning happens there.
- The manor's history as former Low Clade property supports the Concord's scavenged, patched, and politically precarious status.
- The courtyard, central hall, Selina shrine, gates, outer wall, back routes, and maze-like additions all fit one compound.
- Questionable structural integrity supports later Archeon attack damage and Davian's escape sequence.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Concord manor vs hideout vs abandoned house

- Version A: early Chapter 4 material calls it the Concord's manor and describes a former Low Clade manor with gates, facade, courtyard, and central hall.
- Version B: later attack/escape material calls it the Concord's hideout or abandoned house, emphasizing ad hoc additions, maze-like structure, and collapse risk.
- Likely resolution: treat these as the same compound unless the author wants multiple Concord sites.

### Family home vs organizational base

- Version A: Kael, Lirien, Davian, Jalen, and Kelyra appear to live there or use it as home.
- Version B: Concord members, guards, training, repairs, operations, and planning all happen there.
- Likely resolution: accept it as both a family base and Concord operational hideout, with domestic rooms embedded in a guarded compound.

### Stable hideout vs compromised / destroyed location

- Version A: early material treats the manor as active base.
- Version B: after Davian returns with the Heart, Kelyra wants to move immediately because they may have been followed or exposed; later Archeon attack damages/collapses parts of the structure.
- Likely resolution: active base at the relevant manuscript point, later compromised and partially damaged/destroyed.

## Accepted record

### present-canon

- The Concord manor is the Twilight Concord's primary hideout/base at the relevant Book 1 point.
- It is also Kelyra and Jalen's family home/base.
- Concord members and any family they have live there communally, likely including younger people beyond those named so far.
- It is a former Low Clade manor or fortified house in a grim district of ramshackle buildings, smoke, alleys, and surveillance risk.
- The manor was abandoned after an unknown Low Clade conflict and later repurposed by the Concord. Exact history may be unknown in-world.
- It is mismatched, weathered, patched, and dilapidated: old stone, scarred walls, newer wooden additions, repairs, decay, and questionable structural integrity.
- It has gates, an outer wall or enclosed grounds, and a courtyard with uneven cobblestones, training dummies, Concord members/guards, repairs, and muted activity.
- It contains a central hall, formerly a dining area, now used as a planning/meeting space with a long table, mismatched chairs, cracked walls, and patched plaster.
- Selina's shrine stands in the family meeting space.
- The manor/hideout has maze-like security and escape features built from years of expansions and scavenged materials.
- After Davian returns with the Heart, Kelyra is worried the location is compromised. She does not have a solid plan beyond going to the Harbingers and hoping for more power.
- When Kelyra is captured, Karra immediately moves everyone but Davian out.
- The eventual collapse of the manor during the Archeon attack is canon.

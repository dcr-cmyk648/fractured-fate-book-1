# Entity Review: Spire / Presentation Spire

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `loc-spire` as the shared High Clade Presentation / central meeting location in the Six Cities: the public ceremonial and political place where Maya's Presentation occurs and where later High Clade meetings can be convened.

The first boundary issue is separating two entities that older notes and manuscript language can blur:

- the Presentation Spire / Central Spire location used for High Clade Presentation and cross-Clade ceremony
- the Rezin citadel's ordinary central architectural spire, which is already covered by `loc-rezin-citadel`

This file should not own:

- `loc-rezin-citadel`: the Rezin citadel's central architectural spire, Treasury, Registry, old tunnels, or family spaces
- `org-high-clades`: the political definition of High Clade status and governance
- `culture-magus-presentation`: ritual standards, Presented/unPresented status, fashion, speeches, and Shroud expectations
- `thread-high-clade-politics`: Drakhal/Rezin/Archeon political arc and the later meeting's political consequences
- `culture-clade-hierarchy`: caste system, city-wedge governance, and Low/Masked Clade structures

## Established constraints from approved records

- `loc-spire` should focus on the separate Presentation Spire / Presentation location, not the Rezin citadel's central architectural spire.
- The Rezin citadel has a central spire as an architectural feature, but it is not a named or special location by itself. Provenance: `bible/locations/loc-rezin-citadel.md`; decision `0025`.
- Approved High Clade material says the Central Spire is in the center of the Six Cities, but `Spire` is now a historical reference rather than an accurate current description. The accepted direction is an amphitheater built into a blast hole left by a long-ago cataclysm during the High Clade wars. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-high-clades.md`; decision `0014`.
- `CONSISTENCY_QUEUE.md` item `CQ-0019` requires this location review to preserve that the Presentation Spire is separate from the Rezin citadel's ordinary central spire and decide exact public terminology for the Presentation location.

## Manuscript-established information

- Maya sees a tall spire visible in the distance before leaving the Rezin citadel for Presentation. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1278`.
- The procession travels down a wide Spoke road. Other High Clade citadel spires are visible at the edges of the hexagonal city's perimeter. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1317-1338`.
- Current prose calls the Presentation site the Central Spire: a gleaming stone-and-magic pillar, shared domain of all six High Clades, with smooth polished walls and ancient zä. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1338-1368`.
- The site has a checkpoint at the edge of a plaza, arches inlaid with zä, High Clade guards, a cleaner/orderly plaza, merchants/stalls, banners, and an entrance with massive double doors. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1342-1365`.
- Current prose places envoys and representatives inside grand halls and corridors, including Archeon/Cryndor whispering and a Drakhal meeting on the way to the chamber. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1368-1422`.
- The Presentation chamber in current prose is circular, immense, and designed to inspire awe. It has a central raised dais engraved with High Clade emblems and six boxes arranged in a hexagonal pattern corresponding to the six citadels. The floor mirrors the city, with Spoke roads and landmark/crossroads engravings radiating from the dais. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1422-1449`.
- The chamber has zä that respond to the mediator's presence, and Maya perceives power/heat in the stone below the dais during her uncontrolled display. Her fire column shoots upward through an open crown of the Spire. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1491-1596`.
- Later current prose uses the same location for a High Clade meeting. A coded report says the Drakhals called a meeting of the High Clades at the Central Spire. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:14003-14063`.
- In that later meeting sequence, Maya and Niall approach gates carved with every High Clade icon, pass a half-empty market/plaza, leave most escort outside, enter the Spire, and descend steps into a pale-stone circle sunk into the conflux between cities. Maya explicitly thinks the place is named for what had been rather than what was. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:14180-14200`.
- The later meeting setting is an arena/amphitheater-like space with circular tiers, a raised stone dais at the heart, and every High Clade represented around it. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:14192-14235`.

## Outline and planning information

- Early outline material describes the city as hexagonal, with a grand spire in the center as a shared High Clade symbol. It says Maya arrives at the Spire, moves deeper through it, and takes the stage in the grand chamber atop the spire. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3220-3328`.
- Later planning explicitly revises this: "no Spire, it was destroyed, it's an arena now," while people still call the central area the Spire for historical reasons. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:4039`.
- Later planning repeats that the central spire is gone; each High Clade runs a mini city-state but focuses primarily on its citadel, and the arena is in the middle. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:5271`.
- Later notes still use "central spire" casually for a later meeting, likely as inherited terminology rather than settled physical description. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:5545`.

## Consistent information

- The location is a shared High Clade domain, not owned by Rezin or any other single Clade.
- It sits at the center of the Six Cities / city-wedge system and functions as symbolic neutral ground.
- The location supports Presentation ceremonies and later cross-Clade meetings.
- A central dais/stage, circular/arena geometry, High Clade seating/boxes/tiers, and hexagonal city symbolism fit both the current manuscript and the accepted amphitheater direction.
- Spoke roads, checkpoints/gates, High Clade iconography, banners, guards, and controlled plaza/market approaches can survive the amphitheater revision.
- The name `Spire` can plausibly survive as historical/common speech even if the literal tower is gone.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Literal tower vs destroyed/historical Spire

- Version A: current Presentation prose depicts a literal gleaming tower with grand doors, vaulted halls, corridors, and an open crown high enough for Maya's flame to lance into the sky.
- Version B: accepted High Clade direction says the Central Spire is now an amphitheater built into a blast hole, with `Spire` as historical terminology rather than accurate description.
- Likely resolution: reject the literal intact tower as final physical canon, but preserve the public/historical `Spire` term if desired. The location becomes an amphitheater/arena in the central blast-hole site.

### Grand chamber atop the spire vs sunken arena

- Version A: early outline says Maya takes the stage in a grand chamber atop the spire.
- Version B: later manuscript and approved direction already trend toward descending into a pale-stone circle/arena sunk into the conflux between cities.
- Likely resolution: the Presentation stage/dais should be inside or at the center of the amphitheater/blast-hole structure, not at the top of a literal tower.

### Central Spire vs Rezin central spire

- Version A: the Rezin citadel has a central/main spire where the Treasury, old tunnels, Niall spaces, and internal citadel routes appear.
- Version B: High Clade Presentation material also uses Central Spire for the shared city-center location.
- Resolution from prior approvals: these are separate locations. `loc-spire` should not absorb Rezin citadel spire details.

### Six citadels vs seven citadels

- Current approved High Clade structure has six canonical High Clades and six citadels / city-wedges. Later manuscript uses "seven citadels" once in a city-view line.
- Likely resolution: treat "seven citadels" as suspect manuscript cleanup unless later world geography deliberately adds a non-High-Clade citadel.
- Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-high-clades.md`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17785`.

## Proposed accepted record

### present-canon

- The Spire / Presentation location is the shared central High Clade ceremonial and meeting place in the Six Cities.
- It is separate from the Rezin citadel's ordinary central architectural spire.
- It is not currently an intact literal tower. `Spire` is historical terminology for a central site whose original spire was destroyed.
- The current physical form is an amphitheater or arena built into a blast hole left by a long-ago High Clade war cataclysm.
- The location remains symbolically central: it sits at the conflux/center of the Six Cities or city-wedges and functions as neutral/shared High Clade ground.
- The site is used for Maya's Presentation and later High Clade meetings.
- Access is controlled by High Clade gates/checkpoints, guards, and ceremonial/public plaza space.
- The main ceremonial area is circular or amphitheater-like, with a central dais/stage and High Clade seating arranged around it.
- Six High Clade sections/boxes/tiers correspond to the six High Clades/citadels/city-wedges.
- The floor/dais may retain zä, emblems, and map/symbolic geometry tied to the Six Cities.
- Common speech may still call the location the Spire or Central Spire because of historical continuity, even though the physical spire is gone.

# Entity Review: Alara's Journal / Memory Journal

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `object-alara-journal` as Alara Rezin's specific auratic codex: the physical book Maya finds, hides, uses, and loses to Niall, plus the object's discovery site, key inscription, corruption, and Book 1 scene sequence.

It should not own:

- `magic-codex-memory`: general auratic codex mechanics
- `char-alara`: Alara's full biography, personality, abilities, and death
- `event-alara-death-war-memory`: exact Alara death and war-memory sequence
- `char-niall`: Niall's grief arc and any separate Niall codex
- `char-elric` / `magic-ink-boons` / `magic-fateweaving-skein`: exact Elric persistence, Ink, and Skein mechanics
- `loc-rezin-citadel`: full old-tunnel and hidden-chamber map

## Established constraints from approved records

- `Auratic codex` is the accepted technical term for the memory-journal magic category. Codices store immersive experiential memories accessed by key incantation plus aura/tether interaction. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-codex-memory.md`; `decisions/0024-auratic-codex-memory-mechanics.md`.
- Codices do not store aura imprints by default. Elric tampered with Alara's codex to make it anchor an aura-like imprint and carry a corrupted key/bridge. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-codex-memory.md`.
- Elric's codex trap intentionally idealizes or distorts Maya's parents, reinforcing Maya's belief that she can fix impossible losses. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-codex-memory.md`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:679`.
- Maya is still developing as a fateweaver; codex/Skein exposure should not make her a fully trained fateweaver. Provenance: `bible/characters/char-maya.md`; `bible/magic/magic-fateweaving-skein.md`.
- The Rezin citadel has an ancient layer of old tunnels under the main spire, including the codex alcove and Elric's hidden chamber. Exact map and access details remain deferred. Provenance: `bible/locations/loc-rezin-citadel.md`.
- Niall has a codex connected to Alara that he cannot open again but cannot destroy; Niall showing Maya his codex to explain Alara's death remains candidate. Provenance: `bible/characters/char-niall.md`; `bible/magic/magic-codex-memory.md`.

## Source-backed findings

### Discovery and physical object

- Maya dreams of a woman she believes is her mother leading her through the citadel into older, darker tunnels. When Maya touches the figure, its face is an inky void. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2280-2331`; outline summary at `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2972-2988`.
- Maya retraces the dream path, orders her guards to stay behind, descends into old tunnels, and reaches a small alcove matching the dream. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2332-2391`; outline summary at `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2988`.
- At the alcove, Maya feels a presence saying, "I knew you would come"; a stone block slides forward and reveals a hidden hollow. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2391-2405`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2988`.
- Inside is a small aged leather-bound book, surprisingly supple, with `Alara Rezin` inscribed on the front cover. Maya reads this as her mother's journal. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2408-2417`.
- The pages initially appear as an indecipherable cipher. Maya tucks the journal into her robe, sees unexplained footprints in the dust that are not hers, returns to her chambers with her Shroud up, and hides the journal in her chest. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2418-2444`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2992`; comment context in `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.comments.md:1064-1082`.

### Key inscription and first uses

- Maya asks Alden how one would decrypt a theoretical encrypted journal. Alden identifies it as likely an auratic codex, explains key incantations, and warns that the memories can feel like living them. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3148-3183`; outline summary at `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:1197`.
- After Alden sends materials, Maya retrieves the journal from the chest at the foot of her bed. She sees a shimmering inscription inside the front cover and suspects it is the key incantation, while wondering whether it had been there before. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3498-3518`.
- Author comments state the key's appearance should be clearer: Maya should see or suspect Elric near her rooms before the key mysteriously appears, and the corrupted key is what allows Elric to get in. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.comments.md:925-950`.
- Current manuscript shows Maya using the key and faint zän on the parchment to enter vivid Alara memories: battlefield/war violence, a first charged meeting with Niall, and a later ball/garden proposal memory. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3513-3546`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:4218-4289`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6265-6463`.
- The journal is repeatedly hidden in Maya's quarters, first in the chest at the foot of her bed and later wrapped in cloth or beneath folded clothing. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3504`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:4218`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6283`.

### Corrupted bridge and Niall's seizure of the codex

- In the ball/garden proposal memory, Elric appears, freezes the memory, directly addresses Maya as his granddaughter, and exposes her to fate-thread/Skein perception. The codex holds Maya fast until the memory ruptures and the codex flies from her hands. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6451-6601`.
- Niall finds Maya after the rupture and holds up the codex; she sees Alara Rezin's name on the cover. One metal corner is dented from the fall, but Niall still cradles the codex reverently. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7245-7299`.
- Maya tells Niall she found it in the tunnels, hidden in a hollow in the stone, while omitting the dream. Niall says the memory with Elric present is false because Elric was not there that night. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7329-7374`.
- Alden examines the codex and finds the key incantation too long, in a different hand than the notes inside, structurally wrong, and layered with an overlay. He identifies it as a bridge for speaking through the codex. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7374-7419`.
- Alden says the overlay was not recent and was likely stabilized during the initial anchoring. Niall identifies Elric as the likely source or planner, seizes the codex, and leaves. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7482-7542`.

### Return to discovery site and broader chamber link

- Later Niall's workers locate the likely discovery wall after a slow search, finding an empty space behind an unusually thick wall; the wall has no obvious opening mechanism. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18859-18921`.
- Maya uses emerging Skein/fateweaving perception to pass through the sealed wall and then to make the passage accessible, allowing Niall, Harvick, and workers to perceive the tunnel. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18924-19020`.
- At the original alcove, Maya says it felt like someone wanted her to have the codex and that she thought it was her mother. Niall says he now understands and that it must have been the one who gave Alara the codex in the first place. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19063-19075`.
- The party continues past the alcove into Elric's hidden chamber, where ancient zanwork and an Ink simulacrum/remnant connect the codex discovery to Elric's broader plan. This packet should note the link but not own the whole chamber or Elric persistence mechanics. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19080-19324`.

## Likely accepted object record if approved

- Alara's journal is Alara Rezin's specific auratic codex.
- The object is a small aged leather-bound book with `Alara Rezin` on the cover, initially appearing to Maya as her mother's cryptic journal.
- Maya finds it in an old hidden alcove below the Rezin citadel after a dream/presence draws her there.
- The discovery setup is suspicious and Elric-linked, not a simple message from Alara.
- Maya hides it in her quarters, asks Alden about decryption, receives reference materials, and then finds or notices the key incantation.
- The key incantation is corrupted and tied to Elric's bridge/tampering.
- Maya uses the codex to experience vivid Alara memories and to encounter Elric's bridge.
- After the bridge incident, Niall takes the codex.
- The object should retain that the parent-memory trap idealizes or distorts Maya's parents, while the exact content and truth-value of individual Alara/Niall memories remain for later Alara, Niall, and event reviews.

- Current manuscript has Alden say codices anchor auras like the Registry, while the approved mechanics clarify that aura-imprint anchoring is not default and is special to Elric's tampering with Alara's codex.
- Current manuscript lets Maya wonder whether she merely missed the key inscription; author comments want the key's mysterious/corrupted appearance made more explicit.
- The ball/garden proposal memory includes Elric even though Niall says Elric was not there; approved magic record treats this as tampering/bridge, not a normal memory.

## Author decisions on 2026-06-25

1. Durable object name is `Alara's journal`; exact name does not materially matter. Technical type remains auratic codex.
2. This is Alara's specific auratic codex, separate from Niall's own codex / memory book. Elric developed the technology from his forays into fateweaving and provided journals to everyone known to have one: Alara, Niall, Harvick, and himself. He framed them as benign ways to preserve valued or important memories.
3. Maya's discovery sequence is accepted at the high level: dream with inky faceless figure, old tunnel alcove, presence/whisper, hidden stone hollow, and physical book. The book should probably say `Alara`, not `Alara Rezin`.
4. Alara does not guide Maya at all. Any perception that Alara is guiding Maya is part of Elric's trap. The corrupted key enables Elric's bridge.
5. Book 1 object function/sequence is accepted: Maya hides and uses the codex, experiences idealized/distorted Alara/Niall memories, triggers Elric's bridge, and Niall takes the codex afterward. Footprints, original maker/giver, exact memory truth, key-appearance staging, and whether it returns to Maya stay candidate/deferred.

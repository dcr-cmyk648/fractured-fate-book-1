# Entity Review: Heart / Seraph Heart

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `object-seraph-heart` as the Heart / Seraph Heart object: physical identity, containment, power source role, Treasury/Registry relationship, theft/removal consequences, Ink/Skein/fate implications, and object-specific future-plan material.

It should not own:

- `loc-treasury`: Treasury chamber placement, sealed entrance, gravity/weightlessness as a location effect
- `object-tzorrax-sphere`: the sphere/containment object as a separate artifact if kept separate
- `org-registry`: Registry organization, modes, and operational procedures
- `magic-zan-lattices`: zä lattice theory and Heart-powered inefficiency model
- `magic-fateweaving-skein`: broad Skein/fate mechanics
- `thread-davian-identity-guilt`: Davian's resurrection, death/return arc, and relationship consequences

## Established constraints from approved records

- The Treasury is high up in the Rezin citadel and contains the Tzorrax Heart/Sphere containment setup. Provenance: `bible/locations/loc-treasury.md`.
- Treasury weightlessness is a side effect of the Heart interacting with its containment armature; it is not operationally controlled by the Registry. Provenance: `bible/locations/loc-treasury.md`.
- Weightlessness ends when Davian shatters the sphere and removes the Heart. Provenance: `bible/locations/loc-treasury.md`.
- Major Rezin zä systems work but shrink/fail after the Heart is stolen because they were overbuilt around near-limitless input. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-zan-lattices.md`; `CONSISTENCY_QUEUE.md`.
- Davian's end-book resurrection is accepted future-plan material, but exact cost and Heart/Skein/reality mechanics remain unresolved. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-fateweaving-skein.md`; `CONSISTENCY_QUEUE.md`.

### Manuscript-present material

- The Harbingers/Syndicate escalate the Concord operation from contractor-record theft into stealing the Heart from the Rezin Treasury. The leader describes it as the beating Heart inside the citadel; Jalen says no one has even seen it and it might not exist. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1028-1095`.
- Davian studies the Treasury sphere during his contractor cover. The sphere is presented as the treasury/vault itself, suspended in a weightless chamber, with intricate redundant zä lattices on its surface. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3888-4029`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:4480-4555`.
- Current manuscript text still has Marek say the Registry can enable/disable the weightlessness and target individuals or objects. This conflicts with later approved author direction that the effect is a containment side effect produced by Heart/armature interaction, not Registry control. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3888-4029`; superseded by `bible/locations/loc-treasury.md`.
- Davian finds no usable sphere entrance. Tanelle notes that parts of the lattice appear to be holding energy in rather than pushing it out, and guesses that something inside the Sphere may be producing the weightless effect or that there may be an inner layer. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:13700-13840`.
- During the theft, Davian follows a dark thread, uses Antithesis and kinesis/Subversion to connect to the Sphere lattice, releases stored power, and hurls the Sphere at Niall. The backlash shatters both Davian's arms; Niall's lightning and/or the impact shatters the Sphere; gravity returns. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15920-16145`.
- After the Sphere shatters, Davian crawls through the wreckage to an inner core and removes the Heart: a perfectly dark sphere that pulls light into it, bends the world around its edges, and feels cool in a non-temperature way. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15920-16145`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17740-17865`.
- The broken inner core has intricate unfamiliar zä, including Nizän/negation-related designs, and functions as the immediate casing around the Heart. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15920-16145`.
- Maya arrives after the theft and sees the Treasury chamber empty, with the field dead, nothing floating, no containment, and no Sphere. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16200-16235`.
- After the theft, Maya assumes they may be able to get the Heart back and works on interim security while the Registry is down. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18770-18820`.
- Davian later remembers the Heart as alien, "like holding a piece of something that had never agreed to belong to this world," and remembers the theft as feeling inevitable under Ink/dark-thread pressure. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17740-17865`.
- Davian later explains that he felt the energy in the zä around the vault sphere, pulled/converted/released it, and threw the vault at Niall; the backlash shattered both arms and the Ink healed him. He also says the Ink told or showed him to stab Antithesis into the Sphere. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20380-20470`.
- Maya later sketches a repair direction for the damaged Registry: isolate passive detection around entrances and power it with zä sinks instead of the centralized feed. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20525-20555`.

## Proposed accepted canon

If approved, the durable object record should store:

- `object-seraph-heart` is the Heart: a physical dark sphere removed from the inner core of the Tzorrax/Treasury Sphere during Davian's theft.
- In the current manuscript era, the Heart is contained high in the Rezin Treasury inside a Sphere/armature system, with an inner core and specialized zä around it.
- The Heart is real but unseen enough that outsiders can treat it as rumor or myth before the theft.
- The Heart is a near-limitless power source that later Rezin infrastructure has been overbuilt around, especially the Registry's full scope and the Treasury/Sphere containment system.
- The Heart is not a normal Rezin treasure that can be safely used at will. Niall's planning-note direction says it was locked away so no one could use it, which fits the accepted containment model.
- Heart containment transforms or channels the Heart's reality-distorting energy; Treasury weightlessness is a side effect of Heart/armature interaction, not an intentional Registry toggle.
- Removing the Heart ends the weightless field and leaves the Treasury chamber without active containment.
- After theft, the Registry and other Heart-dependent Rezin systems are down, compromised, or radically reduced; Maya's repair direction is narrower passive/gateway detection powered through zä sinks rather than restoring full old functionality.
- The Heart's exact origin, divine/extraplanar identity, Nizän relationship, and final cosmological function remain unresolved.
- The Heart may be involved in Elric's fate sight, Ink/reality work, and Davian's resurrection/reality damage, but exact mechanics remain candidate or later endgame review material unless approved now.

## Candidate or deferred material

- Whether the Heart is literally a seraph's heart, a divine/extraplanar remnant, a battery-like object, a prison/key, or something else remains unresolved.

- Exact Heart-to-Registry power path, storage, discharge, and active-mode behavior remain unresolved.
- Exact Heart-to-Skein relationship remains unresolved. Candidate notes say uncovered Heart weakens the Skein/fabric of reality and may amplify Maya's later fateweaving damage.
- Exact Elric/Heart communion remains unresolved. Candidate notes say communion with the Heart may have enabled or intensified Elric's fate-thread sight.
- Exact role in Davian's end-book resurrection remains unresolved. Davian's resurrection is accepted, but the Heart's technical contribution, cost, and reality damage remain later endgame material.
- The object boundary between the Heart, Tzorrax Sphere, Treasury sphere, vault sphere, inner core, containment armature, and zä casing should be finalized with `object-tzorrax-sphere`.

## Source tensions or obsolete material

- Current manuscript says the Registry controls Treasury weightlessness and can target people or objects. This is obsolete under the approved Treasury decision.
- Current manuscript and notes still mix `Treasury Sphere`, `vault sphere`, `Tzorrax Sphere`, and `sphere`; exact final object terminology remains unresolved.
- Some source text uses older `glyph/jin/zan` terminology. Accepted technical records use `zä`, `zän`, and `zänwright`, with unaccented forms allowed in IDs and rough notes.

# Haloes Review Packet

## Source Summary

Haloes appear to be the ordinary coin/currency of the setting.

Current manuscript examples:

- Davian pays for the mask with `three copper-haloed gold coins`.
- Erynn jokes that Niall pays her `three silver haloes per joke`.
- Harvick flips a `gold halo`.
- Jalen pays a barkeep with a `silver halo`.

Planning notes add the one technical detail:

- Haloes have a zan carved into them.
- The zan can be activated to verify that the halo is real.
- Davian's mask vendor tests the coin when Davian pays.
- Davian gets paid in haloes.
- A Low Clade may make the haloes.

Unrelated uses of `halo` as a light/aura image also appear in the manuscript; those should not be treated as currency references.

## Likely Accepted Core

- Haloes are coins / currency.
- Denominations or materials include at least gold, silver, and copper-haloed gold.
- Authentic haloes have a zan carved into them.
- That zan can be activated as an anti-counterfeit or authenticity check.
- Davian and other characters use haloes in ordinary transactions.

## Candidate / Deferred Material

- Exact monetary system, denominations, values, exchange rates, and whether `copper-haloed gold` is a separate denomination or visual construction.
- Exact minting authority.
- Whether a specific Low Clade makes haloes.
- Exact zan design, activation method, and who can test authenticity.
- Whether the vendor coin test remains in the revised Davian-mask scene.
- Whether Haloes need an object record at all after this review, or should be folded into a later culture/economy/terminology record.

## Continuity Notes

- Davian's mask should stay in `object-davian-mask`; the coin note only overlaps because Davian pays for the mask with haloes.
- Broad zan terminology remains unresolved because of the later `zan`/`za` versus `zä`/`zän` terminology decision. This packet should preserve the coin-authentication concept without forcing project-wide terminology replacement.
- Exact Low Clade production/minting should be preserved as candidate material unless it materially affects class/world-system logic.

## Checkpoint Questions

1. Should `Haloes` be kept as a dedicated object/currency record, or folded into a later culture/economy/terminology file?
2. Are haloes ordinary coins with a zan authenticity mark that can be activated to test whether they are real?
3. Should gold, silver, and copper-haloed gold all remain accepted coin/material language for now, with exact values deferred?
4. Should the Low Clade that may make haloes remain candidate-only, unless later culture/economy review needs it?
5. Should the vendor testing Davian's payment for the mask remain candidate scene detail rather than accepted durable canon?

## Author Decisions

- Keep Haloes as a dedicated object/currency record.
- Haloes are coins with a precious-metal center and a cheaper-metal halo around the center.
- A center zan marks value from 1 to 6 within a metal tier and can be activated to verify authenticity.
- Values follow base 6: 6 copper = 1 silver, 6 silver = 1 gold.
- A 6-gold halo is worth 36 silver and can be called a Moon because it represents a 36-day month of normal wages.
- Normal low-level labor pays about 1 silver halo per day, or about 0.5 to 1 copper per hour.
- Fractional copper coins exist but are not haloed; only values worth testing receive the zan authenticity mark.
- Minting is controlled by an unspecified High Clade, not Archeon, Drakhal, or Rezin.
- A Low Clade proxy manages day-to-day minting operations.
- The vendor tests Davian's mask payment because Davian is giving him a huge amount of money.

## Approval Result

Approved. Accepted facts are stored in `bible/objects/object-haloes.md`; unresolved minting, physical-design, terminology, and scene-wording details are preserved in `planning/candidates/objects/object-haloes.md`.

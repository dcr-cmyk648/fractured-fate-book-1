# Entity Review: Shroud / Shrouds

## Proposed identity

The Shroud is the core magus defensive field/reflex. It presents as a faint visible distortion or barrier around the body, protects against physical and magical harm, participates in energy conversion/sublimation, and is socially central to High Clade status and Presentation.

It appears to be a distinct magical modality from hereditary magic, but one that must be integrated with hereditary magic, bodily awareness, training, and cognition for a magus to fight effectively.

## Manuscript-established information

### Defensive field and body reflex

- Maya describes the Shroud as a defensive reflex hooked into bodily awareness/proprioception: knowing where the body is, while still requiring focus to maintain under pressure. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21580-21655`, Chapter 36/37 training, paragraphs 7538-7553.
- Maya teaches Davian that a magus blocks with the Shroud, not hands, sword, or knife, so the body remains open to form a countering schema. Davian's ordinary defensive reflexes interfere with this. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21435`, Chapter 36 training, paragraphs 7379-7419.
- Davian experiences his Shroud as a defensive reflex he had to discover; under controlled conditions it can settle reasonably, but it remains weak and unstable. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20300-20445`, later interrogation/training, paragraphs 7087-7134; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21690`, Chapter 36/37 training, paragraphs 7379-7553.

### Protection and overlap

- Maya's Shroud appears as a second skin/protective barrier in the prologue; arrows strike it, freeze, and shatter. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:468-500`, Prologue, paragraphs 224-234.
- A Low Clade magus's Shroud blocks Davian's throwing knife and sword attacks until Davian breaks it with Antithesis, sigil flare, and rage. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:500-610`, Prologue, paragraphs 245-311.
- Maya can feel Davian's Shroud pressing against hers where their fields overlap. She uses her own Shroud, containment schema, redirection surfaces, and heat pathways to hold and redirect force. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:40-110`, Chapter 37 opening, paragraphs 12-34.
- Lethira's Shroud makes her speed and strikes physically dangerous beyond ordinary fighting; Kelyra cannot safely block wrong because a Shroud lies behind every strike. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20705-20850`, Chapter 35 Archeon arena, paragraphs 7227-7272.

### Energy handling and sublimation

- Maya says a Shroud tries to convert incoming energy into something aligned with the magus's hereditary magic; speed of thought matters because the magus must redirect the converted energy before it acts directly. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20300-20445`, later training, paragraphs 7116-7129.
- Maya calls this conversion `sublimation`: the Shroud converts incoming energy into something aligned with internal circuits. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20300-20445`, later training, paragraphs 7127-7129.
- During Niall's attack, Maya's Shroud catches lightning at full strength, widens under strain, and redirects sublimated backlash into flame; the scene demonstrates Shroud use under sustained magical assault. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19920-20095`, Chapter 34, paragraphs 6997-7017.
- Maya explains that unfocused energy is volatile and can tear through a person before they control it, which is why Clades force children to manifest a Shroud first. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20300-20445`, later training, paragraph 7129.

### Capacity, throughput, and overload

- Maya teaches that Shrouds have roughly the same capacity; the difference between magi is throughput, meaning how efficiently the magus sublimates energy building in the Shroud before being overwhelmed. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21690`, Chapter 36/37 training, paragraphs 7546-7550.
- Selwin repeats that enough energy delivered fast enough can overwhelm a Shroud before the magus can sublimate it away; Maya confirms this. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21690`, Chapter 36/37 training, paragraphs 7546-7550.
- Davian's Shroud can flicker apart under Harvick's training blows, while Maya identifies improving his Shroud as the only viable near-term path to make his martial arts useful. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21690`, Chapter 36/37 training, paragraphs 7379-7553.

### Training and manifestation pressure

- Maya remembers Niall's childhood pressure: "bring up your Shroud, or you will die." Her reflex appeared after fear, crying, frozen tears, and exhaustion; she is unwilling to put Selwin through the same pressure but worries it may have been necessary. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:40-65`, Chapter 37 opening, paragraph 12.
- Maya says High Clade parents focus on early Shroud manifestation because it protects against backlash and accidents. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19920-19965`, Chapter 33/34 transition, paragraph 6958.
- Selwin is still unable to find the Shroud reflex during training and asks why Davian can learn a Shroud if only someone whose parents are magi can. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21690`, Chapter 36/37 training, paragraphs 7526-7545.

### Social and High Clade role

- Maya's Presentation travel shows Shroud use as protective and status-marking; guards' Shrouds and Ironstriders mark High Clade privilege. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1288-1365`, Presentation approach, paragraphs 653-675.
- Current High Clade records treat Shrouds, lineage, Presentation, and hereditary magical status as foundations of legitimacy. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-high-clades.md`.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Stronger Shrouds versus throughput model

- Version A: characters and social language refer to "strong" Shrouds and modern magi having stronger Shrouds.
- Version B: Chapter 36/37 training states that Shrouds have roughly the same capacity and that the difference is throughput/sublimation efficiency.
- Source of A: general manuscript/social language; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.comments.md:358`; High Clade/Archeon public framing in `bible/organizations/org-archeon.md`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21155-21690`, paragraphs 7546-7550.
- Likely consequence: accepted record should probably treat "strong Shroud" as common shorthand for trained throughput, stability, persistence, and combat efficiency, not literal baseline capacity.
- Newer/stronger source: the Chapter 36/37 training scene appears more technically specific.

### Shroud as separate magic versus hereditary magic gate

- Version A: High Clade culture treats Shrouds, lineage, and hereditary magic as one legitimacy package.
- Version B: outline planning says Shrouds are a different type of magic and can be crossbred with another hereditary magic type.
- Source of A: `bible/organizations/org-high-clades.md`; Presentation/prologue manuscript.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7185-7235`, paragraphs 2817-2820.
- Likely consequence: accepted record can distinguish cultural dogma/social package from underlying magical taxonomy, if approved.
- Newer/stronger source: accepted Naeroth/Archeon decisions already support exception cases but do not fully define taxonomy.

## Proposed accepted record

This is a proposal, not accepted canon until author approval.

### present-canon

- The Shroud is a magus defensive field/reflex that manifests around the body and can block or absorb physical and magical attacks.
- Shroud use is tied to bodily awareness/proprioception and must be maintained under focus, stress, and combat pressure.
- A Shroud is not only armor: it participates in sublimating incoming energy into forms aligned with the magus's internal circuits or hereditary magic, allowing the magus to redirect or dissipate dangerous force.
- Unfocused magical energy is dangerous; Shroud manifestation is a major safety gate for High Clade children because it protects against backlash and accidents.
- Shrouds have roughly similar baseline capacity. Practical "strength" comes from stability, training, reflexive maintenance, throughput, and how efficiently the magus sublimates energy before being overwhelmed.
- Enough energy delivered fast enough can overwhelm a Shroud before the magus can sublimate it away.
- High Clade culture treats Shroud manifestation, Presentation, lineage, and hereditary magic as a legitimacy package.
- Maya is an advanced Shroud user whose Shroud is effectively permanent while she is awake, though she can consciously mute it.
- Davian can form a weak but real Shroud and needs to train it to integrate martial arts, Subversion/kinesis, and Ink exposure.
- Kelyra has a trauma-blocked Shroud: externally, she never manifested one; internally, the capacity is suppressed because she associated Shroud manifestation with Archeon captivity and child loss.
- Selwin currently cannot form a Shroud at all.

### backstory-canon

- Maya's Shroud manifested after Niall's severe fear-based childhood pressure.
- High Clade and Archeon training systems pressure children toward early Shroud manifestation because unmanifested or uncontrolled children are dangerous to themselves and others.
- Archeon publicly brands itself as having unusually powerful Shrouds, but its true combat power depends on a stack of kinesis, Ink, Shroud use, martial arts, and technology.
- Naeroth lineages are a rare exception to the dominant assumption that Shrouds and hereditary magic belong only to humans; Alara had a Shroud alongside fateweaving/Convergence-associated abilities.

### accepted-future-plan

- Maya's prologue should be revised so a distant Drakhal-affiliated attack triggers frantic Shroud/magic use and destructive panic.
- Kelyra's later Shroud work should be tied to choosing to protect her family rather than only suppressing herself to escape Archeon.

### candidate

- Treat "strong Shroud" as in-world shorthand rather than literal greater baseline capacity.
- Define Shroud as a separate inheritance/magic channel from hereditary magic, while High Clade culture treats the package as indivisible.
- Preserve Ink-before-Shroud and Ink/Shroud interruption mechanics for `magic-ink-boons`.
- Preserve Selwin's long-term Shroud possibility or incompatibility for later Selwin/Alara/Naeroth/Dissolution reviews.

### contradiction

- "Strong Shroud" language conflicts with the throughput model unless treated as shorthand.
- Cultural dogma about Shrouds/hereditary magic conflicts with Naeroth, Archeon, Davian, Kelyra, and Selwin exception cases unless separated from underlying magical taxonomy.

### historical-record

- Older review packets, raw imports, comments, and feedback should not be rewritten to match this later Shroud clarification.

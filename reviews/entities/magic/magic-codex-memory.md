# Entity Review: Codex / Memory Journals / Auratic Codices

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `magic-codex-memory` as the owner for the general magic of auratic codices: memory journals that store vivid experiential memories, use aura-tied key incantations, can anchor aura-like imprints, and can be corrupted or bridged by Elric.

It should not own the full object biography of Alara's specific journal. That belongs to `object-alara-journal`, which is queued separately and should inherit the general codex mechanics approved here.

## Manuscript-established information

### General codex mechanics

- Alden identifies Maya's "encrypted" journal as an `auratic codex`. Such books are not encrypted conventionally; they are encoded with a specific incantation to access stored memories. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3162-3177`.
- A well-made journal has a structured key incantation unique to its creator, usually tied to the creator's aura or intent. Poorly made ones may require trial and error. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3171-3177`.
- Alden warns that codex memories can be vivid, feel like living the moment, and be overwhelming or dangerous. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3177-3183`.
- Current manuscript shows the access process as focus on a page, recitation of a key incantation, faint zän etched into parchment, and then immersion into a memory. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3498-3522`.
- Later current manuscript describes accessing a codex as focusing, speaking, and letting the user's aura press into the tether carved there. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:11746-11765`.

### Alara's journal / Maya's codex

- Maya finds a leather-bound journal bearing Alara Rezin's name in a hidden Rezin citadel alcove, after a dream and a sense of presence lead her there. Lifecycle: object details for `object-alara-journal`; general codex pull/presence mechanics for this review. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2972-2992`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18859-19063`.
- The key incantation appears or becomes obvious after Maya asks Alden how to decrypt the journal; author comments say this should be made more obvious and that Maya should see or suspect Elric near her rooms before the key mysteriously appears. Lifecycle: revision candidate / Elric corruption mechanics. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:3507-3516`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.comments.md:925-950`.
- Maya experiences Alara's memories from within, including sensory, emotional, and magical perspective. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6400-6595`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:2733-2816`.

### Niall's codex

- Niall has a codex or memory book connected to Alara that he cannot make himself open again but cannot destroy. Aldira shows it to Maya and verifies it has not been tampered with. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:11746-11764`.
- Maya enters Niall's memory through the codex and experiences his body, sensory field, rage, grief, lightning channels, and dissociation as though inside the event. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:11764-12012`.
- Planning says Niall may show Maya his codex when he cannot explain Alara's death or wants to warn her away from fateweaving. Lifecycle: candidate scene/arc material. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:697-703`.

### Elric bridge / corrupted codex

- Alden detects that Maya's codex key incantation is too long, in a different hand, structurally wrong, and overlaid with something that does not belong. He calls it a bridge: a way to speak through the codex in memories where the writer is represented, or possibly where the memory is in a location the writer knows well. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7378-7505`.
- Alden says codices do not just store memories; they anchor auras, like the Registry. He suggests the bridge may have granted the appearance of sentience to Elric's imprint rather than summoning Elric's consciousness. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7428-7505`.
- Alden later says the overlay was not written recently. It was stabilized through an old ritual-layered method, likely during the initial anchoring. Niall identifies Elric as the likely author or planner. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:7485-7505`.
- Niall says if anything is left of Elric in the codex system, it is an echo, residue, or ghost made of the worst parts of his aura. He realizes Elric gave them the codex for a plan beyond preserving memories. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19057-19105`.
- In Elric's hidden chamber, an Ink simulacrum/remnant interacts with Maya, Niall, and Harvick; it says it is nicer to see Maya somewhere other than a dream or memory. Lifecycle: overlaps Ink, codex, zän, Elric survival, and Skein mechanics. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19129-19324`.

## Existing approved constraints

- Elric's original body was destroyed by Niall, but Elric survives through Ink and the Harbinger leader. Codex/aura mechanisms may carry Elric's voice, trap, imprint, or direct presence, but exact mechanics remain unresolved. Provenance: `bible/characters/char-elric.md`.
- Elric's fate sight is real but its source remains unresolved; possible contributors include Heart communion, Ink, original-sin/reality-fracture knowledge, old research, or some combination. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-fateweaving-skein.md`.
- Maya's fate/Skein perception is real but developing; codex memories and Elric's bridge should not make her a fully trained fateweaver. Provenance: `bible/magic/magic-fateweaving-skein.md`.
- Niall's fear around fate/Skein work is tied to Alara and Elric trauma but is not fully resolved. Provenance: `bible/characters/char-niall.md`; `bible/magic/magic-fateweaving-skein.md`.

## Consistent information

- `Auratic codex` is the current manuscript's technical term for the magic category.
- Codices are books/journals that store memories in a way accessed by key incantation, aura, intent, and/or a carved tether.
- Codex memories are immersive and can transmit sensory, emotional, bodily, and magical perspective.
- Codices can anchor aura-like remnants or imprints, but whether those imprints are sentient is unresolved.
- Elric corrupted or overlaid Alara's codex key with a bridge that lets him interact through some memories.
- The codex trap is part of Elric's long-running plan to pull Maya toward fate/Skein knowledge.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Memory storage versus aura anchoring

- Version A: codices are memory journals that store experiential memories.
- Version B: Alden says codices also anchor auras like the Registry, allowing possible imprints or pseudo-sentience.
- Current recommendation: accept both at high level: normal function is memory access; advanced or corrupted function can involve aura anchoring. Exact imprint/sentience mechanics remain unresolved.

### Elric presence

- Version A: Elric is dead in his original body, so direct conversation through a memory should be impossible.
- Version B: Elric speaks through the codex bridge and later through an Ink simulacrum/remnant.
- Current recommendation: do not settle whether the codex bridge is Elric's true consciousness. Accept only that Elric created or caused a corrupted bridge that can present an interactive Elric-like imprint and affect Maya.

### Key incantation timing

- Version A: current manuscript lets Maya find the key incantation inside the journal after reading Alden's books and wondering whether she missed it.
- Version B: author comments want it clearer that Elric or Elric-linked interference causes the key to appear or become available.
- Current recommendation: store this as revision candidate: make Elric's interference clearer without turning the journal into an ordinary unlocked book.

## Proposed accepted record

These points are proposed for author approval, not yet canonized by this packet.

### present-canon

- `Auratic codex` is the technical term for the memory-journal magic category.
- Codices store vivid experiential memories that can be accessed by a key incantation tied to the creator's aura or intent.
- Access involves focus, recitation, and aura/tether interaction.
- Codex memories can be dangerously immersive, carrying sensory, emotional, bodily, and magical perspective.
- Codices can anchor aura-like imprints in addition to memories.
- Alara's journal is a specific auratic codex; its object history belongs to `object-alara-journal`.

### backstory-canon

- Elric corrupted or overlaid Alara's codex key with a bridge mechanism long before Maya finds it.
- The bridge lets an Elric-like imprint or remnant speak through some memories where Elric is represented or where he has enough connection.
- It remains unresolved whether that interactive presence is true Elric consciousness, an aura imprint, an Ink-linked remnant, or a trap that imitates sentience.

### accepted-future-plan

- The codex trap helps pull Maya toward fate/Skein knowledge and reinforces the end-book pattern in which she believes she can and must repair impossible losses.

### candidate

- Niall showing Maya his codex to explain Alara's death.
- Codex idealization of Maya's parents as part of Elric's trap.
- Harvick having his own Elric-linked codex.
- Exact aura-imprint sentience mechanics.
- Exact Registry/codex/true-name relationship.
- Exact link between codices, Ink, and Elric's persistence.

# Entity Review: Zan / zan lattices / zanwrights

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `Zan / zan lattices / zanwrights` as the owner for written/etched magical symbols, connected magical circuits, and the craft tradition around designing, etching, maintaining, miniaturizing, and interpreting those systems.

It should not own every object that uses zan. Object-specific mechanics should remain with the relevant later records:

- Registry technical details: `loc-registry`, `org-registry`, and this file for lattice mechanics
- Treasury sphere / Heart containment: `object-seraph-heart`, `loc-treasury`, and this file for lattice mechanics
- Niall's silver conduits: Niall / Heart / later object or technology review
- Zirene's bands, wands, and weapons: `char-zirene`, object, and technology reviews
- Archeon gauntlets and Antithesis: `object-antithesis`, Archeon technology, and this file for lattice principles

## Manuscript-established information

### Everyday and institutional zanwork

- The current manuscript presents zän as etched symbols or circuits embedded in architecture, weapons, clothing, walls, doors, and tools. They glow, pulse, hum, and respond to magic. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:312-426`, `981-996`, `1338-1467`.
- Protective clothing, sconces, citadel walls, Central Spire architecture, checkpoint arches, and High Clade boxes use zän as ordinary High Clade infrastructure or ornamented magical engineering. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:234`, `1338-1467`.
- Antithesis and Harvick's sword have visible zän on their blades in the current manuscript. Exact Antithesis mechanics are already routed to later object review and transformation language is obsolete. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:414-426`, `591-603`, `21168-21203`; `decisions/0019-ink-pact-terminology-and-core-mechanics.md`.

### Registry and aurametric systems

- The Registry chamber contains glowing zän, an arcane circle, a central pedestal, an aurametric citadel projection, true-name/aura registration, and wall entries that can locate or verify registered people. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:2670-2805`, paragraphs 1120-1167.
- Maya later uses the Registry to verify Arrek Hadrin and tries to locate him; the Registry confirms his entry but cannot locate him until later, when it pulses and refines to the Treasury corridor with Arrek, Niall, and Harvick. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15140-15970`, paragraphs 5352-5620.
- The approved Registry record establishes the Registry as Rezin-specific ancient magical security/identification; detailed zan-lattice mechanics remain for this review. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-registry.md`.

### Treasury sphere and Heart-adjacent zanwork

- The Treasury sphere is sustained by sprawling, interwoven, redundant zän/lattices. Davian discovers that every stabilization zä has multiple reinforcing sequences, so disabling one node causes the lattice to route power back into the weakened node. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:4480-5445`, paragraphs 1757-1765.
- Planning says the Treasury sphere's glyph/zän sinks transform reality-distorting Heart energy into gravity negation, and that after the sphere breaks Davian can see the twisted wreckage of an intricate zan network around the Heart, including Nizan. Lifecycle: candidate technical material pending author approval. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:8480-8555`, paragraphs 3295-3315.
- Approved Registry material already states that the Registry's overpowered modern operation depends on the Heart and that Maya's post-theft repair ideas involve passive detection, gateways, zan sinks, and more efficient power arrangements. Provenance: `bible/organizations/org-registry.md`.

### Ancient / Elric / structural zanwork

- Elric's hidden chamber contains zanwork embedded into walls, floors, and ceilings in dense overlapping lattices. The zan are unfamiliar, layered beyond easy parsing, structural rather than modular or symbolic in the modern way, and intertwined with the architecture. Maya recognizes parallels to Zirene's older, heavier, persistence-oriented logic. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:18740-19140`, paragraphs 6606-6693.
- Planning repeats that Elric's workspace has zan mechanisms embedded directly into stone, old and superior complexity, denser layering, less symbolic and more structural logic, and a contained Ink pool maintained by those mechanisms. Lifecycle: planning-established/candidate for exact mechanisms. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7535-7690`, paragraphs 2988-2991.

### Zirene's zan theory and skin/metal lattice work

- Zirene identifies hidden body lattices and says magic is not just head-focused; arms, legs, hands, and bodies are used to focus whether people admit it or not. She says Davian's Ink was layered over an Archeon pattern. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`, paragraphs 6854-6859.
- Zirene says Concord sign / masked codes are based on zan. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`, paragraphs 6868-6872.
- Zirene says component za can be miniaturized into fully functional za at smaller scales, with the limit being the zanwright's tooling ability and vision. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`, paragraphs 6876-6879.
- Zirene says a za means something and that representation gives it power. She argues that high-level attached work needs to "understand" the person it is attached to, using a person's za / name / aura as the conceptual problem. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`, paragraphs 6880-6889.
- Zirene uses newer names such as the Blade, the Deep, and the Crown to remember strokes, and says the writing system and hand language come from za. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`, paragraphs 6890-6898.
- Zirene anchors black iron bands to Davian's skin the same way she anchored Niall's metals. The bands are covered in zanwork, are difficult to remove, can kill him if removed or activated, amplify electrical or cooling input, and include failsafes specific to Niall and Maya. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`, paragraphs 6900-6918.

## Consistent information

- Zan/za are meaningful etched magical units, not arbitrary decorative marks.
- Lattices are connected systems of zan/za that can route, reinforce, amplify, transform, detect, store, or maintain magical effects.
- High-end zanwork depends on both symbolic representation and physical craft/tooling.
- Modern practical systems appear more modular and symbolic; older or deeper systems can be more structural, dense, embedded, and persistent.
- Large infrastructure such as the Registry and Treasury sphere relies on ancient, high-power, redundant zanwork.
- The Heart currently overpowers or enables some Rezin systems that would otherwise be inefficient or impossible to operate at their present scope.
- Zirene is an unusually advanced zanwright/technology figure whose work bridges body, metal, person-specific representation, and combat devices.
- Exact naming, spelling, and taxonomy remain inconsistent across sources.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Terminology and spelling

- Version A: current manuscript uses `zä`, `zän`, `glyph`, `glyphwright`, and occasionally broader "zanwork" terms.
- Version B: queue/control files use `zan`, `zan lattices`, and `zanwrights`; later planning also uses `jin`, `jinwright`, and `Zan`.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md`.
- Source of B: `ENTITY_INDEX.md`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7955-8125`; `imports/normalized/bullet-notes.md:132`.
- Likely consequence: this review should settle the reference-file spelling and whether manuscript-facing terms differ from technical reference terms.

### Modern symbols versus ancient representation

- Version A: modern users such as Davian treat a za as a symbol that simply does what it does when etched.
- Version B: Zirene says a za means something, representation gives it power, and high-level work needs to understand the attached person; planning says ancient zan had representational/archetypal meaning later lost in practical simplification.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7955-8125`.
- Current recommendation: accept both as layered truth: practical modern zanwork is often treated like modular engineering, but advanced/ancient zanwork depends on representation and conceptual fit.

### Heart-powered inefficiency

- Version A: modern Rezin systems operate at enormous scope and are trusted as security infrastructure.
- Version B: approved Registry direction and planning say much of that scope depends on Heart-powered near-infinite input and becomes inefficient or nonfunctional without it.
- Source of A: current Registry and Treasury prose.
- Source of B: `bible/organizations/org-registry.md`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7535-7690`.
- Current recommendation: accept that Heart-backed systems are real and powerful, but they are not necessarily elegantly efficient.

### Zirene / zanwright ownership

- Version A: current manuscript names Zirene and shows her building bands for Davian and having made Niall's metals.
- Version B: planning mentions "the zanwright" as the figure who made Niall's metals, the knife, Archeon gear, and Shroud-piercing weapons; some notes leave name/gender open.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19540-19820`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7955-8125`.
- Current recommendation: accept only what approved records already support: Zirene-derived technology is tied to Niall's metals, Davian's bands, Archeon designs, and Antithesis lineage at high level; exact inventory and character scope belongs to `char-zirene` and object reviews.

## Proposed accepted record

Approved by author feedback on 2026-06-24.

### present-canon

- The accepted in-world spelling is `zä`, `zän`, and `zänwright`; unaccented forms may remain in notes and control-file IDs for ease of typing.
- `Zänwright` is the final profession term for Book 1.
- Zä are meaningful magical signs or components that can be etched, carved, written, or embedded into materials, architecture, tools, weapons, clothing, bodies, and aura-adjacent systems.
- Zä lattices work like magical circuitboards: connected systems of components that can reinforce, route, store, amplify, detect, transform, maintain, or discharge magical effects.
- The base-6 number system is structurally linked to zä design, and the strike-line running through numbers and characters conducts energy between zä in a lattice.
- Modern practical zänwork is modular, scalable, and durable. Ancient zä are more complex but less durable and less scalable; their complexity represents more complex circuitry.
- The Registry and Treasury sphere use ancient high-power zä lattices. Their modern Rezin operation is powerful but inefficient and relies heavily on the Heart's power.
- Zirene is an advanced zänwright/technology figure whose work can anchor person-specific lattices to skin or metal and whose designs are connected to Niall's metals, Davian's bands, Archeon technology, and Antithesis lineage at high level.
- Niall's hidden silver conduits are zä-etched lightning modifiers; exact mechanics remain unresolved.

### backstory-canon

- Older Rezin and Elric-associated systems used dense structural zänwork embedded into architecture and designed for persistence.
- Modern writing systems and masked/Concord hand-sign systems derive from older zä/zän principles.
- Ancient zä theory preserved more representational meaning than many practical modern systems retain.

### accepted-future-plan

- After the Heart theft, Maya's Registry repair attempts should treat the old system as inefficient without near-limitless Heart input and should focus on narrower passive/gateway detection rather than restoring full functionality.
- Later reviews should use zan-lattice principles to explain Treasury/Heart containment, Niall's silver, Zirene devices, Archeon gauntlets, and Antithesis without forcing all object mechanics into this one magic record.

### candidate

- Number-system details, including `Nizän` / `nïza` and empty-value notation.
- Archetypal/conceptual zä such as the Tyrant, negation, and balance.
- Person-specific zä, true-name/aura theory, and whether a person can be represented directly.
- Skein-layer zä and deeper reality-interface mechanics.
- Shroud-piercing weapons and guns/wands.
- Naeroth/jin/jinwright origins and Elarion mass-production history.

### contradiction

- Current terminology is inconsistent across manuscript, outline, bullet notes, and control files.
- Some current manuscript Antithesis transformation material is obsolete after the Ink Pact approval.
- Some planning may fold too much technology into Zirene before `char-zirene` and object reviews establish exact scope.

## Questions for the author

Resolved by author feedback on 2026-06-24:

1. Accepted spelling is accented: `zä`, `zän`, and `zänwright`. Unaccented note forms are typing shortcuts.
2. `Zänwright` is the final profession term; `glyphwright` and `jinwright` should not supersede it.
3. Accept the circuitboard model: zä are components in lattices, linked to the base-6 number system and strike-line conduction.
4. Accept broad Heart-powered inefficiency: major Rezin systems work, but many are overbuilt around near-limitless Heart input and fail or shrink after the Heart is stolen.
5. Accept Zirene at high level as the advanced zänwright behind or connected to Niall's metals, Davian's bands, Archeon-derived technology, and Antithesis lineage; exact device inventory waits for later reviews.
6. Accept modern writing systems and masked/Concord hand signs as deriving from zä/zän.
7. Defer number-system, `Nizän` / `nïza`, and archetypal zä material to more specific reviews.
8. Keep Skein-layer zä / reality-layer etching candidate until later cosmology or advanced-zän review.

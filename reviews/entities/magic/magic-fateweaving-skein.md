# Entity Review: Fateweaving / Skein / fate ties

## Proposed identity

This packet treats `Fateweaving / Skein / fate ties` as the working owner for the cluster of magic and metaphysics involving:

- the Skein as a structure, substrate, or dimensional layer of reality;
- strands, threads, and fate ties;
- Maya's emerging ability to perceive, follow, pull, and possibly alter fate threads;
- rare Naeroth access to fateweaving;
- Elric's dark-thread sight and manipulation;
- the late-book resurrection / reality-damage plotline;
- the unresolved relation between fateweaving, Convergence, Ink Pacts, the Heart, original sin, and reality fracture.

The entity may need to split later if Naeroth fateweaving, the Skein as cosmology, and Elric's dark-thread manipulation need separate owner files.

## Manuscript-established information

### Alara, Elric, and first exposure

- In an Alara/Niall codex memory, Elric asks Alara whether she has felt the "strands of fate" and Alara says this is not spoken of among her people. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6463-6481`, codex memory, paragraphs 2416-2428.
- Elric addresses Maya from inside the memory, frames current magic as blood, birthright, zä, sigil, discipline, and will, then points to older currents beneath the lattice and truths older than the first zä. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6481-6520`, codex memory, paragraphs 2431-2446.
- Maya sees time, distance, memory, and possibility fold into layered perception; she then sees many threads in a void, one particular thread that calls to her, and knows she could touch it and change the world. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:6521-6589`, codex memory, paragraphs 2447-2461.

### Maya's current fate/Skein perception

- After the Heart theft aftermath, Maya's dreams and waking perception include bright Skein threads tugging backward and forward through time, feeding guilt, vigilance, catastrophizing, and imagined alternate outcomes. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16935-16990`, Chapter 30, paragraphs 5944-5967.
- Maya states that she can see the threads of fate and uses that claim while confronting Davian about his agency in the Heart theft. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20240-20260`, Chapter 32/33.
- Maya can follow a thread from Davian's knife toward him; the thread presents as a bright/golden path toward the Archeon city. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:17749-17785`, Chapter 31/32.

### The Skein as perception of time, possibility, and reality damage

- During a fate vision, Maya is thrown through Vorren's lineage and branching timelines; she experiences events backward through cause and consequence and witnesses alternate versions of Elric killing Niall and Niall killing Elric. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15880-15920`, fate vision, paragraphs 5588-5601.
- After that vision, Maya is thrown clear of the Skein and sees it as a vast tapestry of reality with a torn gap; dark threads spill from the wound. She tries to force the wound and threads to align or converge, but cannot. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:15920-15935`, fate vision, paragraphs 5602-5609.
- Maya later uses a strand of the Skein to change spatial access to Elric's hidden tunnel, experiencing a mind-space outside reality and briefly perceiving space, time, and possibility together. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19000-19020`, Chapter 32, paragraphs 6648-6652.

### Elric, threads, and reality

- An Elric Ink simulacrum/remnant says that when he listens to the threads, all he hears is corruption, then suggests he has set in motion a scheme to correct what is wrong with reality. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19220-19290`, Chapter 32, paragraphs 6719-6741.
- Niall says Elric was driven to madness by his studies, and treats fate/Skein interaction as dangerous. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19060-19310`, Chapter 32, paragraphs 6671-6741.
- Current manuscript repeatedly links Ink and fate-like threads: Davian experiences dark Ink threads pulling him toward Niall and toward destructive choices, while Maya experiences bright Skein threads tied to guilt, fear, possibility, and Davian. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:11159-11210`, `16153-16276`, `19450-19955`, `16935-16990`.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### One entity or several

- Version A: Current queue groups Fateweaving, Skein, and fate ties into one entity.
- Version B: Evidence suggests at least three distinct ownership needs: Naeroth fateweaving, the Skein as reality/cosmology, and Elric's dark-thread trauma manipulation.
- Consequence: One accepted file may become too broad or muddy unless it explicitly separates these layers.
- Current recommendation: Keep one umbrella file for now, with later split points documented.

### Fate sight and agency

- Version A: Some manuscript and planning language makes threads feel inevitable, especially dark threads and traumatic paths.
- Version B: Approved character arcs require meaningful agency; Elric sees and leans on paths people are likely to follow when acting from trauma, not absolute fate.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16153-16276`, `19450-19955`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:580-700`.
- Source of B: `outline/arcs/thread-elric-reality-skein.md`; `decisions/0007-elric-rezin-remnant-and-reality-skein-role.md`.
- Likely consequence: Accepted mechanics should define fate sight as probabilistic / path-of-least-resistance / trauma-script perception unless author wants stronger determinism.

### Source of Elric's fate sight

- Version A: Planning says communion with the Heart let Elric start seeing fate strands.
- Version B: Comments suggest Ink lets Elric see fate threads more easily.
- Version C: Elric's knowledge may derive from original sin / first magus / reality fracture research.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7623`.

## Proposed accepted record

This is a proposal only.

### present-canon

- The Skein is a real fate/reality structure that can be experienced through threads, strands, paths, possibility, and time-adjacent perception.
- Maya currently has emerging fate/Skein perception. She can see bright threads tied to past/future possibilities, guilt, choices, and paths.
- Maya's fate/Skein perception is emotionally dangerous because it interacts with vigilance, perfectionism, catastrophizing, and responsibility.
- Elric can see fate strands tied to people acting out trauma or negative impulses, and uses that sight to nudge events toward his goals.
- Fate-thread sight does not remove agency. It reveals or amplifies likely paths when people follow trauma scripts, urges, fear, guilt, avoidance, or compulsions.
- Ink and fate/Skein mechanics overlap, but exact technical interaction remains unresolved.

### backstory-canon

- Alara was from Naeroth and had rare access to fateweaving and Convergence heat transfer, as well as a Shroud.
- Naeroth heritage can rarely grant fateweaving and Convergence heat transfer despite the wider High Clade cultural assumption that only humans have hereditary magic and Shrouds.
- Elric studied fate/Skein systems and was damaged by exposure to truths or possible futures he could not integrate.
- Elric's fall is tied to learning that reality was fractured after the original sin of the first magus.

### accepted-future-plan

- Maya's fateweaving/Skein ability is central to the late-book resurrection and reality-damage plot.
- Elric's endgame uses Niall, Davian, Maya, the Heart, Ink, and resurrection to damage or unmake reality.
- Maya entering the Skein to resurrect Davian is part of the accepted future direction; exact mechanics and scene execution remain to be defined later.
- Elric's apparent inevitability should be undermined by characters choosing values and relationships instead of following trauma scripts.

### candidate

- The Heart as the source or amplifier of Elric's fate sight.
- Ink as a visibility or control mechanism for fate threads.
- The beach/shore, moon/eye, threadblights, dark ocean, and larger reality outside the Skein.
- Exact mechanics and cost of tearing fate, resurrection, and reality damage.
- Whether Fateweaving and Convergence share an underlying schema, dimensional model, or Naeroth training foundation.
- Whether Niall's fear that fateweaving is corruptive is correct, partly correct, or mainly projection from Alara/Elric trauma.

### contradiction

- Older wording sometimes treats thread/fate imagery as deterministic; approved character arcs require agency.
- The source of Elric's fate sight is not yet consistent across planning/comment material.
- The relationship between Maya's Fateweaving and Convergence remains unresolved.

# Entity Review: Ink / Ink Boons / Ink Pact

## Proposed identity

Ink is an Elric-origin magical technology/system that can be etched into skin and aura as Boons or Pacts. In current material it grants or modifies abilities, binds users to bargains or routes of influence, accelerates movement, enables aura/name mimicry, heals or sustains bodies under some conditions, creates intrusive/compulsive pressure, and can serve as a path for Elric's persistence or possession.

The terminology is not yet fully stable. Current sources use `Ink`, `Ink Boon`, `Boon`, and `Ink Pact`. A useful distinction may be:

- `Ink Boon`: a granted, finite, task-focused or ability-focused application.
- `Ink Pact`: deeper, longer-term, contract-like Ink binding that may persist, shape the user, or allow Elric influence/possession.

This distinction is proposed, not approved.

## Manuscript-established information

### Syndicate / Harbinger ritual and Davian's Boons

- The early Syndicate/Harbinger leader gives Davian an Ink Boon ritual after Davian volunteers for the Heart theft and threatens to kill Niall. Ink bleeds from the walls, pools into an intricate zä beneath Davian, coils around his body and Antithesis, burns symbols into his skin, and leaves him unconscious. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`, Chapter 2, paragraphs 552-610.
- In that current manuscript version, the leader describes the first rhythm as a mimic's mask originally tuned for Jalen: borrow a face, steal a name, and pass through the gates. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`, paragraphs 603-607.
- In that current manuscript version, the second Boon reshapes Antithesis into a small workman's knife, and Jalen says it can change back into its normal form once. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`, paragraphs 607-629.
- Davian later tells Aldira, Maya, and Harvick that Harbinger Ink let him steal someone else's aura. He shows layered arm patterns, distinguishes Harbinger Ink from ordinary tattoos, and admits he killed Arrek to get the aura. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20180-20235`, later interrogation, paragraphs 7047-7064.
- Kelyra's current manuscript planning of the heist uses aura mimicry and Registry name/aura assumptions, though the later accepted Registry decision simplifies the final canon to Davian adding Arrek's name and relying on Ink to maintain the aura/name bypass. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:1850-1920`, Chapter 3, paragraphs 841-863; `decisions/0017-registry-power-modes-and-elric-bypass.md`.

### Davian's mental pressure, hallucination, and information

- Before Davian receives the ritual, darkness presses in at the edges of his mind and whispers that none of this matters and he should let it collapse. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`, paragraphs 570-576.
- After the Heart theft, the Ink answers when Davian faces Niall: a dark thread unfurls toward Niall and produces the clear impulse to kill him. Davian resists by telling himself it is his choice. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19180-19490`, Chapter 32/33, paragraphs 6798-6805.
- Davian tells Maya and Niall that the Harbingers did something to him with Ink, that it tries to tell him what to do, and that he has information from the Ink in his head. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19180-19490`, paragraphs 6805-6812.
- Davian later says the Ink told or showed him to stab the knife into the Treasury sphere, which made his Subversion/energy work easier. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20180-20460`, later training, paragraph 7136.

### Healing, depletion, and overuse

- Davian says Treasury backlash shattered both his arms and the Ink healed him; without it, he probably would have died. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20180-20460`, later training, paragraph 7128.
- Kelyra's Trial of Dominion scene shows both acceleration and depletion: she uses Ink for speed/perception/force, Karra has Ink-assisted speed and later apparent healing, and Kelyra's Ink runs out when she pushes too far. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16580-16895`, Kelyra/Harbinger scene, paragraphs 5892-5923.
- Kelyra remembers the first Harbinger meeting after Selina's death, Ink burning into her skin, and waking to power: speed, precision, strength, and enough force to kill the prior Concord leader. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16580-16895`, paragraphs 5829-5839.

### Kelyra, Karra, and Trial of Dominion

- Kelyra's old Ink empowered her Trial of Dominion victory over the former Concord leader and helped her take the Concord's leadership. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16580-16895`, paragraphs 5829-5839; `bible/characters/char-kelyra.md`; `decisions/0010-jalen-kelyra-concord-role-swap.md`.
- The Harbinger leader points to Karra and says he made a pact with her. Karra reveals familiar Ink, and the leader frames the fight through pact forms, channels, scales, and Ink flowing toward the one the scales tip toward. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16580-16895`, paragraphs 5861-5872.
- Kelyra sees Karra's Ink patterns on the shoulders/upper chest and infers that placement/timing may matter, but she does not have time to decipher them. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16580-16895`, paragraphs 5878-5891.

### Elric remnant, Ink pool, and linkage

- Maya, Niall, and Harvick encounter an ink-dark simulacrum/remnant of Elric. Niall's lightning punches through it; Ink scatters and reforms. The remnant says its continued existence was an accident caused by too much Ink, and once the Ink dissipates it will finally be destroyed. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19180-19490`, paragraphs 6708-6744.
- Maya concludes there is a link between Elric and the assassin, drawn in Ink. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19180-19490`, paragraphs 6747-6753.
- Planning for Elric's workspace describes an Ink pool tied to the same network as Davian's Ink; Davian is "plugged into a legacy system, not a rogue mutation." Lifecycle: planning-established, not accepted as exact mechanism. Provenance: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:7500-7735`, paragraphs 2979-2997.

### Harvick's prototype Ink Pact

- Harvick says he received a hidden Ink Pact from Elric during early experiments. It felt like an ocean of Ink that could let him do anything, but the second he touched it, it dried up; the tattoo was small and faded almost immediately. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21335-21475`, Chapter 36 training, paragraphs 7452-7460.
- Harvick says Elric had him perform sword forms and listen for a right or wrong way to move. It does not tell him what to do as a voice; it lets him feel faster ways to get somewhere. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21335-21475`, paragraphs 7457-7464.
- This matches the approved Harvick record: his prototype Pact gives bursts of unnatural speed and precision but not Davian-style command pressure. Provenance: `bible/characters/char-harvick.md`; `decisions/0008-harvick-elric-ink-and-mentorship.md`.

### Kelyra old Ink scar reaction

- After Serathis discusses Drakhal, Kelyra feels old Ink on her arm crawl near Serathis and hears or feels the whisper to kill her. Provenance: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20940-21155`, Chapter 35, paragraphs 7370-7374.

## Consistent information

- Ink originates with or is heavily developed by Elric.
- Ink is visible as dark tattoos/patterns that may fade, crawl, writhe, or remain hidden.
- Ink can grant physical enhancement such as speed, precision, strength, perception dilation, or healing/sustainment.
- Ink can grant specialized effects such as aura/name mimicry or object alteration in current manuscript material.
- Ink can create intrusive pressure, command-like impulses, dark threads, or voice-like direction.
- Ink can deplete or run out, and overuse can leave the user vulnerable.
- Ink is used by the Harbingers to consolidate underworld power and enforce dependency.
- Ink can form deeper Pacts that allow long-term influence or possession, especially Elric's possession of the Harbinger leader.
- Prototype Ink can be weaker, subtler, or qualitatively different from later Harbinger Boons, as with Harvick.

## Contradictions and version conflicts

### Boon versus Pact terminology

- Version A: current manuscript frequently uses `Ink Boon` / `Boon` for granted abilities.
- Version B: accepted Elric/Harvick/Archeon decisions use `Ink Pact` for deeper Elric-linked bindings.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`; current Davian/Kelyra material.
- Source of B: `decisions/0007`, `0008`, `0010`, `0012`, `0015`.
- Likely consequence: define a terminology distinction or choose one umbrella term.
- Newer/stronger source: accepted decisions support `Ink Pact` for deeper bindings; current manuscript supports `Boon` as common underworld wording.

### Davian's Boon set

- Version A: current manuscript gives Davian an aura/name mimic Boon and an Antithesis transformation Boon.
- Version B: planning considers alternate Boon sets: aura plus acceleration, one Boon with future-presaging language, family transfer, healing, or removal of the sword-changing Boon.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3965-4045`; `imports/normalized/bullet-notes.md:183`.
- Likely consequence: accepted record should store only aura/name mimicry and Ink pressure as currently durable for Davian, while leaving final Boon set and Antithesis transformation candidate.
- Newer/stronger source: unresolved; Registry decision confirms aura/name bypass but not final Antithesis Boon.

### Transfer and inheritance

- Version A: current manuscript has the leader grant Davian Boons directly.
- Version B: planning considers Kelyra/Jalen/family transfer or first-degree-relative transfer symbolism.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:984-1265`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:3965-4045`.
- Likely consequence: keep transfer mechanics candidate unless author wants them approved.
- Newer/stronger source: unresolved.

### Healing mechanics

- Version A: Davian states Ink healed shattered arms and would have saved him from death.
- Version B: Kelyra/Karra scene shows Karra apparently restored after fatal injury and Kelyra planning to fight until Karra's Ink runs out.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20180-20460`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:16580-16895`.
- Likely consequence: Ink can heal/sustain in some forms, but limits, cost, speed, and whether this is a distinct Boon remain unresolved.
- Newer/stronger source: current manuscript supports healing broadly; exact model unresolved.

### Ink/Shroud interaction

- Version A: current manuscript shows Davian can have Ink exposure and form a Shroud, and Harvick says his prototype does not command him.
- Version B: bullet notes speculate Ink may need to be received before Shroud manifestation or may interrupt Shroud during Ink-adjacent ability use.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:20180-20460`; `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:21335-21475`.
- Source of B: `imports/normalized/bullet-notes.md:183`; `bible/magic/magic-shroud.md`.
- Likely consequence: do not finalize unless author approves a rule.
- Newer/stronger source: unresolved.

### Control versus influence

- Version A: Davian experiences intrusive commands but can resist them and insists on choice.
- Version B: accepted Elric decision says the Harbinger leader is almost completely controlled by Elric through an Ink Pact; Kelyra planning includes possible Ink body cooption.
- Source of A: `imports/normalized/melissa-copy.md:19180-19490`.
- Source of B: `decisions/0007`; `imports/normalized/book-1-outline.md:8170-8245`.
- Likely consequence: Ink influence exists on a spectrum from suggestion/pressure to possession, depending on Pact depth, target, duration, and Elric's involvement.
- Newer/stronger source: accepted Elric/Harbinger decisions.

## Proposed accepted record

This is a proposal, not accepted canon until author approval.

### present-canon

- Ink is an Elric-origin magical technology/system currently distributed through the Harbingers.
- Ink is etched into skin/aura as visible or hidden dark marks, tattoos, or patterns; it may fade, crawl, writhe, or remain partially hidden.
- The Harbingers use Ink Boons/Pacts to grant power, bind people, create dependency, manipulate trauma-driven choices, and consolidate underworld control.
- Davian received Harbinger Ink that enabled Arrek aura/name mimicry for the Rezin Registry bypass and created intrusive pressure toward killing Niall.
- Davian can resist Ink pressure; Ink influence does not erase his agency.
- Kelyra received Ink that helped her kill the prior Concord leader in a Trial of Dominion and take Concord leadership.
- Karra received Ink from the Harbinger leader to challenge Kelyra.
- Harvick has a hidden prototypical Elric Ink Pact that gives bursts of speed/precision and correct-movement sense without a voice.
- Elric uses a deeper Ink Pact to possess the Harbinger/Syndicate leader; by the start of the book, the leader is almost fully controlled.

### backstory-canon

- Elric researched or developed Ink before his original body was destroyed.
- Elric gave Harvick a prototype Ink Pact during early experiments.
- Elric gave Serathis Archeon's first Ink Pact before her Shroud was formed.
- Elric gave the future Harbinger leader a pre-book Ink Pact and used it after bodily death to take over the leader.

### accepted-future-plan

- The Harbinger leader's Elric possession completes near the end after Maya enters the Skein to resurrect Davian.
- Elric's use of Ink remains part of his broader endgame around Davian, Maya, the Heart, and reality damage.

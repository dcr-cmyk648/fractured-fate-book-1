## Integrity Checks

### Clade Hierarchy and Presentation

`culture-clade-hierarchy` and `culture-magus-presentation` agree that formal power is organized around High Clades and Low Clades, with Presented/unPresented status acting as a major pressure point.

Presentation is consistently treated as both ritual and political proof: the candidate must control a Shroud and display enough power that the Clade is not embarrassed.

No conflict found.

### Low Clades, Haloes, and Proxy Administration

`object-haloes` and `culture-clade-hierarchy` agree that a High Clade controls minting while a Low Clade proxy likely handles day-to-day operations.

The exact minting High Clade and Low Clade proxy are deferred. This does not block outline work because Book 1 currently needs the currency logic and class/power structure, not the full mint administration.

No conflict found.

### Base-6 Number System, Zán, and Haloes

`culture-number-system-zan-script`, `object-haloes`, and `magic-zan-lattices` are aligned at the accepted level:

- base-6 structure is culturally central
- zán/zá/zánwright are the accepted spellings
- haloes use zán value marks
- strike-line logic connects written numbers, zán lattice design, signs, currency, and cipher concepts
- Nizán is zero / negation / void and is not a routine placeholder for larger base-6 numbers

Remaining exact chart forms, phonemes, and cipher grammar are candidate material.

No conflict found.

### Creator, Sheol, Nizán, and Cosmology

`culture-creator-sheol` and `culture-number-system-zan-script` are aligned at the current accepted level.

The culture accepts Creator language and vague Sheol language without resolving the true cosmology. Nizán now carries accepted zero/negation/void meaning and a diamond-empty visual form, while the exact relationship among Nizán, the extraplanar deity, Sheol, the Heart, and the Skein remains deferred.

No conflict found.

### Naeroth, Fate, and High Clade Assumptions

`culture-naeroth-lineages` cleanly preserves Naeroth as a rare exception to dominant High Clade assumptions about human hereditary magic. It defers broader demi-human taxonomy and exact Fateweaving/Convergence mechanics to later records.

No conflict found.

## Story-Opportunity Checks

- The culture block gives Maya's Presentation stronger scene pressure: she is not merely introduced; she publicly changes the threat calculus among the High Clades.
- Haloes, base-6 logic, and zán script can make ordinary scenes carry world logic without exposition dumps: payments, signs, number language, and authenticity checks all point back to the same system.
- The Clade hierarchy and Masked Clade terminology sharpen the Concord's contradiction: they resist High Clade power while still adopting the power language of the system.
- The Creator/Sheol/Nizán material gives Elric and Serathis a coherent philosophical pressure point without forcing the book to explain the true cosmology too early.
- Naeroth prejudice and exception status give Maya and Selwin outline-useful social pressure without requiring a full race taxonomy now.

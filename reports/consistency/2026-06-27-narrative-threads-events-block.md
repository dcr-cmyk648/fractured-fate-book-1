## Story-Opportunity Checks

### Maya as Deterrent

Maya's Presentation and Niall's waning power create a strong pressure pattern: Maya thinks she is proving herself, while Niall sees her being pulled into the deterrent role he hoped she might avoid.

Opportunity: chapter architecture should preserve Maya's incomplete knowledge. Her misread of Niall should be emotional, not foolish.

### Davian's Agency Chain

The Heart theft and later manor attack now form a useful agency sequence:

- during the theft, Davian doggedly persists without considering alternatives
- during the manor attack, he begins realizing that the dark thread did not force him; he followed it

Opportunity: later chapter mapping should track how Maya's accusation and Harvick's guidance echo during Davian's manor-attack realization.

### Kelyra's Surface Win / Hidden Loss

Kelyra's Trial works best if she is visibly competent and still loses because she is fighting the wrong contest.

Opportunity: keep the chapter's strong action/emotional structure, but during later revision evaluate whether the Ink depletion/transfer explanation should be replaced by a clearer rigged-system explanation.

### Harbinger Reliability as Weapon

The Harbingers' reputation for honoring bargains is now a story tool: it makes their betrayal more effective because Kelyra believes she remains valuable.

Opportunity: later outline work can use that reputation earlier so the betrayal feels inevitable in retrospect rather than arbitrary.

### Political Secrecy

The block cleanly separates what is true from what is public. This is useful for future chapter architecture because different factions operate from different information:

- Rezin knows the Heart is gone and wants secrecy.
- Drakhal suspects weakness but lacks exact proof.
- Other High Clades may have intelligence hints but not public grounds.
- Maya lacks Niall's full power-decline context.

Opportunity: chapter maps should track faction knowledge states, not just event chronology.

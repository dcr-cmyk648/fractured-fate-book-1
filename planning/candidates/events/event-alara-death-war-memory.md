# Candidate Material: Alara's Apparent Death

## Event Boundary

The accepted event record owns Alara's apparent death. Alara/Niall war memories remain primarily in `object-alara-journal` and later chapter mapping.

If later outline work needs a separate "Niall and Alara war/reform memories" packet, create it only if it adds value beyond the journal, Alara character record, and chapter maps.

## Candidate Death Reveal

Preserve for later endgame/chapter review:

- Niall may show Maya his own codex while warning her away from fateweaving.
- Alara may wake at night screaming about an eye watching her.
- Alara may say she knows how to get "higher" or reach a higher-order dimension / plane.
- Niall may be distracted by a report or crisis and fail to notice Alara leaving.
- Niall may find Alara in a courtyard or open space, surging cold/Convergence/fateweaving and insisting that the thing is "right here."
- Niall's Shroud may barely hold as he tries to reach her.
- The apparent death may involve a flash, ash/cinders, cold, lightning, and Niall breaking down.
- Niall may only return from that breakdown because Maya and Selwin still need him.

## Deferred Mechanics

Leave unresolved:

- exact apparent-death choreography
- exact higher-plane topology
- exact Nizän / Sheol / Skein / extraplanar-deity relationship
- exact "eye" imagery
- whether Alara is recoverable in later books
- how much Maya contacts or perceives Alara during Davian's resurrection
- whether Alara's decline is framed in-world as postpartum psychosis, fateweaving breakdown, higher-plane contact, or some combination

## Elric Timing

Accepted direction: Elric likely did not want Alara dead, since a living fateweaver could have been manipulated as a tool for damaging the Skein and summoning the extraplanar deity to consume existence.

Deferred:

- exact timing of Elric's original-body death relative to Alara's decline
- exact stage of Elric's recrystallization / Syndicate takeover during Alara's apparent death
- whether any Elric remnant had indirect awareness of Alara's decline before later exploiting her codex

## Book 1 Visibility

It remains possible that Alara's apparent death does not come up explicitly in Book 1.

If it appears, the scene should serve Niall's grief, Maya's fateweaving fear/temptation, and endgame setup rather than functioning as detached lore.

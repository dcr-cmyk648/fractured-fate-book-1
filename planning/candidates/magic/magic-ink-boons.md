# Ink Pact Candidate Material

This file preserves useful but not fully approved Ink material. It is not accepted canon unless later reviewed and promoted.

## Superseded Terminology

Older source material uses `Ink Boon`, `Boon`, or mixed `Ink Pact/Boon` language.

Accepted terminology is now `Ink Pact` for all Ink grantings.

## Obsolete Antithesis Transformation

Current manuscript and older planning include an Ink effect that transforms Antithesis into a workman's knife and back.

This is no longer canon. Antithesis is now a knife throughout Book 1.

Preserve for later revision work:

- remove or reinterpret Antithesis transformation language
- account for Davian concealing the knife before he is searched inside the Rezin citadel
- check the author's note that `Alra` helps from inside the citadel; exact name/role needs later clarification

## Transfer Mechanics

Ink Pact transfer between users or relatives remains candidate material.

Current author direction is that transfer is probably too confusing for the Book 1 plot.

## Ink and Shroud Interaction

Tentative direction: Ink may need to be received before Shroud manifestation.

Still unresolved:

- whether Ink-before-Shroud is a hard rule, a rule for certain Pact effects, or a correlation
- whether Ink-adjacent abilities interrupt or destabilize Shroud maintenance
- whether Davian's weak Shroud interacts differently because of his Ink exposure, Subversion, or Archeon lineage

Route to `magic-shroud`, `magic-subversion`, Davian, Archeon, and later combat-mechanics reviews.

## Healing Limits

Accepted direction: deeper forced-restructuring healing exists for Davian, Karra, and later Vorren, and Kelyra does not have it.

Still unresolved:

- exact pain and sensory experience
- cost and depletion
- what injuries it can or cannot restore
- whether healing creates additional psychological or physical consequences
- whether it depends on Elric's direct involvement, Pact depth, stored Ink volume, or user compatibility

## Derivative and Later-Book Ink Pacts

Derivative or mass-produced Ink Pacts are later-book or later-story material, not accepted Book 1 canon.

Preserve:

- Harbinger or Archeon users creating inferior derivative Ink Pacts
- Ink spreading through bodies to create controllable weapons
- later systems derived from Elric's original Ink work

## Elric, Fate, and Ink Pool Mechanics

Preserve for later Elric, Fateweaving/Skein, and Codex reviews:

- whether Ink helps Elric see or manipulate fate threads
- how Elric's Ink remnant or simulacrum forms and dissipates
- how Elric's old Ink pool or equipment network relates to Davian's Ink
- whether Davian is connected to a legacy system rather than a unique mutation

## Zirene and Skin-Etched Zanwork

Bullet notes propose Zirene etching silver zán lattices into Davian's skin after learning from Ink.

This remains candidate material for `magic-zan-lattices`, Zirene, Davian technology, and later body-linked zánwork reviews.

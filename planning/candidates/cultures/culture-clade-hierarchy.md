# Clade Hierarchy / Caste System Candidates

## Resolved by Approved Culture Record

- The only real formal categories needed for Book 1 are High Clades and Low Clades.
- Masked Clades are criminal, underworld, or revolutionary terminology, not a legally equivalent formal category.
- Finer distinctions are handled by family names and reputation rather than additional formal tiers.
- High Clade status is defined by control of one of the six citadels / city-wedges.
- Archeon is the accepted example of violent Low-to-High mobility.
- Low Clades are subordinate formal houses that can serve as councils, proxies, contractors, administrators, and mint managers under High Clade authority.
- Low Clades have Shroud-producing hereditary magi.
- People without Clade affiliation have little access to important formal social roles and generally subordinate themselves to Clade authority or become criminals.
- Masked Clades buy into Clade language and might-makes-right culture even when they see themselves as revolutionary.
- Presented/unPresented status is a core hierarchy pressure point.
- A High Clade that fails to produce enough safely Presented magi shows weakness and may invite attack.
- More Presented magi is generally better only when they are strong and controlled enough to hold their own.

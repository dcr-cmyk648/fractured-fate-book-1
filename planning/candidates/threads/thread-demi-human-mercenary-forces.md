# Demi-Human Mercenary Forces and Death Payments Candidates

## Contract and Payment Details

- Exact death-money contract terms.
- Whether payment is made by guild, company, recruiter, Low Clade broker, direct patron, or another intermediary.
- Whether families receive payment reliably or are commonly cheated.
- Exact death-payment rates.
- Whether surviving crippled creates debt, shame, or family burden.
- Whether suicide after crippling is a known grim outcome.

## Opening Attack Force Composition

Accepted high-level structure:

- Drakhal spends a lot of money to buy extra disposable troops for the opening Rezin attack.
- The force includes both disposable shock troops and more experienced long-term mercenaries or coordinators.
- Demi-human-descended people are overrepresented, but poor humans also serve.

Deferred:

- exact number of troops
- exact ratio of shock troops to experienced mercenaries
- exact lineage mix
- whether Durnai are especially common in archery/infantry
- whether Veyri appear in scouting roles
- whether Ghorreni appear in shock roles
- whether Thaleni appear as rare heavy fighters
- whether any named mercenary becomes relevant later

## Harvick Wall Conversation

Possible scene function:

- Harvick explains why mercenaries are willing to attack despite expected losses.
- He notes that the force is expensive and unusually persistent.
- He explains that experienced mercenaries try to limit exposure to magi and preserve themselves.
- He may need to hide what he knows or suspects about the political backing.

Exact dialogue and placement remain for chapter architecture.

## High Clade Rationalization

Candidate material:

- how High Clade officers describe disposable troops
- whether Rezin/Drakhal/Low Clade brokers use euphemisms for death money
- how commanders distinguish real soldiers from shock troops
- how Maya later learns or infers why the attacking force persisted

## Prose and Chapter-Architecture Notes

- Maya should not know the force structure during the opening fight.
- Later exposition can make her earlier horror more complicated without undoing it.
- Niall's arrival should feel qualitatively different from Maya's first-combat killing: more efficient, more terrifying, and more immediately force-breaking.

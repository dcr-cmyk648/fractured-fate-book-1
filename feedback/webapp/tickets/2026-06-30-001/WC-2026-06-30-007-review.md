# Ticket Review: WC-2026-06-30-007

Status: in-review

This packet is for author discussion only. It does not make canon, update accepted story-bible mechanics, authorize manuscript edits, or resolve the ticket.

## Ticket

Evaluate whether Davian should receive Ink through direct touch from the Harbinger/Elric figure, making that figure the arbiter and purveyor of Ink.

Source comment: `comment-1782783152000-qcjtx7`

## Current Accepted Baseline

- `Ink Pact` is the accepted term for all Ink grantings.
- Ink Pact is an Elric-origin magical technology/system etched into skin and aura.
- Davian receives Harbinger/Elric Ink for the Heart theft and Registry bypass.
- Davian's accepted Pact effects are aura absorption/mimicry while killing Arrek, rapid Skein-related movement, and later forced-restructuring healing.
- Davian receives the deeper healing Pact when Elric visits him in the Rezin citadel after Davian saves Selwin and is promoted.
- The old Antithesis transformation effect is obsolete.
- Transfer between users or relatives remains candidate material and is likely too confusing for Book 1 unless re-approved.
- The current Chapter 29 explanation that Kelyra's Ink runs out because she gave some to Davian is candidate-only and likely needs replacement.

## Source Tension

Current manuscript Chapter 2 has a ritualized Ink granting:

- chamber walls bleed Ink;
- a large zä forms beneath Davian;
- tendrils bind him and sear symbols into his skin;
- the leader describes a mimic-mask effect and an obsolete Antithesis-transformation effect.

Accepted canon already requires replacing parts of that:

- older `Ink Boon` terminology;
- Jalen-seat threat framing;
- Antithesis transformation;
- possibly the exact number and nature of Davian's "two songs" / two effects.

Chapter 29 currently shows the Harbinger leader / Elric figure as the one who distributes Ink, has given Karra a deeper healing Pact, has old links to Kelyra's first Ink, and speaks as someone who can lean on the scales. The comment proposes using that same figure more clearly as the direct-touch arbiter/purveyor of Ink.

## Narrative Leverage

Accepting some version of direct-touch Ink delivery could:

- make the Harbinger/Elric figure feel more dangerous and physically invasive;
- simplify the source of Davian's deeper Ink pressure by tying it directly to Elric;
- make the leader's Chapter 29 authority over Karra and Kelyra feel earned;
- replace the confusing transfer/depletion mechanics with direct differentiated grants;
- support the idea that Elric gives different people different Pact depths/effects;
- make Davian's later pressure toward Niall feel less like generic magic and more like personal manipulation.

The risk is over-defining Ink too early or making the Chapter 2 ritual less visually distinctive if everything is just a touch.

## Cleanest Working Model

The cleanest model appears to be:

- Elric/Harbinger leader is the only true arbiter of Book 1 Ink Pacts.
- Ink can still use visible ritual infrastructure, but the decisive act is Elric/leader contact or directed attention.
- The ritual chamber, zä, pooled Ink, and tendrils are delivery/support infrastructure, not an independent machine anyone can operate.
- Davian receives his initial Pact in Chapter 2 directly from the leader/Elric through the ritual/contact process.
- Davian later receives a deeper healing/pressure adjustment when Elric visits/touches him in the Rezin citadel.
- Karra's healing Pact comes directly from the leader/Elric before Chapter 29.
- Kelyra's older Pact came directly from the same Harbinger/Elric system when she killed the prior Concord leader.
- The Chapter 29 Kelyra-defeat explanation should not rely on Kelyra transferring Ink to Davian. She loses because Karra has a deeper healing Pact and Elric/Harbinger rigging makes the Trial unwinnable.

## Alternative Models

### A. Direct touch is required

Every meaningful Ink Pact requires direct physical contact from Elric/leader or an already-authorized proxy.

Strength: clear, intimate, invasive.

Risk: may complicate scenes where the leader is not physically present or where ritual distance is visually useful.

### B. Direct touch is preferred for major Pacts, but ritual infrastructure can carry lesser or prepared Pacts

Major Pacts like Davian's, Karra's healing, and leader possession require direct touch or near-direct Elric attention. Lesser Pacts can be delivered through prepared zä/Ink infrastructure.

Strength: preserves flexibility and lets Chapter 2 keep ritual spectacle.

Risk: slightly more rules complexity.

### C. Touch is symbolic, not mechanically required

The leader can deliver Ink at a distance through ritual infrastructure, but touch is used when the story wants intimacy or emphasis.

Strength: maximum flexibility.

Risk: may not solve the "arbiter/purveyor" clarity problem strongly enough.

## Proposed Recommendation

Adopt model B.

This preserves Chapter 2's ritual visuals while making the Harbinger/Elric leader the decisive arbiter of Book 1 Ink. It also lets Chapter 29's Karra reveal work cleanly: Karra is not strong because Ink is transferable or because Kelyra depleted herself into Davian; Karra is strong because the leader/Elric deliberately gave her a deeper, unfair Pact.

STORY BIBLE: NAEROTH & LOST COSMIC LORE

SECTION: REGION — NAEROTH
---------------------------
Naeroth is Alara Rezin’s homeland, a collapsed or isolated eastern region known in myth but largely forgotten by Elarion. It was the origin of the earliest practitioners of Convergence and Fateweaving. Long ago, it suffered a magical trauma period that led to the emergence of Threadblights and the first Eclipsed. The surviving culture suppressed knowledge of these events through enforced silence, taboo, and the destruction of historical records.




By the time Alara was born:
- Naeroth’s magical power structure had decayed.
- The elder class retained forbidden knowledge but lacked power to stop Alara.
- Alara was the last known High-Clade-level Convergence user.
- Her political leverage and magical prestige allowed her to propose a diplomatic marriage to Niall, leveraging Elarion's power.
- She left legally and voluntarily, using her status as Naeroth’s “military backbone” to justify alliance-building.




Naeroth officially forbids:
- Discussion of Fateweaving
- Study or public depiction of Threads
- Resurrection practices




They rely on Convergence users for defense, even as they fear them. Most citizens know only fragments of lost rituals and old nursery rhymes. The only preserved written records are in a now-forgotten pictographic language.




---------------------------
SECTION: LANGUAGE
---------------------------
- Naeroth originally used a complex pseudo-pictographic writing system (akin to ancient Chinese or oracle bone script).
- After integration with Elarion and other regions, it adopted a phonetic/alphabetic script for communication and diplomacy.
- The old pictographs were never widely taught again and are now mostly unreadable except by a handful of surviving elders.
- These pictographs carried conceptual, non-phonetic meaning and cannot be “sounded out,” further deepening the loss of ancient magical knowledge.
- Alara, trained for diplomatic contact, spoke Elarion’s common tongue fluently and without accent — which surprised Niall early in their interactions.

SECTION: ENTITY TYPES
---------------------------




[1] ABYSSAL — (Void-Touched / Returned From Death)
--------------------------------
- Resurrected through convergence or fate-altering magic.
- Return changed, echoing, and incomplete.
- May display fragmented memory, speech-layering, unknown glyphs, or cold aura.
- Associated symptoms: soul-hollowing, emotional detachment, compulsive ideation.
- Naeroth considers resurrection a dangerous tear in the weave. Multiple resurrections exponentially increase risk.
- Only tolerated in desperate cases; heavily warded burial rites are standard.




[2] UNWOVEN — (Thread-Broken / Fate-Maddened)
--------------------------------
- Convergence users who touch Fateweaving without safeguards.
- Break psychologically and causally; often become recursive or nonlinear in thought.
- Symptoms: prophetic delusions, symbolic speech, loss of linear identity, alternate timeline echoes.
- Culturally erased from Naerothan texts. Known only through redacted accounts and suppressed songs.




[3] ECLIPSED — (Abyssal + Unwoven Fusion)
--------------------------------
- Extremely rare hybrid of the Abyssal and Unwoven states.
- Capable of altering magical resonance and attracting reality corruption (Threadblights).
- Only known case: Elric Rezin.
- Behaviors: apocalyptic utopianism, charismatic paradox, breaks in causality, unstable sigils.
- Believed to herald structural collapse of the weave itself.




---------------------------
SECTION: THREADBLIGHTS
---------------------------
- Not living beings but manifestations of weave failure.
- Appear where reality is thinned, especially after resurrection, failed fateweaving, or Eclipsed events.
- Known Types:
  - Grinters: long-limbed, stuttering figures with a grinning face-mask. Erode spell structure and cause emotional dissonance.
  - Palimpsests, Simulacrants, Threadblight-insectoids — each reflects a form of narrative or metaphysical breakdown.




- Threadblights were endemic in Naeroth post-Original Sin, and were suppressed via military-purges during the Suppression Campaigns.
- Elric’s scheme reintroduced them to Elarion via Alara’s arrival and the ripple effect of her resurrection of Niall.

Religion and Philosophy in Elarion
🔷 Dominant Philosophy: Strength as Virtue
The central philosophical belief across Elarion is a tacit form of Social Darwinism, especially among the High Clades. Strength—particularly magical strength—is seen as proof of superiority and the right to rule. This belief is dressed in the trappings of meritocracy: Clades justify their dominance not just through magical power, but through disciplined training, strategic breeding, and generational refinement.
                              * High Clade Ideology: Power justifies authority. Nobility is an expression of magical lineage and martial dominance.


                              * Critique from Within:


                                 * Maya openly challenges this worldview in her Presentation, calling out the hollowness of the Clades' supposed values.


                                 * Alara once told Niall that the society is obsessed with caste and hierarchy derived purely from the ability to kill.


                                    * Underclass Complicity: Ironically, even resistance movements and criminal organizations emulate Clade traditions—such as the Trial of Dominion—implicitly validating the Clades' structure of power.


🕊️ The Creator and the Sacred
Religion in Elarion is not institutionalized. Most people accept, almost offhandedly, the existence of a Creator—a vaguely defined force credited with the origin of all life and the structure of reality.
                                       * Popular Belief: There is a Creator. That’s just obvious. But the Creator is distant, and worship is rare.


                                       * Alara’s Tradition:


                                          * Reverence for the concept of the "Created"—things touched by beauty, love, or wonder.


                                          * The Creator is seen as benevolent, and the tragedy of the world lies in forgetting that sacredness.


⚫ Elric’s Gnostic Nihilism
Elric’s descent into madness stemmed from uncovering what he believed to be evidence that reality itself is corrupted—touched by an extraplanar, malevolent deity. His evolving philosophy parallels gnosticism, where the material world is a flawed echo of what should have been.
                                             * Core Belief: The timeline diverged from a “pure” form. Reality is now structurally broken, incapable of justice or redemption.


                                             * Philosophical Death Drive: Because no good can exist without eventual corruption, destruction is the most merciful option.


                                             * Psychological Mirror: Elric’s ideology reflects an inability to reconcile beauty and suffering—he cannot accept a flawed world as worthy of preservation.

MAGIC ORIGINS & SHROUD LORE
SECTION: TWO LINES OF POWER
All modern magic traces back to two fundamental systems:
                                                1. Primordial Magic (The Forgotten Discipline)


                                                   * The earliest known form of magic, predating all Clade structures.


                                                   * Based on give-and-take: force cannot be created or destroyed, only redirected — all magic must obey energetic balance.


                                                   * Cannot be used to generate raw force or explosive effects without a corresponding cost or sacrifice.


                                                   * Efficient use minimizes backlash; inefficient use causes internal damage or paradoxical feedback.


                                                   * Requires intense physical precision, movement-based shaping, and deliberate focus.


                                                   * Though its name was lost to time, it resurfaced in recent generations within the Virellis bloodline, without any link to the modern hereditary powers — a rare genetic convergence that brought it back with full strength.


                                                      2. Reality-Softened Magic (Born of the Original Sin)


                                                         * Long before recorded history, a powerful magus named Zerakh’tum — ancestor of the Rezin line — made contact with an extraplanar deity. In doing so, he externalized the cost of magic across reality itself, fracturing the strands of fate and reshaping the metaphysical framework of the world.


                                                         * This did not create new magic but altered reality's resistance, making it more malleable to the human mind.


                                                         * Hereditary magic emerged as a result: defined by neural/glyphic circuits passed through bloodlines.


                                                         * Each Clade’s magic manipulates one narrow domain of physics:


                                                            * Niall: charge conduction


                                                            * Maya: heat inversion


                                                            * Selwin: decay/dissolution


                                                            * Others: severance, light warping, material bonding, etc.


                                                               * These sigils manifest as glowing facial or cranial marks during magic use.


SECTION: THE SHROUD — INTERFACE OF BOTH SYSTEMS
                                                                  * Shrouds are semi-autonomous magical fields used for combat and defense.


                                                                  * Traditionally believed to be extensions of hereditary magic.


                                                                  * In reality: Shrouds are built on Subversion architecture, then amplified and flavored by sigil-based powers.


                                                                  * This blending is misunderstood — most believe their Shroud is unique to their Clade.


                                                                  * This misunderstanding reinforces Clade power structures.


SECTION: ARCHEON CLAN DECEPTION
                                                                     * The Archeon line had weak hereditary power but strong martial discipline.


                                                                     * They discovered that channeling Subversion into the body could produce powerful, personal Shrouds.


                                                                     * They repackaged this as a "super-Shroud lineage."


                                                                     * Through military success and branding, they became High Clade.


                                                                     * Their Shrouds rely heavily on internalized Subversion boosted by minor sigil effects.


SECTION: MAYA'S REALIZATION
                                                                        * During battle with Archeon magi, Maya senses two distinct threads of magic:


                                                                           * A visible sigil-layer.


                                                                           * A deeper, older current — Subversion.


                                                                              * Later, while studying Davian’s magic, she confirms:


                                                                                 * His Subversion mimics aspects of Shrouds.


                                                                                 * Subversion responds to intent, structure, and motion — not lineage.


                                                                                    * She begins to suspect the Clade narrative is built on misunderstanding or intentional obfuscation.


SECTION: COSMIC TRUTH — WHAT NO ONE KNOWS
                                                                                       * Hereditary magic only exists because reality is broken.


                                                                                       * The Original Sin weakened the weave of reality, allowing cognition to shape nature.


                                                                                       * The Clades exploit this flaw, breeding minds optimized to manipulate a single domain.


                                                                                       * Subversion predates this — it was once the only magic.


Implication:
Without the fracture, there would be no Convergence. No Shrouds. No Clades. No Threadblights.
SECTION: MAYA & DAVIAN — CONVERGENCE OF POWER
                                                                                          * Maya represents the perfected post-Sin magic: Convergence.


                                                                                          * Davian wields sharpened Subversion: older, purer, more flexible.


                                                                                          * Their alliance bridges the two magical lineages.


                                                                                          * If they uncover the truth:


                                                                                             * They could dismantle the High Clades' legitimacy.


                                                                                             * Or be destroyed by those who benefit from the lie.


But without that truth, they risk reenacting the same cycle that broke the world in the first place.

Magic system
Magic system


Two main kinds of magic: Original / pre-0R, and post-0R hereditary (reality-softened) magic


Original: based on energy exchange. This is reality-based magic. There is still a genetic component because it does require the mind, and a more powerful mind (in this case, thinking deeply and/or quickly) is needed to grasp and coordinate the flows of natural energies. If you want to produce flame from nothing, then you’d have to somehow drain energy from your body, and the exchange rate there (which is also adherent to principles of efficient energy conversion) isn’t great / you’d basically die. You could do something like, storing energy in glyphs and then releasing / directing the energy. 
                                       * Backlash: the backlash from this would be any energy required to make the spell work, and can take many different forms. In the fire example, the backlash would be both the energy required for the fire, and the energy required to force energy to a place it doesn’t want to go (heat won’t want to flow towards an area that’s already hot). 
                                       * Overall, this “magic” is more how glyphs work, and using the mind and body to duplicate those in a flexible manner. It’s much better at releasing energies into lower states than it is at concentrating or up-converting energies.
                                       * Because of this, the “Subversion” magic here is just an expression of Original magic, but it feels like something more powerful because there are so many magic users around. Like how you might be able to use jiu-jitsu more effectively on an opponent with a lot of momentum. The backlash still applies, but can be circumvented by some extent by using glyph-based tools to absorb the backlash - so a kinetic backlash could be redirected into a hard metal, etc. The energy for the redirection still comes from the body, but that’s less. 


Hereditary (reality softened): after the evil deity touched reality, reality was softened in a way that made it more responsive to the human mind. There are different genetic “circuits” within the mind that can see and interface with different energy pathways. This allows energies to be moved in ways that violate the laws of physics, such as forcing heat to flow towards a hot area, or charge to not strictly flow via potential and resistance. However, the mind has to be used to hold these channels open, and visualizing and holding all these channels is a heavy cognitive load. 
                                       * Because of the political and military importance of this system, magi are taught from a very young age how to try to access these abilities. Similar to how language is acquired, early learning and practice can affect the way the brain develops, meaning that the earlier the learning starts the more powerful a magus can become. This is also heavily influenced by genetics. Magi are also bred for cognitive speed, to the point that the highest order of magi can almost perceive everything moving in slow motion if they focus appropriately. Most of these details are not known to the general public, both because of the value of hiding the information (or at least taking advantage of your clade’s superior knowledge) and because of the dual “divine right” / “social darwinism” ideas that have become entrenched in society. 
                                       * The downside of the deep entrenchment of magical abilities with early childhood experiences is that the reflexes are so ingrained they can come out if not regulated. So a dysregulated adult (or child) could be using magic uncontrollably. This leads to a high mortality rate, which is how Presentation was developed - presenting a child who has passed the critical time of self-regulation to the point they will not spontaneously die, embarrassing your clade. 
                                       * The difficulty of wrestling a channel is in proportion to its “bandwidth”
                                       * There’s also something about studying and mastering magic itself that helps rewrite the brain to work faster, but it may also have negative consequences for attention long term, and the training to always be looking for threats also can be exhausting. The magic itself does also boost the speed of cognition. 




Shrouds: unknown to anyone (except Archeon, to some extent), Shrouds are a combination of hereditary magic with original magic. The High & Low Clades are those who have both the ability to use hereditary magic and original magic, in such a way that they can form Shrouds. This allows them to use their incredibly dangerous hereditary magic more safely, and also as a byproduct makes conventional warfare essentially obsolete. Troops are used to occupy areas, send messages, and slow down / distract magi, but killing an aware magus is almost impossible. This also means that all magi are trained to keep their Shroud up at all times.
                                       * Shrouds actually work by energy transformation, using awareness of the body and the space around it to identify threats. This acts almost like a “6th sense” in an automatic fashion, shielding the user from attacks from any direction or at any speed. 
                                       * The cognitive burden of Shrouds is not as apparent, as it acts almost subconsciously once the user has been trained. The constant use of Shrouds, the training when young to make it a reflex, and the more instinctual way they are used means magi do not even realize these are a separate type of magic. 
                                       * The other contribution to not realizing the basis of Shrouds is that the energy transformation is being offset and complimented by the magus’ own reality-bending abilities. For instance, if hit by an arrow, Maya’s shroud would convert that energy to fire, freezing the arrow by pulling both the pure kinetic energy and the heat (which is just the random kinetic energy of the atoms) out of the arrow. She can then use her own magic to redirect that fire into the ground, shoot it out her back, etc etc, allowing her Shroud to absorb a near-infinite amount of energy. The conscious perception is that all the work is being done by application of hereditary magic, rather than it being a side effect of Shroud usage.
                                       * Archeon had fairly low-grade kinetic hereditary magic, but they realized that if they combined:
                                       * Martial arts /physical training (including some of the “magic” martial arts that will come up)
                                       * Development of techniques for maximizing Shroud efficiency
                                       * Breeding for Shrouds rather than hereditary magic
                                       * Glyphic tech to allow more absorption and transfer
                                       * They could “fake” their way to being a High Clade by taking another High Clade by surprise and killing them, and then selling their power through a political facade. The Drakhals are aware of this to some extent although over-interpret the Archeon’s use of Glyphic tech as their key to success. This means the Drakhals are more willing to ally with Archeon, knowing they have the political means to undercut them at any time. 


Ink Boons: these are the most direct expression of reality distortion. The ability to distort reality is inscribed as a circuit on the skin, similar to how mental circuits allow for reality distorting magic. Like all things, using these effectively is influenced by genetics - an acceleration boon can be used better by someone who already has a quick mind.
                                       * Like the combination of Shrouds and hereditary magic, original magic can also make an Ink Boon more effective. Possessing genetic ability to think very quickly is amplified by certain boons. These characteristics (combined with “Subversion”) allow Davian to be more effective that most people with similar boons. 
                                       * The distortion type is determined by the circuit within the boon. For acceleration, it’s dragging body parts around mentally, forcing them to move at a much higher speed than possible. Healing restores something to an earlier state.
                                       * The mental feeling of using one of these is like seizing an inky rope. The tighter you hold onto the rope, the slower you descend - paradoxically, using a boon as a higher level would prolong its duration. However, this also leads to a corresponding toll on the body, with the highest level of something like acceleration possibly tearing your arm off, moving the body faster than it can stand from a neurologic or cardiac perspective, etc.

zä / zän Lore Reference
Definition:
                                       * zä = a single etched inscription in metal.

                                       * zän = plural; collections of zä form functional circuits.

                                       * zän lattice = a network of zä arranged to channel, store, or transform energy.

                                       * zänwright = specialist who etches, maintains, and recharges zän.

________________


Nature & Function:
                                          * zä are arcane inscriptions, cut with exacting geometry into specific metals.

                                          * Each zä’s function depends on:

                                             * Form → geometry determines energy behavior.

                                             * Metal → material governs conduction, resistance, and degradation rate.

                                                * Together, zän act like components in a circuit: shaping energy, stabilizing flows, and powering wards or weapons.

                                                * Energy cannot be created — only transformed, redirected, or stored. zän are most efficient at dispersing energy into lower states (e.g., draining heat, bleeding momentum). Concentrating energy is possible but unstable and dangerous.

________________


Power Source:
                                                   * Special battery-metals are etched with foundational zän that degrade as they discharge stored potential.

                                                   * Lattices typically blend battery cores with supporting zän types, distributing strain across the system.

________________


Degradation & Maintenance:
                                                      * Every zä etched into a lattice slowly wears down as both metal and form degrade under stress.

                                                      * A single failing zä can destabilize the entire lattice.

                                                      * zänwrights apprentice for years to learn proper etching. Sloppy work produces bleeding, instability, or catastrophic backlash.

________________


Types of zä (The Six Core Forms):
                                                         * Anzän (grounding) → dissipates backlash into inert matter. Metals: lead, obsidian. Always burn out first.

                                                         * Kizän (storage) → stores energy, releases in surges. Metals: silver, gold. Glow faintly when full.

                                                         * Bazän (restraint) → slows energy flow, disperses excess as heat. Metals: iron, bronze. Degrade quickly.

                                                         * Fozän (conduction) → directs energy from point to point (constraining it within the metal it’s etched into). Metals: copper, silver. Most basic form.

                                                         * Pulzän (timing) → controls rhythm of energy release, lets lattices “breathe.” Metals: tin, alloys. Fragile, temperamental.

                                                         * Veizän (filter) → screens one energy type, allows another. Metals: rare alloys, mythic metals. Elite, nearly unseen outside High Clades.

Origin:

                                                                        * Focuses on survival, precision, and subversion—turning an opponent’s momentum and arrogance against them.

Method:

                                                                           * Prioritizes rapid disarmament, joint breaks, and pressure-point strikes.

                                                                           * Optimized for fighting magi: techniques target weak points in Shrouds or break concentration through pain and shock.

Philosophy:
“The body’s not a weapon. It’s a fuse. The trick is deciding when to burn out.” — Kelyra

2. The Flow Forms (Harvick’s Discipline)
Origin:
 A derivative of ancient Naerothan meditative arts, taught to Harvick by an unnamed wanderer. The original name was unpronounceable in the common tongue; Harvick refers to it only as “the forms.”
Method:
                                                                              * Sequences of controlled movements combining breath, balance, and focus.

                                                                              * Each form is both a physical pattern and a cognitive map—when performed perfectly, it creates a resonance between the body’s motion and the practitioner’s Shroud.

                                                                              * Enables short bursts of augmented strength, speed, and perception by “borrowing ahead” from the Shroud’s potential energy.

Mechanics:
                                                                                 * The forms must be memorized exactly; any deviation collapses the internal rhythm, risking backlash or injury.

                                                                                 * The effect is multiplicative, amplifying whatever physical and magical attributes the user already possesses.

                                                                                 * Excess use causes neural and muscular exhaustion, likened to burning one’s awareness faster than the body can sustain.

Philosophy:
 Harvick teaches it as a tool for grounding and regulation, not aggression. He admits he doesn’t fully understand how it works—only that it’s older than any current martial tradition.
“I don’t name things I don’t understand. I just remember what they felt like.” — Harvick

3. Archeon Shroud Arts
Overview:
 The Archeon lineage weaponized the Flow Forms and the Nameless Art through structured training. They realized that a Shroud’s augmentation scales multiplicatively with the user’s physical ability.
Method:

                                                                                    * Breeding for superior reflexes and physical control rather than magical intellect.

                                                                                    * Use of Subversion magic to reinforce kinetic strikes—redirecting impact vectors mid-swing or during contact.

Result:
 Archeon combatants can achieve High Clade–level power output for brief windows, but the techniques make them predictable: each form telegraphs its intent, and they rely on rhythm over adaptability.
Serathis Archeon’s Innovation:

Creepy parable
In the Time Before Thorn, there lived a child without angles. He dwelt in a meadow that neither wilted nor woke, where the trees gave fruit regardless of season, and no leaf ever turned its face to die. The air was milk-sweet and warm-blooded. Joy dripped from the sky like rain that forgot to end. When he laughed, the laughter echoed back—like the world itself smiled. When he dreamed, the stars dimmed their fire, waiting for him to finish.
They called him the Softling, or the One Uncut.
But the Softling grew—as all things must, though no one ever told him this would hurt.
He saw edges. Places where the meadow thinned and the sky grew teeth. Where the grasses whispered secrets in languages with too many syllables. Where the light wore unfamiliar colors and refused to explain itself.
He asked the sparrow-things what lay beyond the world’s breath. They only shivered, repeating the names of winds that no longer blew.
So the Softling left the place of Always.
First he found the Place of Lanterns, where the light was shivering and small. The people burned wax idols to keep away That-Which-Presses-From-Below. Their hands trembled. Their laughter had too many edges. “Why do you fear?” the Softling asked.
An old woman placed a flickering flame in his palm and said, “Because we remember what it cost to forget.”
Next he found the City of Teeth, where stone wept and masks had grown into the faces beneath. They spoke only in echoes, or in tongue-knots long dead. Beneath a monument of weeping salt, a man curled like a question left too long. “Why do you mourn?” the Softling asked.
The man answered: “Because I cared—and the caring opened a mouth that never closed.”
Then he walked a plain where time gathered like rot. The sun was a bloodless smear. There he met a figure wrapped in bandages that bound neither wound nor memory—sitting beside a pool of black water that did not ripple.
“Why do you wait?” asked the Softling.
The figure replied: “Because the longer I search, the less I remain.”
The Softling wandered further. His feet bled glyphs. His back bowed beneath names he did not remember learning. He slept beneath broken towers where dreams went to die. The wind no longer touched him kindly. The stars became eyes without lids.
He forgot the shape of the meadow.
And then—at the edge of all edges—he came upon a sea.
It was not water. It was not sky. It was not night.
It was ink.
Not flowing—but breathing.
Not waves—but pulses.
A tide that remembered the first silence and waited to be remembered again.
And from beneath that un-sea came a voice—not spoken but unfolded, a sound that grew like mold in the mind. It had no mouth, but it taught understanding. It had no face, but it wore recognition.
You have wandered far, O Bloom-Treader, it said. You have touched the world’s teeth. Let me give you rest.
The Softling knelt on the edge where the earth gave up.
“Who are you?” he asked.
The voice replied: I am the Root that grows beneath endings. I am the Gentle Maw. I am the Hush that devours all meaning.
“Will you take away my ache?” asked the Softling.
Yes, said the voice. Your ache, and all that causes it to ache. Your joys. Your hungers. Your questions, which are knives. Your names, which are a cage.
“What will be left of me?” the Softling whispered.
Nothing, said the sea. And in nothing: no pain. No need. No heat of being. Only stillness.
And so he waded in.
The ink kissed his knees and stole the memory of grass. It licked his chest and took his longing. When it touched his lips, he forgot how to ask.
It reached his eyes.
And the last thing he saw was a starless bloom unfolding—a flower made of ink, with too many petals and no stem. And then - finally - his pain was gone.

High Clade Attire — Symbolism and Function
Core Philosophy
                                                                                                                                       * Bare arms signify self-reliance and unmediated lineage — magi show that their power comes from their blood, not their tools.

                                                                                                                                       * The vest (or its feminine equivalent) acts as a visual marker of mastery and rank: sleeveless, close-fitted, and heavily embroidered with familial zän or jin-lattice motifs.

                                                                                                                                       * Each vest’s embroidery is designed not just ornamentally but symbolically conductive— it channels Shroud energy efficiently through metallic thread.

________________


Women’s Attire (Rezin and High Clade Standard)
Silhouette:
                                                                                                                                          * Fitted embroidered vest (called a corvan) over a sleeveless chemise or binding layer, leaving the arms completely bare.

                                                                                                                                          * The lower garment is a divided skirt (riven skirts) — structured like wide, paneled trousers disguised as a skirt, allowing for freedom of movement.

                                                                                                                                          * The hem and panel edges are weighted with fine chain or inlaid metal, to keep them from whipping during combat and to conduct Shroud stabilization energy.

Materials & Craft:
                                                                                                                                             * Vest: Layered silk or treated arcane fiber, embroidered with metallic thread (silver for Energetics, copper for Subversion, gold for Lineage houses).

                                                                                                                                             * Underlayer: Breathable arcane mesh designed to diffuse ambient charge and prevent burns from channeling.

                                                                                                                                             * Skirts: Reinforced weave (often leather-backed), pleated for range of motion but with reinforced seams so a magus can fight without tearing fabric.

Accessories:
                                                                                                                                                * Belt Assemblage: Multiple fastening loops for scrolls, ji plates, or weapon holsters (stylized, functional).

                                                                                                                                                * High Clade arm cuffs: Decorative bracers that don’t obscure the arms but accentuate muscle tone and status — engraved with faint sigils that glow under channeling.

                                                                                                                                                * Shroud veil: Optional, translucent fabric suspended from the shoulders, representing containment of power — purely symbolic among younger magi, practical among older or ceremonial dress.

The Ancient Withdrawal (Elric’s interpretation)
Elric believes:
                                                                                                                                                                                                               1. The Creator once filled all things — the original, “correct” weave of reality.

                                                                                                                                                                                                               2. Creation could not contain perfection, so the Creator withdrew, leaving a hollowed-out, damaged world.

                                                                                                                                                                                                               3. This withdrawal created:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * the Deep (Nizan) – the chaotic substrate exposed where perfection receded.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                  * the Skein – an emergency pattern holding reality together.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                        * The cost: the Skein slowly eroding, reality fed to the Deep.

Based loosely on the Kabbalistic idea that chaos precedes order, Elric concludes:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                 * His early desires for reform

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                    * He cannot hold:

 It must be only one:

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * either fully redeemable

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       * or fully worthless

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                          * Reality is too flawed for perfection to return.

                                                                                                                                                                                                                                             * The Nizan is not a rival deity, just the unseen substrate.

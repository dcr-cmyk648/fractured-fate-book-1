## Actual Events

- Maya walks through battle-smoke, blood, wounded soldiers, and dead Rezin personnel while projecting calm Rezin poise.
- The sight of dead and wounded Rezin soldiers triggers echoes of the men she killed on the wall.
- Aldira pulls Maya into a private conference room and prevents soldiers from following.
- Maya collapses into a chair once the door closes and finally lets her Shroud drop.
- Niall enters charged with lightning and fury rather than visible relief.
- Maya reports Serathis, Drosin, close combat, and Archeon coordination while trying to sound orderly and competent.
- Niall says Maya is exhausted, should have anticipated the magus-wearing tactic, and used energy inefficiently.
- Maya defends herself by saying she handled them easily and that Archeon looks worthy of High Clade contempt.
- Niall turns the question on Aldira: how did the Registry let Maya get blindsided?
- Aldira reports that multiple unregistered auras appeared at once, moved too fast for Registry resolution in passive mode, killed messengers, and exploited a Registry designed for stealthy assassins rather than blitzing High Clade magi.
- Niall and Aldira infer that Archeon could not finance the assault alone and that Drakhal must be involved, meaning at least two High Clades cooperated in secret.
- Maya repeats Serathis's warning that other High Clades resent Rezin dominance and asks why her Presentation did not frighten them into silence.
- Niall looks guilty, then says no one should be able to strike his daughter inside his citadel and walk away.
- Aldira says Niall set the precedent.
- Niall refuses to explain but eventually permits Aldira to show Maya the memory, warning Aldira not to show anything else.
- Aldira opens a hidden compartment and retrieves a dusty codex connected to Niall / Alara.
- Aldira says Niall has not been able to open it again but could not destroy it.
- Aldira checks the key/incantation and says it has not been tampered with.
- Maya uses the codex and is pulled into Niall's embodied memory.
- In the memory, Niall walks a broken Spoke road in grief-rage after Alara's death.
- Younger Harvick walks beside him despite Niall ordering people away.
- Harvick challenges Niall's claim that there is no other way and says plenty of other ways exist.
- Niall argues that councils, reform, cooperation, and polite politics failed, and that Alara's death proved the system rotten.
- Harvick says Niall cannot make everyone honest by making himself cruel.
- Harvick warns that this will not work and that the mantle Niall is creating will not be something his family can simply set down.
- Niall reaches the Lyranth citadel, thanks Harvick for coming, and walks alone across open ground.
- Niall blasts the gate apart with lightning and destroys towers/defenders as he walks toward Selara.
- Selara appears with composure, soldiers, and magi, expecting the situation to remain legible as a political/magus confrontation.
- Niall asks for cooperation and reform; Selara frames his assault as a tantrum and threatens to put him down.
- Niall identifies defenders, magi, and Selara in a slowed battle-state, then calls down branching lightning from the sky.
- Niall kills defenders, overwhelms Selara's Shroud, and executes her with a lightning channel through her body.
- Niall notices bone-deep exhaustion and says to the ruin that this is what happens if people do not listen to him.
- Maya returns from the codex shaken, identifies Niall's anger as covering something else, and asks if he will do it again.
- Aldira says she does not think so, but does not fully explain why.
- Maya concludes the myth of Niall as perfect force-of-will magus is false.
- Maya understands that the end of expansion war was enforced by Niall's personal threat, not treaty or moral consensus.
- Aldira explains that Maya's Presentation may be exactly why Drakhal/others attacked: they hoped to see weakness, saw strength, and may have moved before other Clades decide Maya can inherit Niall's deterrent role.
- Aldira frames the High Clade order as covert pressure held under a lid by fear of Niall.
- Maya refuses to process the weight fully and pivots to practical Archeon strategy.
- Maya describes feeling Serathis's Shroud plus a second layer of magic that ate at her defenses.
- Maya notes Archeon gauntlets, unfamiliar zänwork, and a fate-thread tug.
- Erynn interrupts and orders Maya to eat and bathe.
- Maya tries to go train immediately, saying her anti-Archeon techniques must be burned in perfectly.
- Erynn challenges the word "perfect" and argues that training while exhausted would make Maya more vulnerable.
- Maya redirects to productivity and goes to the records annex with Erynn.
- Erynn challenges Maya's need to prepare for everything and says Maya may prefer being uncomfortable to admitting she cannot prepare for everything.
- Maya requests Archeon records from the old librarian.
- The available records are thin: Archeon has been High Clade for one generation; Serathis is founder; Serathis had a magus husband who frayed mentally and whom she killed.
- Maya imagines Niall going mad and destroying the citadel.
- Maya summarizes Archeon breeding programs, lack of pre-High-Clade records, Serathis's grandchildren, and few Presented magi.
- Erynn notes High Clades can keep secrets inside their walls because no one with real knowledge has to leave.
- Maya notes Archeon gauntlet zänwork she cannot recognize and that Serathis herself wore no equipment.
- Erynn names the obvious: Archeon is hiding something and just tried to kill Maya with it.
- Harvick arrives.
- Maya asks whether his friends are all right; Harvick says he loses friends every time this happens, then awkwardly clarifies the zänwrights are fine because he is friends with one.
- Maya presses Harvick on martial arts, old war experience, his sword, and his hidden edge.
- Harvick evasively admits to possible special Naeroth-adjacent physical techniques that temporarily improve strength or speed, but downplays them.
- Maya asks about zän; Harvick says zän can store and release energy but he has not heard of pure strength zän.
- Maya reasons Archeon may have made such technology cheaper or more efficient.
- Harvick says Niall fears this kind of technique spreading because it would shift the balance.
- Maya concludes Archeon has a technological breakthrough dressed up as pure magical strength, and that if the High Clades knew, they would punish Archeon for violating the hereditary-magic status story.
- Harvick names the broader critique: High Clades justify rule through Shrouds and hereditary magic, but Archeon proves the system is really about power.
- Maya asks Harvick to teach her the special hand-to-hand techniques; Harvick says he is more of a sword man but knows someone good at hand-to-hand.

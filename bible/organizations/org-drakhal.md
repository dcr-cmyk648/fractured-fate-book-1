# Drakhal Clade

## Identity

Drakhal is a High Clade associated with force-magic, birth-contract politics, political maneuvering, and anti-Rezin strategy after the Heart theft.

Exact Drakhal magic taxonomy remains for later magic review. The older `Aegistry` term remains candidate terminology, not settled accepted vocabulary.

## Leadership and Key Figures

Evana Drakhal is the current Drakhal Head Magus and Caius's mother.

Caius Drakhal is the main young Drakhal representative and marriage-candidate figure.

Vorren is Caius's older brother. He was publicly banished from Drakhal at Niall's insistence, which gave Caius the heir position. Vorren still works for Drakhal-aligned interests in secret, not because he cares about Drakhal, but because Evana and Elric can manipulate his desire for chaos and death into situations that serve their plans.

Elric directly coordinates with Drakhal, Vorren, and Archeon through the Harbingers. That the Harbinger leader is actually Elric in another body is not generally known.

`Ivana` and `Evand` are obsolete prior draft options for Drakhal leadership naming.

## Political Strategy

After the Heart theft, Drakhal moves against the Rezins because Niall may be vulnerable. Drakhal still fears Niall and avoids direct danger or fair fights unless the situation is overwhelmingly favorable.

Drakhal's current plan is to propose that Caius marry Maya and that Niall accept banishment. They know Niall is essentially certain to refuse. The proposal is designed to goad him into one of two outcomes:

- a lethal confrontation with Drakhal and many other High Clades where Drakhal expects to win
- a path to the Archeon citadel, where Vorren is waiting with a lethal trap for Niall

Either way, Drakhal positions itself for maximum upside while shielding itself from direct danger or conflicts that are not overwhelmingly in its favor.

## Archeon and Breeding-Contract Deal

Drakhal is entangled with Archeon through leverage, agents/spies, shared anti-Rezin interests, and breeding-contract negotiations.

The Drakhal/Archeon breeding-contract deal where Drakhal agrees to let Archeon breed Kelyra with Vorren is a canon verbal agreement between the parties.

Whether Vorren knows about the deal, would consent to it, or would actually follow through remains unresolved and belongs to later Vorren, Drakhal, Archeon, and plot reviews.

## Birth Contracts and Records

Drakhal participates in High Clade birth-contract politics. Rezin records include a Drakhal ledger with many uncertain birth notations, birth contracts, outside-Clade entries, and unconfirmed maternal lines.

These records connect Drakhal to broader High Clade practices where producing a Presented child can dramatically shift status, favor, and protection.

## Current Manuscript State

Current manuscript material shows Evana and Caius entering Archeon space during Kelyra's captivity, criticizing Serathis after Archeon fails to recover the Rezin Heart thief, and treating Archeon as a less-fortunate ally while still being wary of what fighting Serathis inside her own citadel would mean.

Caius displays force-magic or distortion around his hands when angered.

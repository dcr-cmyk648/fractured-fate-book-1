# Registry

## Identity

The Registry is the Rezin citadel's ancient magical security and identification system.

It is Rezin-specific. The Rezin citadel's access to the Registry is part of why that citadel position is so desirable, and the other High Clades covet it.

This file owns the Registry as an institution/system. The physical chamber belongs to `loc-registry`; detailed zä-lattice mechanics belong to `magic-zan-lattices`.

## Functions

The Registry records and verifies people through true name and aura.

It supports contractor and personnel registration, clearance, location, verification, intruder detection, and lethal response when active.

The Registry is the backbone of Rezin citadel security and a major political/administrative advantage. It allows freer contractor and personnel movement than other High Clades can safely permit.

## Active and Passive Modes

Passive mode identifies and alarms without full lethal response.

Active mode identifies and triggers lethal magical response against unregistered or intruding auras.

Active mode has limited short-period discharge capacity. It cannot be used during full-scale battles without almost immediately running out of power. The limits of active-mode attacks are visible when Caius enters the Rezin citadel while the Registry is active.

Exact alarm thresholds, lethal-response mechanics, and discharge limits remain for `magic-zan-lattices`, `loc-registry`, and later scene/revision review.

## Power and the Heart

The Registry originally was not designed with the Heart in mind. In its original form, it would have been assumed to operate only passively and at much more limited scope.

The Heart now provides most of the Registry's power. The Registry cannot function in the overpowered way it was later designed to operate without the Heart.

Exact Heart-to-Registry power path, power storage, and discharge behavior remain for `object-seraph-heart` and `magic-zan-lattices`.

## Bypass and Compromise

The Registry is extremely strong. Rezin confidence in it is not simple overconfidence.

The successful bypass comes from unknown Elric-supplied magic that exploits extremely specific circumstances. Elric was previously Head Magus of the citadel and knows the Registry very well.

Davian's accepted heist path is that he adds Arrek's name and relies on the Ink Pact to maintain the aura/name bypass. He does not also add and later remove his true name in the accepted version.

The Registry is still vulnerable to overwhelming conditions. Rapid multi-attacker scenarios can exceed passive detection's practical response capacity.

## Current and Future State

After the Heart theft, the Registry is mostly down or compromised.

Maya attempts a band-aid repair after the loss of the Heart causes the Registry to attempt to run a grossly outsized and inefficient system. Her redesign ideas include isolating passive detection, focusing it around entrances or gateways, and using zä sinks or more efficient power arrangements.

Those redesign plans are not realized during the course of Book 1.

# Clade Hierarchy / Caste System

## Identity

The Clade hierarchy is the caste structure that organizes formal power in the Six Cities.

The only real formal categories needed for Book 1 are `High Clade` and `Low Clade`.

`Masked Clade` is criminal, underworld, or revolutionary terminology rather than a legally equivalent category.

Finer social distinctions are usually handled by family names and reputation, not by additional formal tiers. For example, everyone knows the Rezins and Drakhals are more powerful than many other houses without needing a separate tier label.

## High Clades

High Clades are the ruling Clades of the Six Cities.

A High Clade is formally defined by control of one of the six citadels / city-wedges. Current canonical High Clades are Rezin, Archeon, Nyctaris, Drakhal, Cryndor, and Lyranth.

High Clade status can change through violent displacement. Archeon is the accepted example: it rose from Low Clade status by taking a citadel from a prior High Clade.

High Clade rule is rooted in citadel control, hereditary magic, Shroud-producing magi, bloodline status, wealth, political violence, and the ability to defend what the Clade claims.

## Low Clades

Low Clades are formal subordinate houses under High Clade authority.

They can serve as councils, proxies, contractors, administrators, and practical managers of important processes. Sorevin is a Rezin proxy administrator, and Haloes minting is controlled by a High Clade with Low Clade proxy management.

Low Clades also have Shroud-producing users of hereditary magic. From a practical standpoint, any house doing important work needs magi able to lead and protect it; otherwise another group can kill them and take their position.

Historically, this is how the hierarchy developed. Houses with magic and enough force could hold positions; houses without enough force were subordinated, displaced, or erased. Over time, the categories of High Clade and Low Clade became formalized around that reality.

People without Clade affiliation have little access to important formal social roles. If they have no magic or no protected place in a Clade structure, they generally either subordinate themselves to Clade authority or become criminals.

## Masked Clades

Masked Clades are underworld, criminal, or resistance organizations that appropriate Clade language.

High Clades treat the term `Masked Clade` as mockery rather than legal equivalence. They find it absurd that organizations without High Clade magic, citadel legitimacy, or formal status would call themselves Clades.

Masked Clades may see themselves as revolutionaries, but they are also implicitly buying into Clade language and the culture of might makes right. Their titles, masks, Trials of Dominion, and power struggles mirror the system they claim to oppose.

The Twilight Concord is the approved Masked Clade example.

## Presented and UnPresented Status

Presented and unPresented status is a core pressure point in the hierarchy.

If a High Clade does not produce enough magi who can be safely Presented, that is a major sign of weakness and may invite attack from another Clade, High or Low, if the vulnerable Clade controls something worth taking.

The bar for Presenting a magus is high. A Presented magus must be strong and controlled enough that announcing them publicly is worth the risk.

Presentation also creates exposure: other Clades learn that the family has someone strong enough to announce, making that person and their abilities politically relevant.

Generally, more Presented magi is better, but only if they are high-quality enough to hold their own. Weak, uncontrolled, or unsafe candidates can be a liability rather than a strength.

Detailed ritual, fashion, training, speeches, and Presentation scene mechanics belong to `culture-magus-presentation`.

## City-Wedge Hierarchy

Each High Clade governs a city-wedge / legally distinct city from its citadel.

Low Clade councils and proxy structures divide responsibility within city-wedges differently depending on the city. High Clade rulers generally prioritize citadels and elite power centers over broader city-wedge administration.

Areas far from Spoke roads and near city-wedge boundaries are poorer, less maintained, and less supported. This neglect is part of how the hierarchy appears in daily life.

# Demi-Human Peoples, Origins, and Social Roles

## Identity

This record owns the broad demi-human / non-human-descended lineage structure as it matters to Book 1 social hierarchy, prejudice, military use, and mercenary economics.

Naeroth-specific lineage facts are owned more narrowly by [Naeroth and non-human lineages](culture-naeroth-lineages.md). This record keeps the broader six-lineage structure and human-language social framing.

## Six Lineages

There are six major lineages. This aligns culturally with the base-six number system.

Human-dominant culture usually labels humans as `1`, with other numeric zän corresponding to other lineages:

| Number | Human-language name | Singular / adjective | Broad description |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | human | human | Humans can produce Shrouds and reality-distorting hereditary magic. High Clades treat this as proof of human superiority, although the hidden truth is that this power originates with the original sin and damage to reality. |
| 2 | Durnai | Durna | Dwarf-like lineage: shorter, stockier, compact, and strong. Durnai are the most prevalent demi-human lineage in the Six Cities. |
| 3 | Ghorreni | Ghorren | Orc-like lineage: generally larger, muscular, with subtle eye and forehead shape differences. They are somewhat common in the Six Cities, but face stronger stereotypes around unintelligence and violence. |
| 4 | Veyri | Veyr | Thin, large-eyed, nervous-appearing lineage, almost as if they are "prey" animals. Demi-human descendants manifest these traits to different degrees and are more often used as scouts than muscle. |
| 5 | Thaleni | Thalen | Giant/troll-like lineage. They face even more significant racism than Ghorreni, are rare in the Six Cities, and are visually obvious when present. |
| 6 | Naerothi | Naerothi | Maya's demi-human lineage. Naerothi are closest in-world to elves through perceptions of elegance, beauty, refinement, and harmony with nature. They are rare in the Six Cities and face fear-based racism around bewitching or manipulation. |

The non-human names are human-language phonetic glosses of words from each lineage's ancestral tongue. These glosses are often inaccurate approximations of the actual sounds.

All non-human lineages are pluralized in the human language by adding `i`. This does not necessarily reflect how pluralization works in the ancestral languages.

Everyone broadly speaks the human language as a trade language because of High Clade military dominance.

## Human Dominance and Magic Ideology

Humans can produce Shrouds and reality-distorting hereditary magic.

High Clades are deeply self-congratulatory about this magic and use it to justify human dominance and expansionist policies. Their military power makes the ideology hard to challenge politically.

The hidden truth is that this power is tied to the original sin and damage to reality, not to any inherent moral superiority. High Clade society generally does not know this.

Naerothi are the rare accepted demi-human exception because they can produce Shrouds and rare fateweaving / Convergence-associated abilities. Convergence is accepted as Naerothi-linked heat-transfer magic. Other demi-human lineages are not known to produce hereditary magic or Shrouds in the accepted Book 1 structure.

## Status and Prejudice

Pure versions of any non-human lineage are extremely rare in the Six Cities. Humans are the major exception because human dominance and High Clade power make human lineage socially central.

Most demi-human lineages in the Six Cities are heavily interbred with humans. The vast majority of the time, people look fairly ordinary and only subtle traces of non-human lineage can be seen.

People who look less obviously human are more likely to be pushed toward social outskirts, rural margins, or the criminal world because social pressure, discrimination, and lack of magic make mainstream advancement difficult.

Any level of non-human lineage is generally treated as low-status under High Clade culture. This prejudice is reinforced by human magical dominance and by the assumption that non-human lineages cannot produce the kinds of magic that let High Clades rule.

Different lineages face different stereotypes:

- Durnai are common and practical enough to be widespread in labor, military, and lower-status urban roles.
- Ghorreni are stereotyped as unintelligent or violent, which makes them more likely to be low-class, marginalized, or reluctant to live in the Six Cities.
- Veyri may be read as nervous, watchful, or prey-like and are often routed toward scouting or observation roles rather than brute force roles.
- Thaleni face severe visible prejudice and are rare in the Six Cities, with more living in rural areas outside direct or constant Clade influence.
- Naerothi face a different fear-based racism, with rumors that they can bewitch or manipulate others.

Anti-Naeroth rumors that Alara bewitched Niall into ending the war and marrying her are accepted canon rumors. They are partly grounded in the rarity of Naeroth interactions, not in objective truth.

How much Davian has specifically heard about those rumors remains candidate material.

## Military and Labor Roles

Durnai are the most prevalent demi-human lineage in the Six Cities. Their compact strength and campaign efficiency make them useful in armies and mercenary forces, especially roles such as archery and infantry where size, endurance, food requirements, and strength matter.

Ghorreni can plausibly appear in labor, guard, or shock-force roles, but prejudice around violence and intelligence limits their social position and may make many avoid direct Six Cities life.

Veyri are more often used as scouts than muscle because their traits suggest watchfulness, sensitivity, and alertness.

Thaleni mostly live in rural areas outside direct or constant Clade influence. A few serve the Harbingers in the catacombs beneath the city.

## Mercenary Death Money

Mercenaries are often desperate poor people who accept lethal risk in exchange for death payments to their families.

This matters morally. Mercenaries can be attackers and also exploited poor people. Threatening a wounded mercenary with permanent crippling can be worse than death in a death-money economy because it leaves the person alive but unable to support their family.

Detailed military organization, contract terms, and death-payment economics belong to `thread-demi-human-mercenary-forces`.

## Continuity Constraints

- Do not give all demi-human lineages hereditary magic or Shrouds.
- Preserve Naerothi as the accepted demi-human magic exception.
- Preserve human dominance as ideological and military, not morally or cosmologically justified.
- Preserve that human magic dominance is secretly tied to damaged reality / original sin.
- Preserve that pure non-human lineages are extremely rare in the Six Cities and that most demi-human-descended people look mostly ordinary with subtle lineage traces.
- More visibly non-human demi-humans should be more likely to appear on social outskirts, rural margins, or in criminal/underworld spaces because of discrimination and lack of magic.
- Do not treat anti-Naeroth rumors as objective truth.
- Do not make mercenaries morally interchangeable with High Clade magi; many are poor people accepting lethal risk for family survival.
- Use the human-language plural forms: Durnai, Ghorreni, Veyri, Thaleni, Naerothi.

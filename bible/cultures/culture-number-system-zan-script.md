# Number System / Zän Script

## Identity

The culture uses a base-6 number system.

The number system is culturally central and structurally linked to zän lattice design. Numeric notation, zän script, hand signs, haloes, Concord cipher, and magical circuitry share underlying visual and functional principles.

## Terminology

Accepted spelling uses accents on the `a`:

- `zän`: singular/root term for a meaningful sign, magical component, or script element
- `zä`: plural for multiple signs/components
- `zänwright`: profession term for people who design, etch, maintain, and interpret zän lattices

Unaccented forms such as `zan`, `za`, and `zanwright` may appear in notes or control-file IDs for ease of typing, but accepted in-world reference spelling uses the diaeresis accent.

The prior acute-accent `zán` / `zá` / `zánwright` convention is superseded.

## Strike-Line Logic

The strike-line is a core visual and functional feature of the number system and zän lattice design.

In writing, numbers and characters are organized around a line with crossing marks, not around Arabic numerals. In zän lattices, the strike-line conducts energy between zä.

The same structural idea makes zän script, currency marks, hand signs, Concord cipher, and magical circuitry feel like related cultural technologies rather than unrelated systems.

## Nizän and Zero

The Nizän is the zän of zero / negation / void.

In the written numeric system, Nizän means absolute zero: a value of nothing. It is not routinely used as a placeholder inside larger base-6 numbers.

Nizän has no strike-line. Its visual form is two sets of overlapping parallel lines, leaving a diamond-shaped empty central area.

When Maya sees the `eye` of the extraplanar deity at the conclusion of Book 1 while resurrecting Davian, it appears as a diamond-shaped eye. The exact cosmological relationship among Nizän, void, Sheol, the extraplanar deity, the Heart, and the Skein remains deferred.

## Multi-Digit Numbers

Longer numbers are strung along a longer strike-line with multiple crossing marks.

A value equivalent to decimal 6 would be conceptually written as a base-6 `10`, but the in-world notation would look more like a first position marked for value 1 followed by an intentionally blank second position. That second empty value is not normally written with Nizän; it should use a lengthened blank segment or a small intentional notch so the notation cannot be confused with a shorter number.

Nizän is used numerically only when the value itself is absolutely nothing / zero.

## Monad, Dyad, and Hand Signs

`Monad` is accepted for single-digit numbers.

`Monadic` is accepted for single-handed signs.

`Dyad`, `triad`, and related terms are accepted for multi-digit numbers by digit count.

`Dyadic` and related terms are accepted for two-handed or multi-handed signs by sign structure.

The exact phonemes, dialect variants, hand-shape inventory, and full chart remain candidate material unless they become outline-relevant.

## Haloes and Daily Visibility

Haloes make the number system visible in daily life.

Haloes use a center zän to indicate value within a metal tier. Those marks run from 1 to 6 within the accepted currency structure, and the mark can be activated to verify authenticity.

The 36-day month and the Moon value for a 6-gold halo reinforce the base-6 cultural structure.

A six-day week may be called a `hexday`.

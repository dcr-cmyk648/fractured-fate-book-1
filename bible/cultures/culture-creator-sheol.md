# Creator / Sheol / Gods and Religious Language

## Identity

`Creator` is the accepted in-world term for the Creator.

The culture broadly acknowledges that there is a Creator, but modern practice primarily ignores the concept except in oaths, curses, exclamations, older worship traditions, art, and inherited architecture.

The dominant modern High Clade posture is deist-like: the Creator made the world, set it in motion, and left. Serathis and Elric believe this more explicitly than most.

## Religious Language

Creator language can appear as ordinary oath, curse, exclamation, invocation, or purpose language.

This does not mean every speaker is devout. In modern usage, the Creator often functions as inherited religious language or cultural reflex rather than active worship.

Older Creator-worship existed. The Archeon throne room's stained glass windows are accepted remnants of prior High Clade Creator-worship by earlier occupants.

## Sheol

`Sheol` is accepted as in-world language for something or somewhere bad.

It can imply a hell, death, void, non-existence, planes outside the Skein, a place or condition associated with an extraplanar deity, or another hostile metaphysical outside. Its exact meaning remains vague in Book 1.

Sheol can be used in curses and religious or mythic comparison without requiring the speaker to define what Sheol literally is.

## Gods and Watchmaker Cosmology

References to characters as gods, Death, Sheol, false gods, or divine figures should be treated carefully. Many such references are rhetorical, thematic, political, or candidate cosmology rather than settled literal theology.

The Niall/Serathis conversation about Death, Sheol, gods, the absent Creator, and the broken world is an accepted future scene/confrontation direction, but the exact dialogue remains in development.

That conversation is one place where the Elric/Serathis cosmology can surface: the Creator as absent watchmaker and reality as a now-broken created order. This worldview underlies Elric's psychotic depression.

## Canonical Uncertainties

- The true nature of the fractured fate of reality remains unresolved but important.
- The literal nature of Sheol remains vague.
- Whether Sheol is void, non-existence, outside-Skein planes, an extraplanar-deity realm, or something else remains unresolved.
- The exact relationship among the Creator, first magus, original sin, the Skein, Heart, Nizán, Sheol, and reality fracture remains deferred.
- Whether there is a good deity, evil god, dark god, extraplanar deity, Nizán entity, Zerakh'tum, or other literal cosmological force remains unresolved.
- Alara's personal good-God/spiritual orientation belongs to `char-alara`, not this culture-wide record.
- Exact religious institutions, worship practices, clergy, holidays, doctrine, and modern distribution of belief remain deferred.

# Alara's Journal

## Identity

Alara's journal is Alara's specific auratic codex.

The object record uses the durable name `Alara's journal`; its technical type is `auratic codex`.

It is separate from Niall's own codex / memory book.

## Physical Object

The journal is a small aged leather-bound book. Its cover should probably be marked simply `Alara`.

When Maya first finds it, the pages appear to be written in an indecipherable cipher.

Later, a key incantation appears or becomes visible inside the front cover. The key is corrupted and tied to Elric's bridge/tampering.

The journal may have faint zán on its parchment. In the current manuscript, one metal corner is dented after Maya drops it during the bridge incident.

## Discovery

Maya finds the journal after a dream involving a figure she believes might be her mother. The figure's face becomes an inky void.

Maya follows the dream path into the old tunnel layer beneath the Rezin citadel. In an alcove, she experiences a presence or whisper and finds a hidden stone hollow containing the book.

Alara does not guide Maya. Any perception that Alara is guiding Maya is part of Elric's trap.

## Elric's Trap

Elric developed auratic codex technology from his forays into fateweaving.

He provided journals to everyone known to have one: Alara, Niall, Harvick, and himself. He presented them as benign ways to preserve valued or important memories.

Alara's journal is specifically tampered with by Elric. The corrupted key enables Elric's bridge, letting an Elric-linked presence interact through the codex beyond normal codex behavior.

The journal's parent-memory function is part of Elric's trap. It idealizes, selects, or distorts Maya's parents, preserving emotionally meaningful and partly reliable material while shaping Maya toward the belief that she can repair impossible losses through enough effort.

Elric's distortion later backfires in a limited way: because the memories preserve a real positive version of Niall through Alara's perception, Maya can eventually integrate Niall as both broken and aspirational, helping him temporarily overcome his grief.

## Book 1 Sequence

Maya hides the journal in her room, uses Alden's reference materials to access it, and enters multiple Alara memories.

The bridge incident occurs when Elric's presence enters or hijacks the memory sequence. Maya is exposed to fate/Skein perception through the bridge event.

After the bridge incident, Niall takes the codex.

## Canonical Uncertainties

- Who originally made Alara's physical codex, beyond Elric developing and distributing the technology.
- Whether Alara received it directly from Elric or through another route.
- Who made the unexplained footprints at the discovery site, if that detail remains in revision.
- Exact key-appearance staging, including whether Maya sees Elric near her room before the key appears.
- Exact truth, distortion, selection, and final prose content of each Alara/Niall memory.
- Whether the journal ever returns to Maya after Niall takes it.
- Exact relationship among Alara's journal, Niall's codex, Harvick's codex, and Elric's own codex beyond the accepted shared origin.

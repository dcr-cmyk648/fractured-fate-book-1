# Heart

## Identity

The Heart is the object contained inside the Rezin Treasury sphere: a perfectly dark sphere whose edges bend light and whose touch feels cool or cold in a way that is not ordinary temperature. Davian experiences it as alien, like a piece of something that never agreed to belong to the world.

The accepted in-world name is simply `the Heart`.

Some old manuscripts may call it the `Tzorrax Heart`, but that is old-document terminology and may not be directly referenced in Book 1. `Seraph Heart` is not the accepted in-world name.

The Heart record owns the object itself, its containment/armature relationship, its Rezin power-source function, its theft/removal consequences, and accepted object-specific links to the Skein, Elric's fate sight, and Davian's future resurrection.

## Containment

In the current manuscript era, the Heart is housed high in the Rezin Treasury inside the current Treasury sphere.

The Treasury sphere contains both the Heart and the armature for containing it. Older terms such as `Tzorrax Sphere` are outdated or overlapping terminology that refer to the Heart/containment system rather than a separate durable object record.

The Heart would generally have been in its containment armature even before Niall removed access. Niall later removed the ability for anyone to access the Heart, making it not merely protected Rezin property but something locked away from use.

The Heart is not a normal treasure or power source that can safely be retrieved, handled, or exploited at will.

## Physical and Magical Description

The Heart appears as a perfectly dark sphere. It pulls light into itself or bends the world around its edges.

It feels cool or cold in a way unrelated to ordinary temperature. Its sensory quality is alien and world-displaced.

The Heart is a near-limitless power source around which later major Rezin systems became overbuilt.

## Registry and Rezin Infrastructure

The Registry was not originally designed with the Heart in mind. In its current expanded form, the Registry depends on Heart-level input for its overpowered full functionality.

The Heart now provides most of the Registry's power and supports the major Rezin zán systems that were later built around near-limitless input. After the Heart theft, those systems cannot continue operating at the same scope.

After the theft, the Registry is down, compromised, or radically reduced. Maya's repair direction is narrower passive/gateway detection powered by zán sinks or other more efficient arrangements rather than restoration of the old Heart-fed scope.

Exact Heart-to-Registry power path, storage, discharge, and active-mode behavior remain unresolved.

## Treasury Weightlessness and Removal

Treasury chamber weightlessness is produced by interaction between the Heart and its containment armature. It is a side effect of containment, not an operational Registry toggle.

When Davian shatters the Treasury sphere and removes the Heart, the weightless field ends. The Treasury chamber has no active containment field afterward: nothing remains floating, and gravity returns.

Tanelle notices that the Treasury sphere lattice behaves like containment rather than outward weightlessness generation, but she does not know the Heart or the full Treasury secret.

Davian uses Antithesis, kinesis/Subversion, Ink pressure, and the Treasury sphere's own lattice energy to hurl the sphere at Niall. Antithesis functions as a schema sink/anchor and channel in the action. The backlash shatters Davian's arms. The sphere breaks, exposing the Heart.

## Skein, Elric, and Resurrection

The Heart itself does something to the Skein. The exact technical relationship among the Heart, Skein, reality damage, containment, and Heart exposure remains unresolved.

Elric's fate sight is canonically at least somewhat linked to exposure to the Heart. The exact source breakdown among Heart exposure, Ink, original-sin/reality-fracture knowledge, old research, or other factors remains unresolved.

The Heart will be present for Davian's accepted end-book resurrection and will interplay with Maya's fateweaving, the Skein, and related mechanics. Exact technical details, costs, and reality-damage consequences remain unresolved for later endgame and thread review.

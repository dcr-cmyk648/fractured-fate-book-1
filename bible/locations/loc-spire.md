# Spire / Central Amphitheater

## Identity

The Spire is the shared central High Clade ceremonial and meeting location in the Six Cities. It is where Maya's Presentation happens and where later High Clade meetings can be convened.

This file owns the physical shared location, its broad civic placement, and its central meeting/Presentation function. Presentation ritual and culture belong to `culture-magus-presentation`; High Clade governance belongs to `org-high-clades`; the Rezin citadel's ordinary architectural spire belongs to `loc-rezin-citadel`; later political consequences belong to `thread-high-clade-politics`.

## Name

In-world, the location is commonly called the Spire or Central Spire.

`Spire` is historical terminology rather than an accurate current physical description. The original central spire was destroyed long ago.

`Presentation Spire` can appear as casual descriptive speech because Presentations happen there, but it is not the official in-world name.

## Physical Form

The current Spire is not an intact literal tower.

Its current physical form is an open-air amphitheater or arena built into a blast hole left by a long-ago cataclysm during the High Clade wars.

The old rubble and remnants have been removed from the area. The name survives through history and usage, not because obvious broken-spire ruins still dominate the site.

## Placement

The Spire is at the center of the Six Cities.

It sits at the symbolic and civic center of the six High Clade city-wedges and functions as shared ground rather than territory owned by any single High Clade.

All six High Clade citadels have central towers. Seen from the Spire, those towers stand around the outer edge of the city at the points of a hexagon-like geometry, defining the rough shape of the Six Cities around the central shared location.

There are six High Clade citadels, corresponding to the six canonical High Clades. One-off manuscript references to seven citadels are obsolete/suspect unless later geography deliberately creates a separate non-High-Clade structure.

## Access and Shared Security

The Spire is one of the few places where the High Clades truly collaborate.

Its approach can include a plaza or market-like controlled public space, checkpoints, gates or arches marked with High Clade iconography, High Clade guards, banners, and ceremonial security.

The site is shared domain. Access and security are politically sensitive because no single family is supposed to own the space.

## Ceremonial Space

The main space is open-air and amphitheater-like, with circular geometry, a central dais or stage, and High Clade seating arranged around it.

Six High Clade sections, boxes, or tiers correspond to the six High Clades, their citadels, and their city-wedges.

The dais, floor, or arena surface may retain zán, emblems, and map-like symbolism tied to the Six Cities, Spoke roads, citadels, and High Clade order.

Maya's Presentation happens in this open-air amphitheater version. Current literal tower approach, halls, vaulted interior, chamber-atop-spire, and open-crown language should be treated as manuscript-revision material rather than accepted physical canon.

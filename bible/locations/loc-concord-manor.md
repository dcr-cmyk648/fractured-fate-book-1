# Concord Manor

## Identity

The Concord manor is the Twilight Concord's primary hideout/base at the relevant Book 1 point.

The Concord manor, Concord hideout, and abandoned house references all point to the same primary compound.

It is both an operational Concord base and a communal home/base for Concord members and any family they have. Kelyra and Jalen's family lives there, and other younger people likely live there as well even if they have not been individually named.

This file owns the physical location. Concord leadership, Trial of Dominion, masks/sign/cipher, and organization culture belong to `org-concord`; Kelyra and Jalen's roles belong to their character files.

## Setting and History

The manor is a formerly abandoned Low Clade manor in an old, decrepit part of town.

It is possible no one currently knows the exact former owner or the precise conflict that left it abandoned. The useful present fact is that it was Low Clade property, abandoned, then repurposed by the Concord.

The surrounding district is grim: ramshackle buildings, smoke, alleys, and surveillance risk. Exact district name and relation to Redbridge, Sorevin, or other city areas remain deferred.

## Compound

The manor is mismatched, weathered, patched, and dilapidated.

It includes old stone walls scarred by past battles, newer wooden additions, visible repairs, decay, sagging structures, and questionable structural integrity.

It has gates, an outer wall or enclosed grounds, a courtyard, a main house/manor structure, back routes, windows, roof access, and alleys beyond the compound.

## Courtyard and Activity

The courtyard has uneven cobblestones, training dummies, Concord members or guards, repair work, muted conversations, and everyday operational activity.

The courtyard is also where Davian returns broken with the Heart. Concord members gather there, Tessa brings supplies, and Kelyra receives the Heart from him.

The manor is compromised after Davian returns with the Heart. Kelyra does not have a fully solid plan beyond going to the Harbingers and hoping for more power, but she is worried the location is compromised.

When Kelyra is captured, Karra immediately moves everyone but Davian out. Concord members obey her because they respect the Trial rules enough and are frightened enough after Davian's explosive reappearance to accept emergency direction from her. Karra likely has to lie to Davian's siblings to get them out.

## Interior

The central hall is a former dining area repurposed into a meeting/planning space. It has a long table, mismatched chairs, cracked stone walls, patched plaster, and a sparse, aged feel.

Selina's shrine is in the family meeting space, not necessarily the general operational hall if those are later distinguished. It has fresh flowers in current manuscript material.

The manor/hideout has maze-like additions built over years from scavenged materials: rooms grafted onto rooms, corridors narrowed or widened, old stone meeting newer timber, questionable beams, and routes Davian knows well enough to use in an emergency.

## Archeon Attack and Collapse

The eventual collapse of the manor during the Archeon attack is canon.

During that attack, the manor's poor structural integrity matters: rooms, roof sections, and beams can fail under Archeon magi impacts and Davian's escape attempts.

## Canonical Uncertainties

- Exact district name and city placement.
- Exact former Low Clade owner and abandoned-conflict history.
- Exact compound map and escape-route geometry.
- Exact family/domestic room layout versus operational rooms.
- Exact number of Concord members and families living there.
- Exact sequence and extent of post-Heart evacuation and Archeon-attack damage.
- Whether the Concord has additional safehouses distinct from this primary manor.

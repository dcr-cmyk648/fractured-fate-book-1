# Harbinger Betrayal / Trial of Dominion and Kelyra Capture

## Identity and Boundary

This event owns the immediate post-Heart-delivery Harbinger betrayal, Karra's Trial/penalty challenge, and Kelyra's capture by Serathis/Archeon.

The stable entity ID remains `event-harbinger-raid`, but the accurate display name is `Harbinger betrayal / Trial of Dominion and Kelyra capture`. Earlier notes used `raid` for the broader sequence leading up to the Vorren fight; this accepted record is narrower.

The Heart theft / Treasury breach event owns the chain through Kelyra delivering the Heart to the Harbingers. This event begins at or immediately after the Harbinger leader refuses the bargain and turns the handoff into punishment.

The later Archeon attack on the Concord manor is separate fallout, not the main event owned here.

## Accepted Sequence

After Davian returns broken with the Heart, Kelyra concludes that the Concord manor is exposed and that the Harbingers must give the Concord power to defend itself. She promises the Concord that the Harbingers will reward everyone, though the promise is thin.

Karra asks to accompany Kelyra and Jalen because Davian saved her life during the Sorevin job. Kelyra reads Karra's mistrust: Karra fears Kelyra and Jalen may take the Harbinger reward and abandon the rest of the Concord.

Kelyra, Jalen, and Karra use a Harbinger dead drop and are led through confusing alleys and tunnels into a hidden Harbinger chamber. Kelyra recognizes the place from her first Harbinger meeting after Selina's death, when she received Ink and killed the former Concord leader.

The Harbinger leader takes the Heart from Kelyra and passes it to the masked figure beside him, accepted elsewhere as Vorren. He refuses to honor the bargain as Kelyra expected, indicates that Niall is still alive, and turns the scene into a punishment for failing to complete the full intended contract.

The leader reveals that he has made an Ink Pact with Karra. Karra exposes Ink and says Kelyra is doing something too dangerous.

Kelyra accepts the fight as a penalty. The challenge is both legitimate enough under Concord / Trial of Dominion custom to matter and rigged by Harbinger/Elric manipulation.

Kelyra is more skilled than Karra. She apparently defeats or kills Karra through superior Archeon-trained martial technique. Karra rises whole because she has deeper forced-restructuring Ink healing personally granted by the Harbinger leader / Elric, which Kelyra does not.

Kelyra does not realize how heavily the odds have been weighted against her. From the hidden side of the event, victory is essentially impossible once Karra's deeper Ink healing and the Harbinger/Elric rigging are in play.

Karra overwhelms Kelyra but does not kill her. She apologizes, says she did what she had to, and flees the circle.

The Harbinger leader speaks in thread/Ink/void language. Serathis then appears, and an Archeon magus seizes Kelyra. Serathis greets Kelyra as daughter, turning the Harbinger betrayal into an Archeon handoff and capture.

## Consequences

Kelyra is removed from the Concord manor and returned to Archeon control.

Karra survives and later evacuates everyone but Davian from the compromised manor. Concord members obey because the Trial rules are legitimate enough to matter and because they are frightened after Davian's explosive return.

Davian is left exposed for the later Archeon manor attack, where he confronts Archeon magi and begins more clearly recognizing that he had choices earlier, including telling Maya and asking for help.

## Character and Story Function

Kelyra:

This event tests Kelyra's belief that power is the only path to family safety. She chooses the Harbingers because she thinks she is still valuable enough for them to honor the bargain. Instead, the system she used to survive and take power is turned against her.

Karra:

Karra is an internal challenger, not a random enemy. Her betrayal remains emotionally legible because her motives mix ambition, fear that Kelyra is spiraling into dangerous and unrewarding revenge/destruction missions, and deliberate Harbinger manipulation.

Jalen:

Jalen supports Kelyra before the fight and gives practical advice. His presence keeps the event tied to the family and Concord leadership stakes rather than making it only an action sequence.

Harbingers / Elric:

The Harbingers' reputation for keeping bargains becomes part of the trap. They can betray Kelyra because she believes they do not break deals with valuable people. Elric/Harbinger manipulation also exploits Concord's own Trial structure.

Serathis / Archeon:

Serathis's appearance recontextualizes the whole betrayal as part of the Archeon capture chain, not merely underworld punishment.

Kelyra's capture feeds the accepted Kelyra Archeon recovery arc: Serathis uses release-and-catch half-truths, Lethira, and family/project language to manipulate Kelyra, while Kelyra's eventual Shroud breakthrough is tied to protecting or helping someone rather than becoming Serathis's proof.

## Continuity Constraints

- The stable ID remains `event-harbinger-raid`; the accepted display name is `Harbinger betrayal / Trial of Dominion and Kelyra capture`.
- This event owns the leader's refusal of the bargain through Karra's Trial/penalty, Karra fleeing, Serathis appearing, and Kelyra being seized.
- The later Concord manor attack is separate Archeon-led fallout.
- Karra's challenge must be both legitimate enough under Concord/Trial custom and rigged by Harbinger/Elric manipulation.
- Karra cannot be treated as Kelyra's equal in skill without Ink.
- Karra's healing is deeper forced-restructuring Ink healing personally granted by the Harbinger leader / Elric; Kelyra does not have it.
- Kelyra has no reason to anticipate Karra's healing except the fresh but unexplained evidence of Davian's healing. Because she believes her lethal blow ended the fight, Karra's recovery can plausibly make Kelyra drop her guard.
- Kelyra's defeat should preserve her competence. She wins the surface fight but loses the hidden/rigged system.
- The current manuscript's Ink-running-out and transfer-to-Davian explanation is not accepted canon.
- Davian should not fully understand the Kelyra/Elric bargain or capture mechanics when the event occurs.

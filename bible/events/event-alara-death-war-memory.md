# Alara's Apparent Death

## Identity and Boundary

This event owns the high-level historical event of Alara's apparent death and its consequences for Niall, Maya, Selwin, Elric's long plan, and later fateweaving/Skein material.

The stable entity ID remains `event-alara-death-war-memory`, but the accepted event boundary is narrower than the original label. Alara/Niall war memories remain primarily owned by [Alara's journal](../objects/object-alara-journal.md) and by later chapter mapping. This event does not duplicate the codex-memory packet except where those memories affect Alara's apparent-death consequences.

## Accepted Core

Alara's apparent-death route is accepted at the high level: postpartum/fateweaving decline after Selwin's birth leads to her apparent death.

From Book 1 character perspective, Alara is dead or unrecoverably lost. Niall believes she is unrecoverably dead/lost and does not know the hidden higher-plane truth.

At the hidden cosmology level, Alara is likely trapped in a higher plane after going there to try to kill the extraplanar deity. Exact higher-plane, Nizän, Sheol, Skein, "eye," and extraplanar-deity mechanics remain deferred.

The event may not come up explicitly in Book 1. If it does, the exact staging remains a later chapter/endgame decision.

## Niall

Alara's apparent death is Niall's defining grief wound.

Niall's response is catastrophic grief followed by suppression. He becomes more rigid, controlling, isolated, and theatrically ruthless. He tries to preserve Alara's moral/reform vision through control and dominance, but this makes him less able to live with Maya and Selwin as real people.

Niall subconsciously blames Selwin for Alara's decline and apparent death. He does not consciously know he is doing this unless later approved.

Niall's belief that Alara is gone, and his fear around fateweaving, help explain why he cannot honestly guide Maya through the dangers she is beginning to encounter.

## Maya and Selwin

Maya does not know the hidden higher-plane truth during ordinary Book 1 action.

Maya's knowledge of Alara comes primarily through absence, family silence, and Alara's journal. The journal memories are meaningful and partly reliable, but they are also selected, filtered, and trap-shaped through Elric's tampering.

Selwin is not responsible for Alara's apparent death. His role in the family aftermath is as the child whose birth and mixed inheritance are associated with Alara's decline, and as the living person onto whom Niall projects grief and discomfort.

## Elric

Elric likely did not want Alara to die. From his perspective, Alara was a potential fateweaver who might have been manipulated to damage the Skein and help summon the extraplanar deity to consume existence.

During Alara's decline and apparent death, Elric was already dead in his original body and still in the process of recrystallizing / taking over the Syndicate through the Ink Pact route.

Elric's accepted active role here is therefore not causing Alara's death. His accepted role is exploiting the aftermath through the tampered Alara codex, the parent-memory trap, and the broader fate/trauma pressure system.

## Story Function

This event:

- turns Niall's reform impulse into grief, control, and dominance
- explains Niall's fear and avoidance around fateweaving
- creates the family silence and absence through which Maya experiences Alara
- strengthens the tragic pressure around Selwin without making him culpable
- supports Elric's later manipulation of Maya through meaningful but distorted parent memories
- sets up the end-book resonance between Alara's apparent death, Maya's fateweaving, and Davian's resurrection without solving the full cosmology now

## Continuity Constraints

- Do not treat Alara as literally guiding Maya through the journal.
- Do not treat the Alara/Niall war memories as pure fabrication.
- Do not make Niall consciously blame Selwin unless later approved.
- Do not give ordinary Book 1 characters knowledge that Alara is likely trapped in a higher plane.
- Do not make Elric responsible for causing Alara's death under current accepted canon.
- Keep exact death-scene choreography candidate/deferred unless later approved.
- Keep exact higher-plane/Nizän/Sheol/Skein mechanics deferred for later endgame, fateweaving, and cosmology reviews.

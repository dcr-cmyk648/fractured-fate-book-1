# Kael

## Identity and Role

Kael is Davian's younger brother and one of Kelyra and Jalen's living children.

He receives a brief dedicated character record rather than being folded into a grouped sibling file because he has a specific Book 1 function: he is Davian's living younger-brother anchor, and Selwin reminds Davian of him.

Kael lives in the Concord manor family space with Lirien and the wider Concord household.

## Personality and Function

Kael is a child who idolizes Davian, treats missions and fighting as exciting, and uses a wooden sword as his child-warrior symbol.

His trust in Davian matters because it sharpens Davian's guilt and exposes the gap between Davian's heroic self-image and actual violence. Kael assumes Davian's missions are right because Davian is doing them; Davian knows the reality is darker.

Kael should remain mostly a family-stakes pressure point for Book 1. Any independent future-book role remains candidate material unless later outline work needs it.

## Relationships

Davian:

Kael looks up to Davian as a heroic older brother. Davian's desire to protect Kael is real, but Kael's trust also makes Davian's murder of Arrek, Heart theft, and self-justifying violence harder to simplify.

Lirien:

Kael is more eager and impulsive in the current material. Lirien is more composed and precise. Together they give the Virelli household texture.

Selwin:

Selwin reminds Davian of Kael. This is a durable Davian-arc function because it helps Davian see an innocent High Clade child rather than only a future enemy.

Kelyra and Jalen:

Kael is one of the children Kelyra and Jalen claim to be protecting, and also one of the children exposed to danger through secrecy, Concord operations, Harbinger bargains, and Archeon attention.

## Current Manuscript State

Kael greets Davian eagerly at Concord manor after the Sorevin/Syndicate setup, asks whether Davian fought anyone, and wants to hear the exciting version of the mission.

Before the Heart theft, Kael tries to follow Davian with a wooden sword and says that if Davian is doing the mission, it must be the right thing.

During Davian's later return home, Kael and Lirien are by the fireplace. Kael swings a wooden sword, greets Davian joyfully, asks about his mission, and offers Davian the wooden sword.

After Davian leaves again, Kael and Lirien are sent to bed amid tears and questions while Kelyra and Jalen sit with the consequences.

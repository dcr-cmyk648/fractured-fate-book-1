# Selina

## Identity and Role

Selina is Kelyra and Jalen's deceased child and Davian's sibling.

She receives a dedicated standard-depth record because she is likely to matter in later books and because her death shapes Kelyra, Jalen, Davian, the Concord, Antithesis, and Davian's later Nizän mask.

Selina appears mostly through memory, shrine, aftermath, and family lies in Book 1, but she is not just background grief. She is the wound Kelyra keeps converting into ideology and strategy.

## Archeon and Training

Kelyra ran from Archeon when she realized she was pregnant with Selina because Archeon children are taken from mothers and trained rather than raised.

Kelyra trained Selina with Archeon-derived martial and kinesis-adjacent techniques before later training Davian.

Kelyra's later family mythology about noble secret resistance grows out of this training, but it is not fully truthful. It is Kelyra's attempt to turn trauma, secrecy, and revenge into a destiny story for her children.

## Death

Selina went off to kill Vorren because of Kelyra's anti-Clade/destiny framing.

She was killed by overwhelming High Clade/Archeon-level power.

Kelyra's internal framing is that her mistake was letting Selina get away on her own to attack Vorren too early, instead of training her more and coming up with a better plan to kill him.

This makes Selina's death part of Kelyra's self-justification: Kelyra does not conclude that she should stop making weapons of her children. She concludes that Davian can become the weapon Selina was not if she trains and uses him better.

## Davian's False Understanding

Davian does not know the truth of Selina's death.

Kelyra tells Davian that Selina left to work for the High Clades, passed her knife to him as a keepsake because she did not want to fight and kill anymore, and was later killed by Vorren for no reason.

In reality, Selina was already dead by the time Kelyra later tells Davian about Vorren.

This lie shapes Davian's relationship to Antithesis, High Clades, Vorren, and his own violence.

## Object Links

Selina used Antithesis before Davian.

Kelyra gives Antithesis to Davian under a false family-heirloom story. The dagger is not actually a family heirloom; Kelyra stole it from Archeon and falsely named it Antithesis.

Davian's original start-book mask is not Selina's.

The later Concord mask Kelyra gives Davian, marked with the Nizän to encourage him to kill Niall, is Selina's old Concord mask with the Nizän added.

No other Selina keepsake is currently necessary.

## Family Impact

Kelyra:

Selina's death drives Kelyra toward Harbinger Ink, the Concord takeover, and the false family mythology of destiny/resistance.

Jalen:

Selina's death is a core wound for Jalen and shapes his anti-Clade grief and fear of failing another child. Jalen remains support/planning/logistics rather than the primary magical or martial trainer.

Davian:

Selina's death and Kelyra's lies about it become part of Davian's revenge logic, Arrek murder rationalization, Niall mission, fear of helplessness before overwhelming power, and relationship to Antithesis.

## Concord Shrine

Selina has a shrine in the Concord manor family meeting space with fresh flowers in current manuscript material.

## Narrative Functions

- Family wound behind Kelyra's Ink bargain and Concord leadership takeover.
- Emotional fuel for Davian's anti-Clade violence and later guilt.
- Proof that Kelyra's family mythology mixes real grief with manipulation.
- Hidden-truth dependency for future Davian/Kelyra/Vorren fallout.
- Object-history owner for Selina's use of Antithesis and the later Nizän mask.
- Possible later-book pressure point if the truth of her death resurfaces.

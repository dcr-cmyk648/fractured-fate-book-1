# Arrek Hadrin

## Identity and Role

Arrek Hadrin is the real Low Clade glyphwright/contractor whom Davian murders and replaces.

Davian's use of `Arrek` as a cover identity belongs mainly to Davian's record. This file owns Arrek as the murdered worker whose name, aura, records, and ordinary life make that cover possible and morally costly.

Arrek is not a fake identity invented by Sorevin, the Concord, or the Harbingers. His Sorevin, guild, and Registry records are valid.

## Basic Profile

Arrek is twenty-six years old, male, brown-haired, average height, and guild-licensed as a glyphwright.

He has approximately fourteen years of experience including apprenticeship.

He has no surviving primary relatives. His mother died eighteen years earlier, and his father is unlisted.

He has two surviving cousins debt-contracted to Jorra Halvek.

Harvick's first-pass impression of Arrek is that he is moody, seems like a nice guy, is good at hitting things with a sword, and does not really know what he is doing.

## Sorevin and Rezin Contract

Arrek is scheduled for the Rezin citadel ward-overhaul project through the Sorevin contractor network.

He has valid references through Rellen Jastor and Sera Vadrin, both affiliated with the Sorevin contractor network. He lacks a master recommendation because his master died six years earlier.

His vetting packet records no criminal history beyond one bar altercation seven years earlier with no charges filed.

By Sorevin vetting standards, Arrek shows no evidence of vulnerability to bribery, coercion, or subversive influence beyond ordinary Low Clade precarity.

## Debt and Ledger

Arrek was living poorly because he was sending substantial money toward buying out his cousins' debt contracts.

His cramped, neglected home contains glyphwright tools, half-finished zän sheets, worn books, and a combined ledger/journal showing regular payments to Jorra Halvek.

The Rezin contract matters to Arrek because High Clade pay could let him free his cousins.

## Murder and Aura Theft

Davian and the Concord track Arrek to a bar and follow him into an alley.

Kelyra pressures Davian to kill Arrek by framing him as a High-Clade collaborator and all Clade collaborators as evil.

Davian murders Arrek in the alley while simultaneously using Harbinger Ink to capture/steal/mimic Arrek's aura.

Davian then uses Arrek's aura and name as the Rezin-citadel cover.

Current manuscript/source material still contains stale Jalen-fieldwork pressure references around the murder sequence. Accepted canon routes the field pressure to Kelyra.

## Narrative Functions

- Makes Davian's cover identity morally costly rather than merely tactical.
- Provides the valid Sorevin/guild/Registry identity that makes the infiltration possible.
- Shows that Davian killed an ordinary Low Clade worker, not a clean enemy.
- Strengthens Davian's guilt, confession, and later movement toward responsibility.
- Grounds Maya and Aldira's investigation: Arrek's records are real, so the deception is difficult to explain through ordinary fake-identity logic.
- Gives future chapter architecture a concrete reason to preserve or revise the house/ledger sequence.

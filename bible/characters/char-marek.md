# Marek

## Identity and Role

Marek is the Group One zänwright supervisor for the Rezin citadel Treasury restoration project.

He is a brief-depth but dedicated supporting character, not part of the generic contractor group. He makes the worksite feel operationally credible: assignments exist, safety rules exist, workers have supervisors, and Davian's sudden promotion has practical consequences.

Sorevin vetting specifically selected some supervisors because they were solid, dependable, and non-brilliant rather than exceptional zänwrights. Marek fits that profile.

## Personality and Work Function

Marek is practical, clipped, dry, competent, efficient, and work-focused.

He understands official work procedures, worker assignments, safety expectations, and the supervisory side of Treasury restoration. He is not a major technical genius. His value is dependability, steadiness, and practical supervisory competence.

He can give dry, realistic commentary about Rezin power and worker vulnerability without becoming a revolutionary voice or major political actor.

## Treasury Knowledge

Marek does not know the Heart or true Treasury secret.

He is not smart enough to infer the real Treasury mechanism. Only Tanelle gets as far as vaguely suspecting that something is wrong, and even she does not know the Heart or full system.

Marek has received and accepted the official explanation for the chamber's weightlessness. That explanation came through Maya, who also does not know the true Treasury secret. The accepted truth remains that Treasury weightlessness comes from the Heart interacting with its containment armature, not from ordinary Registry control.

## Davian / Arrek

After Davian saves Selwin, Davian receives a real promotion to supervisor under the Arrek cover.

The promotion is not fake, even though Davian is using a false identity. It happens because he saved Selwin and because the Treasury project needs another supervisor to help Marek. Marek probably had been asking Maya for another supervisor, but Maya had deferred the issue because of other work.

Davian is not actually ready for the supervisory role. He relies on Marek's tutoring, plans, schematics, and practical knowledge to function.

Marek initially calls out Davian for not knowing how to do the job, but pragmatically agrees to help. Davian likely offers Marek part of the supervisor bonus in exchange, and Davian may over-read Marek's judgment of him.

## Tanelle and the Work Crew

Marek recognizes Tanelle's productivity and technical output, saying she does the work of three, while also finding her constant talking costly.

Exact relationships among Marek, Tanelle, Kolvar, Jessa, and other workers remain flexible.

## Narrative Functions

- Worksite authority: gives the Treasury zänwright project procedures, hierarchy, and safety rules.
- Practical supervisor: provides the support mechanism that lets Davian's real-but-unearned promotion function.
- Dry class realist: comments on Rezin power from a worker-level perspective.
- Worker advocate: tells Maya the workers need concrete reassurance after alarms and lockdowns.
- Davian foil/support: calls out Davian's incompetence and manipulation while still helping him.

# Caius Drakhal

## Identity and Role

Caius Drakhal is Evana Drakhal's son, Vorren's younger brother, a Presented Drakhal magus, and the primary young Drakhal representative in Book 1.

Caius became Drakhal heir after Vorren was publicly banished at Niall Rezin's insistence.

He is a recurring antagonist and Drakhal pressure point, not a Book 1 final-boss-level figure. His main Book 1 function is to make Drakhal's political threat personal through courtship pressure, public provocation, direct violence, and rivalry with Maya and Davian.

## Power and Status

Caius is a Presented Drakhal force-magus. Current manuscript material shows force-magic or distortion around his hands when angered. Exact Drakhal magic taxonomy and whether `Aegistry` survives as terminology remain unresolved.

Caius is politically protected. He can act dangerously, kill or endanger civilians, and rely on Drakhal's position to frame his actions as self-defense or legitimate pursuit of the Shade.

## Personality and Developmental Profile

Caius is polished, arrogant, dangerous, status-conscious, and insecure.

He has a narcissistic edge that leads him to act out in dangerous or deadly ways. This can include visiting Maya when she does not answer his letter quickly enough and killing civilians while trying to capture the Shade.

Caius wants control and recognition. His confidence is real but brittle, especially around Maya's power, Vorren's shadow, and any reminder that Evana controls the larger political game. He performs superiority before he has the emotional stability to carry it.

His need to dominate is partly High Clade entitlement and partly personal compensation for helplessness and humiliation connected to Vorren.

## Drakhal Political Function

Caius is the Drakhal marriage-candidate figure in the proposed Maya/Caius marriage and Niall-banishment political trap.

From Evana's, Elric's, and Serathis's perspective, the offer is bad-faith. It is meant to pressure the Rezins, provoke Niall, create legitimacy for Drakhal action, and feed the broader Archeon/Vorren trap.

Caius likely has not been fully looped into that decision-making. He thinks he has a real shot and may treat the courtship as personally meaningful, politically deserved, or both.

Caius does not appear in the final Archeon/Vorren fight. Drakhal wants plausible deniability, so Vorren is the only Drakhal magus committed to the Book 1 endgame trap.

## Relationships

### Maya Rezin

Caius pressures Maya as an intended marriage candidate, political rival, and status threat.

He tries to position himself as older, more experienced, and better prepared for High Clade politics. Maya's power and public emergence trigger jealousy, even when he recovers his composure quickly.

### Davian

Caius is a useful antagonist for Davian because he turns Davian's protective impulse, guilt, jealousy, and violence back on him.

Caius can accuse Davian of pretending to be a hero while really wanting to destroy people he envies. Davian's attack on Caius can be both protective and self-destructive, and Maya stopping Caius becomes a key moment in Davian seeing Maya's values clearly.

### Evana Drakhal

Evana restrains and manages Caius in public. Caius has real power and status, but not full control. Evana is smoother, more strategic, and more fully informed about the trap.

### Vorren

Vorren is Caius's older brother and the former heir displaced by public banishment. Caius benefited from that banishment, but Vorren remains a source of fear, humiliation, and insecurity.

Exact Caius/Vorren history and abuse pattern remain unresolved.

## Narrative Functions

- Political provocation: gives Drakhal's marriage/handover gambit a concrete face.
- Maya pressure point: tests Maya's authority, insight, and mandate to protect.
- Davian foil: exposes Davian's shame, protective violence, and fear that he is only destructive.
- Drakhal impunity face: shows how High Clade position can convert civilian harm into public self-defense.
- Vorren contrast: polished insecure heir versus fulfilled destructive appetite.
- Later antagonist seed: remains available beyond Book 1 without needing to enter the final Archeon/Vorren fight.

## Current Manuscript State

Caius appears with Evana in Archeon space during Kelyra's captivity. He smirks as Evana criticizes Serathis, reports Drakhal agents have found a suspected Rezin informant, says Drakhal already had the worker killed, and frames the act as ordinary counter-espionage doctrine.

When Serathis orders them out, Caius is offended and his force-magic stirs around his hands. Evana restrains him after seeing the surrounding Archeon magi.

Current manuscript aftermath also treats Caius's street fight as politically consequential: Rezin advisors recognize that Drakhal can claim self-defense, that the scandal will not break Drakhal, and that Maya letting the Shade go gives Drakhal material to twist.

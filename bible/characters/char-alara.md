# Alara

## Identity

Alara is Naerothi, Niall Rezin's deceased wife, and Maya and Selwin's mother.

She is "dead" from the perspective of Book 1 characters, but at the hidden cosmology level she is likely trapped in a higher plane after going there to try to kill the extraplanar deity. No current Book 1 character knows this. It should not come up directly in Book 1 except possibly through Maya's end-book contact with higher planes during Davian's resurrection.

Alara is structurally central despite being absent from present action. She strongly influences the plot through her connection to Niall, Elric, Maya, Selwin, the Naerothi lineage, fateweaving, and the codex trap.

## Abilities

Alara's accepted high-level ability package is:

- Shroud
- fateweaving
- Convergence heat transfer

Convergence's accepted owner record defines it as Naerothi-linked heat-transfer magic that manipulates heat flow rather than creating heat from nothing. Alara had Convergence at a high level, but exact Alara usage, limits, terminology, and how these abilities relate to Maya, Selwin, Naerothi inheritance, and the Skein remain for later magic and endgame reviews.

## War and Reform

Alara was a capable anti-Elarion commander or war leader before joining Niall.

She opposed Elarion's High Clade caste logic, especially its obsession with magical killing potential and the expendability of soldiers.

Niall was already a hopeful reformer before Alara. He had some similarity to very young Elric in that respect, and Elric likely allowed Niall to pass through that phase in the hope that Niall would eventually turn toward Elric's goals.

Alara gave Niall's reform ambitions a more concrete spiritual and moral undertone. Her apparent death then twisted that reform impulse into grief, rigidity, control, and post-Alara dominance.

## Spirituality and Moral Vision

Alara is spiritually and morally serious.

At the high level, she sees ordinary life, consciousness, goodness, and moral knowledge as miraculous or Created. She recognizes moral reality as something deeper than High Clade power or tactical advantage.

Exact theology, good-God language, and how much of her spiritual orientation appears directly on page remain flexible.

## Niall

Alara is Niall's lost beloved and central grief wound.

Their relationship begins in wartime opposition and becomes a shared hope of reforming the world. Alara does not create Niall's reform impulse from nothing, but she gives it moral and spiritual shape.

After Alara's apparent death, Niall cannot integrate the grief. He suppresses it, becomes more ruthless, and projects control, fear, and perfectionism onto Maya and the world around him.

## Maya

Maya knows Alara through absence, family silence, and codex memories.

Alara's memories are emotionally meaningful and partly reliable, which is exactly why they are dangerous. Elric only has to lean into Alara's positive perception of Niall to distort the memories in ways that play into Maya's belief that she should be able to achieve essentially anything by trying hard enough.

That trap also turns back against Elric by the end of Book 1. Because the memories preserve a real positive version of Niall, Maya can eventually see Niall as both a broken man and an aspirational figure. Integrating that dialectic helps Niall temporarily overcome his grief.

Alara should influence Maya without literally guiding her through the journal. Any perceived direct guidance through Alara's journal is part of Elric's trap.

## Selwin

Selwin is Alara and Niall's son.

The accepted direction is that Alara's postpartum/fateweaving decline after Selwin's birth leads toward her apparent death. Niall believes Alara is unrecoverably dead/lost and does not know the hidden higher-plane truth. Niall at least subconsciously blames Selwin for this, but he does not consciously know he is doing so unless later approved.

Exact postpartum, fateweaving, higher-plane, "eye," Nizän, Sheol, and death-scene mechanics remain deferred.

## Elric

Elric recognizes or exploits Alara's fateweaving/Skein relevance and tampers with her codex.

Elric likely did not want Alara to die, because a living fateweaver could have been manipulated to damage the Skein and summon the extraplanar deity to consume existence. During Alara's decline and apparent death, Elric was already dead in his original body and still in the process of recrystallizing / taking over the Syndicate through the Ink Pact route.

He may have approved or encouraged the Niall/Alara union because it fit his longer plans, but exact motives remain unresolved.

Elric's codex trap depends on Alara's memories being meaningful enough to Maya that they feel like revelation rather than manipulation.

## Current Manuscript State

Current manuscript material establishes Alara through:

- Maya finding Alara's journal / auratic codex
- memories of Alara confronting young Niall during war negotiations
- a battlefield memory where Alara prepares to fight Niall
- a ball/garden memory where Alara sees Niall's vulnerability and accepts his union/reform proposal
- Elric interrupting the garden memory and using the codex bridge to reach Maya
- Niall's grief, silence, and inability to talk plainly about Alara
